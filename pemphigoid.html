BULLOUS PEMPHIGOID (BP)

🎯 Must-Know Facts

  • Most common subepidermal blistering disorder
  • Age: 60-70 years (risk ↑ 300-fold after 90)
  • Most common immunobullous disease OVERALL
  • Target antigens: BP180 (BPAG2) NC16A domain + BP230 (BPAG1)
  • Antibodies: Mainly IgG4 and IgG1

🧬 Pathogenesis Cascade

Step 1: IgG binds to BP180 NC16A (non-collagenous domain)
Step 2: Autoreactive T-cells → Th2 response (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13)
Step 3: Epitope spreading → antibodies to more regions
Step 4: Complement activation → Eosinophil recruitment
Step 5: MMP-9 & neutrophil elastase → Subepidermal blister

🩺 Clinical Presentation

📍 Non-Bullous Phase (Urticarial)
  • Intense pruritus - may be isolated
  • Fixed urticarial papules/eczematous plaques
  • Often annular configuration
  • ~20% of patients remain in this phase
  • Precedes bullous phase in 50%
📍 Bullous Phase
  • TENSE bullae (1-4 cm)
  • Clear > blood-tinged fluid
  • Symmetrical distribution
  • Location: Flexures + Abdomen (most common)
  • Mucosa: 10-30% (vs PV 100%)
  • Heals with: hyper/hypopigmentation + rarely milia

Board Exam Pearls

  • Elderly patient can have negative DIF but positive ELISA
  • IgE antibodies → early urticarial phase
  • Peripheral eosinophilia in 50%
  • Associated with Multiple Sclerosis (strongest)
  • Dementia = risk factor for relapse
  • Drug-induced: more mucosal involvement, Nikolsky may be (+)
  • HLA associations: HLA-DQB1*0301 in whites - HLA-DRB1*04 and -DRB1*1101 in Asians
  • Scoring system: Bullous Pemphigoid Disease Area Index (BPDAI)

💉 Treatment Algorithm

Drug-Induced BP

Always take a detailed drug history.
Discontinuation of the offending drug may or may not lead to improvement;
standard therapies may still be required

Localized/Mild Disease

First line: Super-potent topical steroids (Clobetasol 10-30g/day)
Alternative: Doxycycline + Nicotinamide
If mucosal: Add Dapsone

Generalized Disease (>10 blisters/day)

First line: Prednisone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day
OR Clobetasol 40g/day (equally effective)
Taper: Over 6-9 months

Steroid-Sparing Agents

• Azathioprine 0.5-2.5 mg/kg (most common)
• Mycophenolate mofetil 1.5-3 g/day
• Methotrexate 7.5-15 mg/week
• Dapsone (especially if mucosal involvement)

Refractory Cases

• Rituximab (anti-CD20)
• Omalizumab (targets IgE)
• IVIG
• Dupilumab (promising)

Monitoring Remission

Clinical: BPDAI or daily blister count. Absence of new lesions/pruritus for ≥2 weeks before tapering systemic steroids.
Utility for guiding treatment remains to be established, but correlations exist:
ELISA: Serum levels of IgG autoantibodies to BP180 (NC16A ELISA) correlate with disease severity.

  • Anti-BP180 IgG levels by ELISA at days 0, 60, 150 may help predict relapse (a small <20% decrease between days 0-60 associated with relapse).
  • Predictors of future relapse:
    • High BP180-NC16A ELISA score (>27 U/ml) at cessation of therapy.
    • Positive DIF findings at cessation of therapy.

Relapse

Relapse: ~30% in first year; ~50% after therapy cessation (mostly within 3 months).
Risk factors: Extensive disease, associated dementia, high BP180 ELISA, positive DIF at cessation.

PEMPHIGOID GESTATIONIS

🤰 Essential Facts

  • Variant of BP in pregnancy
  • Target: BP180 NC16A (same as BP)
  • Timing: Any trimester (2nd/3rd most common)
  • 75% flare at delivery
  • Associated with trophoblastic tumors
  • HLA associations: DR3 (70%), DR4 (50%)

🩺 Clinical Features

Distribution Pattern
  • Starts: Umbilicus → Trunk → Generalizes
  • Annular urticarial/vesicular plaques
  • SPARES mucous membranes (MCQ!)
  • Rapid progression

Pregnancy Complications

  • Premature delivery risk ↑
  • Small for gestational age (SGA)
  • Neonatal blistering: 10% (mortality 15-30%)
  • Recurrence: 50% in subsequent pregnancies
  • Can skip pregnancies (5-8%)
  • OCP triggers recurrence (25-50%)

💊 Treatment Approach

Mild Disease

Topical high-potency steroids + Antihistamines

Severe Disease

-Prednisone 0.5mg/kg
⚠️ Increase dose at delivery

The use of systemic corticosteroids during the pregnancy appears to be relatively safe. Although early studies suggested an association between systemic corticosteroids and risk of miscarriage or congenital malformation (oral clefts), larger population-based studies have not confirmed these findings. However, limited data suggest an increased risk of preterm birth or low birth weight for women with pemphigoid gestationis or other autoimmune diseases exposed to systemic corticosteroids during the pregnancy

- Alternative therapies that have been successfully used in a few patients with severe, persistent, postpartum pemphigoid gestationis include azathioprine, high-dose intravenous immunoglobulins (2 g/kg per cycle), cyclosporine

CICATRICIAL PEMPHIGOID (MMP)

🎯 Critical Features

  • Mucosal predominant + SCARRING
  • Age: 60-80 years, Female > Male
  • HLA-DQw7 association
  • Anti-epiligrin variant = MALIGNANCY
  • Most common sites: Oral (90%) > Conjunctival (40%)

🧬 Three Main Subgroups

1. Anti-BP Antigen MMP (Largest)
Target: BP180 C-terminus
Skin + mucosal involvement
2. Anti-Epiligrin MMP (18-30%)
Target: Laminin 332 (α3 chain)
⚠️ Malignancy association
3. Ocular MMP
Target: β4 subunit of α6β4 integrin
Nearly exclusive ocular involvement

🩺 Clinical Manifestations by Site

👄 Oral (85%)
  • Desquamative gingivitis
  • Sites: Gingiva > Buccal > Palate
  • Painful chronic erosions
  • Rarely see intact blisters
  • → Periodontal damage → tooth loss
👁️ Conjunctival (75%)
  • Bilateral > Unilateral
  • Starts as non-specific conjunctivitis
  • Symblepharon (adhesions)
  • Trichiasis → Entropion → Corneal damage
  • End stage: BLINDNESS
🔸 Other Sites
  • Nasopharyngeal → hoarseness, epistaxis
  • Esophageal → dysphagia, strictures
  • Genitalia → narrowing of orifice
  • Skin (25-45%): Heals with atrophic scars

EPIDERMOLYSIS BULLOSA ACQUISITA

🎯 Key Differentiators

  • Target: Type VII Collagen (anchoring fibrils)
  • Two variants: Mechanobullous vs Inflammatory
  • Strongest association: IBD (Crohn's)
  • Blister level: Sub-lamina densa
  • Often refractory to treatment

🩺 Two Clinical Variants

⚙️ Mechanobullous (Classic)
  • Resembles hereditary dystrophic EB
  • Trauma-prone sites: hands, feet, elbows
  • Mitten-hand deformities
  • Heals with: scarring + milia + dyspigmentation
🔥 Inflammatory
  • More common than mechanobullous
  • BP-like presentation most common
  • Can also mimic: MMP, LABD, Brunsting-Perry
  • Still heals with scarring + milia

Associated Conditions

  • IBD (most common - Crohn's > UC)
  • less common with LAD97 97 cleaved portion of LAD01
  • SLE
  • Thyroiditis
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Rheumatoid arthritis

LINEAR IgA DISEASE

🎯 Two Age Groups

  • Adults: LABD (>60 years, often drug-induced)
  • Children: CBDC (avg 4 years)
  • Target: LAD-1 (120kD fragment of BP180)
  • less common with LAD 97 . which is cleaved portion of LAD-1
  • Common: other medications up to 1-780 days : 1. Penicillins 2. Cephalosporins 3. Captopril > other ACE inhibitors 4. NSAIDs
  • Characteristic: "Crown of jewels"

Drug Causes - "V-PCAN"

Vancomycin (most common, 2-21 days)
Penicillins
Cephalosporins
ACE inhibitors (Captopril)
NSAIDs

🩺 Clinical Pearls

👑 "Crown of Jewels" Pattern
  • Annular/polycyclic configuration
  • Vesicles at periphery
  • Central crusting/erosion
  • Herpetiform arrangement
📍 Distribution
  • Children: Lower trunk, thighs, groin, buttocks, face
  • Adults: More widespread
  • Isomorphic response (Koebner +)
  • May involve mucosa (resembles MMP)

Associations

📝 Autoimmune
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Lupus
  • Sjogren syndrome
  • Thyroiditis
  • Ulcerative colitis/Crohns
🏥 Malignancy
  • B-cell lymphoma
  • Chronic lymphocytic leukemia
  • Carcinoma of the bladder
  • Carcinoma of the thyroid
  • Carcinoma of the colon
  • Carcinoma of the esophagus
🧠 Neurological
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Neuromyelitis optica
🤢 Infection
  • Varicella zoster virus
  • Acute Mononeuclosis
  • Paeclomyce lung infection
  • Influenza vaccine
  • Antibiotic-treated tetanus

    PEMPHIGOID VARIANTS

    🎯 Unusual Clinical Variants

    • Important for board exams - often tested!
    • Same pathogenesis as BP but different presentations
    • Treatment generally follows BP guidelines

    📋 Complete Variant List

    1. Pemphigoid Vegetans
    • Vegetative plaques in intertriginous areas
    • Resembles pemphigus vegetans clinically
    2. Dyshidrosiform Pemphigoid
    • Palmoplantar vesicles and bullae
    • Mimics dyshidrotic eczema
    3. Pemphigoid Infantile/Childhood
    • Starts as acral bullae
    • Then generalizes with facial/genital involvement
    • Need DIF to distinguish from CBDC
    4. Pemphigoid Nodularis
    • Resembles prurigo nodularis
    • Typically lacks bullae
    5. Lichen Planus Pemphigoides
    • BP develops after history of LP
    • Bullae on unaffected skin
    6. Localized Pemphigoid
    • Sites: pretibial, peristomal, vulvar, umbilical
    • Stump pemphigoid (amputation sites)
    • Radiotherapy sites, paralyzed limbs
    7. Anti-p200 Pemphigoid
    • Target: Laminin γ1
    • Classic BP eruption + head involvement
    • NO mucosal involvement
    • Associated with psoriasis
    • Neutrophilic > eosinophilic → Dapsone helpful
    8. Anti-p105 Pemphigoid
    • Resembles SJS/TEN
    • Severe presentation

    Drug-Induced BP Features

    • Affects younger age groups
    • More mucosal involvement
    • Nikolsky sign may be positive
    • Appears on normal skin (vs urticarial in classic BP)
    • More eosinophilia
    • Histopathologic findings: Intraepidermal vesicles, necrotic keratinocytes, or thrombus formation (Classic BP is subepidermal)
    • Latency: 1-48 months
    • IIF often negative

    🎯 Quick Memory Tricks

    ROOF vs FLOOR:
    "BP Lives on the ROOF" (BP, PG, LABD = Roof)
    "EBA Falls to the FLOOR" (EBA = Floor)

    Serration Pattern:
    "N for Nice diseases" (BP, MMP = N-serrated)
    "U for Ugly prognosis" (EBA = U-serrated)

    Age Groups:
    60s = BP
    70s = MMP
    4 years = CBDC

    🚨 Board Exam High-Yield Differentials

    • Tense bullae + elderly + eosinophils = BP
    • Pregnancy + umbilicus + spares mucosa = PG
    • Mucosa + scarring + blindness = MMP
    • Anti-epiligrin MMP = Check for malignancy!
    • IBD + trauma sites + U-serrated = EBA
    • Vancomycin + crown of jewels = LABD
    • Child + annular bullae = CBDC
    🎓 FINAL EXAM TIPS

    Top 10 Board Exam Pearls

    1. BP = Most common immunobullous disease overall
    2. NC16A = Key epitope for BP180 antibodies
    3. Epitope spreading = Explains disease progression
    4. ELISA = Correlates with disease activity (not IIF!)
    5. Dapsone response differentiates Bullous SLE from EBA
    6. Laminin 332 in MMP = Screen for malignancy
    7. Vancomycin = #1 cause of drug-induced LABD
    8. Multiple sclerosis = Strongest BP association
    9. Floor pattern = Think EBA (Type VII collagen)
    10. Pregnancy pemphigoid = Always spares mucosa

    DIF Patterns

    • N-serrated: BP, MMP, LABD
    • U-serrated: EBA

    Salt-Split Results

    • ROOF: BP, PG, LABD, some MMP
    • FLOOR: EBA, anti-epiligrin MMP

    Target Antigens

    • BP180: BP, PG, MMP, LABD
    • BP230: BP
    • Type VII collagen: EBA
    • Laminin 332: Anti-epiligrin MMP

    First-Line Treatments

    • BP: Topical clobetasol
    • MMP: Dapsone + topical
    • EBA: Systemic steroids
    • LABD: Dapsone
