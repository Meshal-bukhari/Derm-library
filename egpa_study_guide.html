EGPA Clinical Features

Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis
Dermatology Board Review Guide

🧠 MASTER MEMORY HOOK - "AEV"

Asthma (almost ALL patients)
Eosinophilia (≥1500 or ≥10%)
Vasculitis (necrotizing)

3 Phases Memory: "APE" → Allergic → Proliferative (eosinophilic) → Explosive (vasculitic)

🎯 HIGH-YIELD NUMBERS

Cutaneous Involvement

  • 40-75% have skin findings
  • ~15% present with skin lesions

System Involvement

  • 75% Mononeuritis multiplex
  • 50% Cardiac involvement
  • 50% ANCA positive
🔄 THE THREE PHASES
1

Allergic/Prodromal Phase

Feature Details Board Pearls
Asthma Almost ALL patients, often severe Cornerstone of diagnosis
Allergic Rhinitis Exceedingly common Part of "allergic march"
Nasal Polyposis Very common + sinusitis Can precede vasculitis by years
Otitis Media Frequent Part of upper airway involvement
2

Eosinophilic Phase

Finding Criteria/Details Clinical Significance
Peripheral Eosinophilia ≥1500 cells/μL OR ≥10% Hallmark finding - MUST know this number!
Organ Infiltration Lungs & GI tract especially Tissue eosinophilia
Elevated IgE Frequently found Supports allergic nature
3

Vasculitic Phase

System Manifestations Board High-Yield
Constitutional Fever, malaise, weight loss, arthralgias Systemic inflammation
Neurologic Mononeuritis multiplex (75%) Pain, numbness, weakness
Cardiac Cardiomyopathy, pericarditis (50%) Major cause of death
🎯 CUTANEOUS MANIFESTATIONS (HIGH-YIELD FOR DERM BOARDS)
Lesion Type Description Frequency/Significance Board Pearl
Petechiae & Purpura Most common skin finding Phase 3 (vasculitic) Small vessel vasculitis
Churg-Strauss Nodules Papulonecrotic lesions on extensors Pathognomonic when present PNGD on histology!
Subcutaneous Nodules Painful nodules Medium vessel involvement Can ulcerate
Livedo Racemosa Broken, irregular pattern Medium vessel vasculitis vs. livedo reticularis
Retiform Purpura Stellate, angular purpura Medium vessel occlusion Poor prognostic sign
Non-vasculitic "Allergic" Rash, urticaria, pruritus More than other AAVs Nonspecific findings
🔬 LABORATORY FINDINGS

ANCA Pattern

  • Only ~50% ANCA positive
  • When positive: p-ANCA/anti-MPO
  • ANCA + → ↑ neuropathy, glomerulonephritis
  • ANCA - → ↑ cardiac involvement

Key Labs

  • Eosinophilia ≥1500 or ≥10%
  • Elevated serum IgE
  • ESR/CRP elevated
  • Check cardiac enzymes if suspected
⚡ QUICK COMPARISON: EGPA vs OTHER AAVs
Feature EGPA GPA/MPA
Asthma Almost ALL patients Rare
Eosinophilia Defining feature Absent/minimal
Renal involvement Less common Very common
Cardiac involvement Common (50%) Rare
ANCA positivity ~50% >90%

🎯 BOARD EXAM TAKEAWAYS

EGPA = "ASTHMA + EOSINOPHILS + VASCULITIS"
• If you see asthma + eosinophilia → think EGPA
• Churg-Strauss nodules = PNGD histology
• 50% cardiac involvement (major cause of death)
• Only 50% ANCA positive (p-ANCA/MPO when +)
• Less renal involvement than GPA/MPA
