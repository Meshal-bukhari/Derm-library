NODULAR
AMYLOIDOSIS
(AL - Localized)		 Amyloid Location:
• Diffuse dermis & subcutis
• Blood vessel walls

Pattern:
• Much deeper than lichen type
• Extensive involvement 		Key Associated Finding:
• Perivascular plasma cell infiltrate
• "Nearly universally present"
• Can be inconspicuous
• These cells produce the amyloid
PRIMARY SYSTEMIC
AMYLOIDOSIS
(AL - Systemic)		 Amyloid Location:
• Widespread dermis & subcutis
• Around sweat glands
• Within blood vessel walls

Special Pattern:
• "Amyloid elastosis" 		Associated Findings:
• Rarely: monoclonal plasma cell infiltrate

Key Differentiator:
• Amyloid coats elastic fibers
• Rarely seen in primary cutaneous forms
• Clue for systemic disease

HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM PEARLS
Universal Pathognomonic Finding Congo red apple-green birefringence under polarized light
⭐ DEFINITIVE confirmatory test for ALL amyloid types
Location = Single Most Important Clue • Papillary dermis ONLY → Macular/Lichen (Keratin-derived)
• Diffuse dermis/subcutis/vessels → Nodular/Systemic (Light chain-derived)
Key Differentiating Features • "Festooned" rete ridges = Lichen amyloidosis
• Plasma cell infiltrate = Nodular amyloidosis
• "Amyloid elastosis" = Systemic disease clue
IHC Pattern Recognition • Keratin-positive → Primary cutaneous (Macular/Lichen)
• Light chain-positive → Plasma cell origin (Nodular/Systemic)
Potassium Permanganate Test • AA loses Congo red (sensitive to oxidation)
• AL retains Congo red (resistant to oxidation)
Clinical Correlation Clues • Melanophages → Clinical hyperpigmentation
• Sweat gland involvement → Systemic disease
• Vessel wall deposits → Higher systemic risk
H&E Recognition Pattern Pink, homogeneous, hyaline material with cracks/fissures
⚠️ Always confirm with Congo red - H&E alone insufficient
Alternative Confirmatory Stains • Thioflavin T → Bright UV fluorescence
• Crystal violet → Metachromasia
• All secondary to Congo red
