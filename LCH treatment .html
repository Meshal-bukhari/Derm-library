LANGERHANS CELL HISTIOCYTOSIS (LCH) - Stratified Treatment Approaches
ASYMPTOMATIC
VERY MILD
DISEASE
🎯 EXPECTANT MANAGEMENT
  • Some patients may not require specific treatment
  • Candidates:
    • Single-lesion eosinophilic granuloma
    • Very limited, asymptomatic congenital self-healing LCH
  • ⚠️ Still requires monitoring
MILD
SINGLE-SYSTEM
SKIN DISEASE
🥇 SKIN-DIRECTED THERAPIES (First-Line)
Topical Agents:
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Topical antimicrobials (if secondarily infected)
  • Mechlorethamine (topical nitrogen mustard)
  • Imiquimod
Phototherapy:
  • Narrowband UVB (NBUVB)
  • PUVA (Psoralen + UVA)
EXTENSIVE
CUTANEOUS
DISEASE
🥈 SYSTEMIC IMMUNOMODULATORS
Indications: Widespread skin disease OR refractory to topical therapies
  • Thalidomide
  • Azathioprine
  • Methotrexate
SYSTEMIC
DISEASE
(Multisystem/
High-Risk)
💊 SYSTEMIC CHEMOTHERAPY
Used for: Letterer-Siwe, multifocal Hand-Schüller-Christian disease
  • Vinblastine
  • Systemic Corticosteroids
  • Cladribine
  • Interferon alfa-2a
TARGETED
THERAPY
(Modern Revolution)
🎯 MUTATION-DIRECTED THERAPY
"Revolutionized treatment strategies" - especially for aggressive/resistant disease
BRAF V600E Mutation (Most Common):
→ Selective BRAF Inhibitors (e.g., Vemurafenib)
BRAF + Other MAPK Pathway Mutations:
→ MEK Inhibitors (e.g., Cobimetinib)
Includes MAP2K1/MEK1 mutations
REFRACTORY
RELAPSED
DISEASE
🔄 SALVAGE THERAPIES
For patients not responding to initial chemotherapy
Second-Line Chemotherapy:
  • Cladribine
  • Cytarabine
Intensive Option:
  • Autologous Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT)
🔑 QUICK REFERENCE - TREATMENT HIERARCHY
Mild: Topicals → Phototherapy
Extensive: Systemic immunomodulators
Systemic: Vinblastine + Steroids
Targeted: BRAF/MEK inhibitors
Refractory: Salvage chemo → HSCT
