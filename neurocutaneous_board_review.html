🎯 HIGH-YIELD BOARD FACT: NF1 has complete penetrance with 8 different types
Genetics & Inheritance:
• 50% sporadic mutation
• 50% Autosomal dominant mutation (from the mother more severe)
• Chromosome 17 q11.2 (long arm) encodes neurofibromin (tumor suppressor protein)
• NF negative regulates RAS pathway

🔬 Pathophysiology:

Malignant transformation of neurofibroma secondary to second mutation, most commonly in p53

RAS activates: stem cell factor (SCF)/c-kit signaling, mammalian (mechanistic) target of rapamycin (mTOR), and mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) pathways

📋 DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA FOR NF1

A. For individuals WITHOUT a parent diagnosed with NF1 (need 2 or more):

  • 6 or more café-au-lait macules over 5 mm in greatest diameter in prepubertal individuals and over 15 mm in greatest diameter in postpubertal individuals
  • Freckling in the axillary or inguinal region
  • 2 or more neurofibromas of any type or 1 plexiform neurofibroma
  • Optic pathway glioma
  • 2 or more iris Lisch nodules identified by slit lamp examination or 2 or more choroidal abnormalities (CAs) – defined as bright, patchy nodules imaged by optical coherence tomography (OCT)/near-infrared reflectance (NIR) imaging
  • A distinctive osseous lesion such as sphenoid dysplasia, anterolateral bowing of the tibia, or pseudarthrosis of a long bone
  • A heterozygous pathogenic neurofibromin 1 (NF1) variant with a variant allele fraction of 50% in apparently normal tissue such as white blood cells

B. Child of affected parent (need 1 or more):

A child of a parent who meets the diagnostic criteria specified in A merits a diagnosis of NF1 if 1 or more of the criteria in A are present

🔬 ROLE OF THE TUMOR MICROENVIRONMENT IN NEUROFIBROMA FORMATION

NF1 nullizygous (NF1-/-) Schwann cells oversecrete stem cell factor (SCF), which binds the Kit receptor on NF1 heterozygous (NF1+/-) mast cells. This leads to mast cell recruitment and their activation to produce growth factors that stimulate neurofibroma development. Inhibition of KIT signaling by imatinib mesylate can block neurofibroma formation and progression.

⏰ AGE-BASED DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE

Birth/First Year:
  • CALMs: Most common cutaneous lesion and 2nd to be visible after plexiform NF (not in scalp/palm and soles) - tend to fade later in life
  • Origin: Birth or first year of life, noticeable during the first year of life, 97% visible at 6 years of age
  • Criteria: 6 lesions size 5mm before puberty, and 1.5 cm after puberty
4-6 Years:
  • Freckling: 1-2 mm pigmented macule on fold area (axilla most common, groin, neck) - Crowe's sign
  • Origin: Start at 4-6 years, 80% visible at 6 years of age
  • Criteria: Bilateral involvement
Around Puberty:
  • Neurofibroma: Plexiform neurofibroma first cutaneous lesion to develop at birth in 25% of all patients (become apparent 4-5 years)
  • Origin: Become apparent at 4-6 years, clinically visible around puberty, plexiform neurofibroma (congenital origin)
  • Criteria: 2 neurofibromas or 1 plexiform neurofibroma

💊 CUTANEOUS TREATMENT

NEUROFIBROMA:

  • Removal only for cosmetics or complications (compression effect)
  • Single → surgical, Multiple → CO₂ laser
  • Itching: antihistamine (ketotifen) for symptomatic relief

PLEXIFORM NEUROFIBROMA:

  • ✅ Imatinib, sirolimus, PEG interferon-α-2b; and photodynamic therapy are under investigations

  • ✅ Selumetinib MEK1/2 inhibitor new treatment FDA approved 25mg BID (GI side effect, and increase CPK) → risk of rhabdomyolysis

📋 MANCHESTER CLINICAL DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA FOR NF-2

A patient has the clinical diagnosis of NF-2 if one of the following criteria is present:

  • A. Bilateral vestibular schwannomas
  • B. First degree family relative with NF-2 and unilateral vestibular schwannomas or any two: meningioma, schwannoma, glioma, neurofibroma, or posterior subcapsular lenticular opacities
  • C. Unilateral vestibular schwannoma and any two: meningioma, schwannoma, glioma, neurofibroma, or posterior subcapsular lenticular opacities
  • D. Multiple meningiomas (two or more) and unilateral vestibular schwannoma and any two: schwannoma, glioma, neurofibroma, cataract
🎯 BOARD NOTE: "Any two" refers to two individual tumors or cataract.

🔑 KEY FEATURES OF NEUROFIBROMATOSIS 2

  • • Autosomal dominant transmission; affects approximately 1 in 40,000 individuals
  • • Approximately half of the cases are due to de novo mutations rather than inherited germline mutations (similar to NF1)
  • • Bilateral vestibular schwannomas, meningiomas and spinal tumors
  • • Two-thirds of patients develop cutaneous schwannomas, and approximately 50% develop café-au-lait macules (CALMs), but fewer in number compared to NF1*
  • • No Lisch nodules or mental retardation/learning disabilities
  • • NF2 gene is located on chromosome 22q12 and encodes a cytoskeleton-associated protein of ~600 amino acids named merlin/neurofibromin 2, which is structurally related to the ERM (ezrin-radixin-moesin) protein family of membrane-cytoskeleton linkers
🎯 BOARD FACT: *In two large series of patients with NF2 (n = 161), only two individuals had more than five CALMs of the required size to meet NF1 criteria and over half of those with any CALMs had only one (41 of 72).

🎯 HIGH-YIELD BOARD FACTS: TSC - Know the developmental timeline and mTOR pathway!
Genetics & Inheritance:
• 75% spontaneous mutation
• Autosomal dominant mutation
• Hamartin: chromosome 9 q34 (TSC1)
• Tuberin: chromosome 16 p13 (TSC2) (most common and severe)

🔬 mTOR Pathway:

Tuberin inhibits the cell growth and proliferation that result from mTOR signaling

TSC1 and TSC2 are located on chromosomes 9q34 and 16p13, respectively. The TSC1 product, hamartin, interacts with the TSC2 product, tuberin, and negatively regulates signaling by the small GTPases Rab and Rheb. Like Ras, the activity of Rab and Rheb is dependent upon bound GTP, and tuberin (like neurofibromin) accelerates hydrolysis of GTP to GDP in order to turn off Rab and Rheb. Rapamycin (sirolimus) and its analogs (e.g. everolimus) form a complex with the FK506-binding protein-12 (FKBP12), and together they bind to and inhibit mTOR.
