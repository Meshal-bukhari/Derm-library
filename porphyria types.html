Porphyrias – Classification, Enzymes, and Key Features

Disorder Classification Deficient Enzyme Inheritance Key Differentiating Features (Clinical & Lab)
ACUTE PORPHYRIAS
Acute Intermittent Porphyria (AIP) Acute, Non-Cutaneous Porphobilinogen (PBG) deaminase AD Most common acute porphyria.
Purely neurologic: abdominal pain, psychosis, neuropathy.
No skin findings.
Lab: Markedly elevated urinary ALA & PBG during attacks.
Variegate Porphyria (VP) Acute, Cutaneous (Neurocutaneous) Protoporphyrinogen oxidase AD "South African Porphyria."
Cutaneous fragility + neurologic attacks.
Lab: Elevated urinary aminolevulinic acid (ALA), porphobilinogen (PBG), elevated fecal protoporphyrin > coproporphyrin.
Plasma fluorescence peak: 624–626 nm.
Hereditary Coproporphyria (HCP) Acute, Cutaneous (Neurocutaneous) Coproporphyrinogen oxidase AD Like VP (cutaneous + neurologic), but rare.
Lab: Elevated ALA/PBG during attacks.
Fecal coproporphyrin > protoporphyrin.
Plasma fluorescence peak: 619 nm.
ALA-D Deficiency Porphyria Acute, Non-Cutaneous ALA dehydratase AR Extremely rare.
Pure neurologic symptoms, like AIP.
No skin findings.
Lab: Elevated ALA; PBG normal.
NON-ACUTE PORPHYRIAS (All Have Cutaneous Features)
Porphyria Cutanea Tarda (PCT) Non-Acute, Cutaneous Uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase Acquired (Type I) or AD (Type II) Most common porphyria worldwide.
Blistering, skin fragility, hypertrichosis in sun-exposed areas.
Triggers: Alcohol, estrogens, Hep C, HFE mutations.
Lab: ↑ urinary uroporphyrin I, fecal isocoproporphyrin.
Erythropoietic Protoporphyria (EPP) Non-Acute, Cutaneous Ferrochelatase "Semi-dominant" AD Onset in childhood.
Immediate photosensitivity (burning, stinging, edema), no blistering.
Leads to waxy scars, risk of liver disease (~5%).
Lab: Elevated free erythrocyte protoporphyrin.
Congenital Erythropoietic Porphyria (CEP) Non-Acute, Cutaneous Uroporphyrinogen III synthase AR "Günther Disease."
Severe photosensitivity from infancy, mutilating scars, loss of digits.
Erythrodontia (red teeth), pink diapers, hemolytic anemia.
Lab: ↑ urinary URO I & COPRO I.
Hepatoerythropoietic Porphyria (HEP) Non-Acute, Cutaneous Uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase AR Homozygous/compound heterozygous form of PCT.
Onset in infancy. Similar skin findings to CEP but less hematologic involvement.
Lab: Same as PCT but with ↑ erythrocyte zinc protoporphyrin.
X-linked Dominant Protoporphyria (XLDPP) Non-Acute, Cutaneous ALA synthase 2 (gain-of-function) XLD Similar to EPP: immediate burning/stinging with sun.
May have more liver involvement.
Lab: ↑ erythrocyte protoporphyrin (≈40% zinc protoporphyrin help to differentiate from EPP).
