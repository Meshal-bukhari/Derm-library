Nonbullous Congenital Ichthyosiform Erythroderma

Board Exam Study Guide for Dermatology Residents

📝 Learning Objectives for Board Certification

  • Understand genetics and inheritance patterns of nonbullous CIE
  • Recognize clinical presentation and evolution from collodion baby phenotype
  • Know associated genetic mutations and their mechanisms
  • Differentiate from lamellar ichthyosis and other ichthyosis disorders
  • Identify complications and associated syndromes
  • Understand management principles and systemic treatments
  • Know diagnostic testing approaches
  • Recognize potential long-term complications

1️⃣ Genetics & Inheritance

Inheritance Pattern: Primarily AUTOSOMAL RECESSIVE (rarely autosomal dominant)
Multiple genetic mutations identified with different mechanisms

Key Genetic Mutations

ALOXE3 & ALOX12B

• Involved in production of epidermal lipids

• Important for skin barrier function

NIPAL4

• Ichthyin mutations affect transmembrane protein

ABCA12

• Controls liver transport protein

• May cause severe forms of the disorder

• Some harlequin ichthyosis cases evolve into CIE

TGM1

• Some mutations of transglutaminase I cause nonbullous CIE

Sphingosine-1-phosphate lyase

• Loss-of-function mutations

• Associated with nephrosis, ichthyosis, and adrenal insufficiency

2️⃣ Clinical Presentation & Evolution

Classic presentation: COLLODION BABY at birth → evolves over days to weeks into generalized erythema + small white scales
Collodion Baby at Birth
↓
Generalized Red Skin (Cracked)
↓
Decreased Barrier Function + Water Loss
↓
Small White Scales + Persistent Erythema
↓
Lifelong Condition

Clinical Features

Skin Findings

  • Generalized erythema (prominent)
  • Small white scales
  • Thickened palms and soles
  • Decreased sweating (hypohidrosis)

Facial Features

  • Mild ectropion (common)
  • No ear/nose deformities

Scalp & Hair

  • Thick scalp scaling
  • Scarring alopecia (not uncommon)
⚠️ Complications:
  • Hyperthermia (due to decreased sweating)
  • Hypernatremia (increased transepidermal water loss)
  • Systemic infection risk
  • Chronic dermatophyte infections
  • Rare: cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma in adults

5️⃣ Management Principles

Topical Therapies

  • Petrolatum/Eucerin after bathing
  • Propylene glycol (40-60%) under occlusion
  • Alpha hydroxy acids (lactic, glycolic, pyruvate)
  • Topical retinoids (avoid in infants - percutaneous absorption)

Systemic Treatment

  • Acitretin (Soriatane)
  • Use requires experience with side effects
  • Monitor closely

Supportive Care

  • Nutrition (protein, calories, fluids)
  • Psychological support
  • Multidisciplinary team approach
  • Ophthalmology (for ectropion)
  • Social work involvement
⚠️ Board Trap: Don't use topical retinoids in infants due to percutaneous absorption risk

💡 Board Review Question

Which gene mutation in nonbullous CIE is associated with nephrosis and adrenal insufficiency?

  • A) ABCA12
  • B) ALOXE3
  • C) Sphingosine-1-phosphate lyase
  • D) TGM1

Hover for answer: C - Sphingosine-1-phosphate lyase loss-of-function mutations cause nephrosis with ichthyosis and adrenal insufficiency

🎯 Board Exam High-Yield Points

  • Collodion baby → small white scales + erythema
  • Autosomal recessive inheritance (rarely dominant)
  • Multiple gene mutations: ALOXE3/ALOX12B, NIPAL4, ABCA12, TGM1
  • Main differential: lamellar ichthyosis (larger, darker scales)
  • Complications: hyperthermia, hypernatremia, infections
  • Treatment: topical moisturizers, keratolytics, systemic acitretin
  • No topical retinoids in infants!
