🔬 Necrobiotic Xanthogranuloma (NXG)

Histopathology Quick Reference | MCQ Board Exam Review

I. ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES (Low Power)
Feature Description
Pattern Palisading granulomatous infiltrate with necrobiotic zones
Location Mid-dermis extending into panniculus (deeper than many other granulomas)
II. CELLULAR FEATURES (High Power)
A. Granulomatous Infiltrate
Histiocytes • Foam cells (xanthoma cells) - lipid-laden, bubbly cytoplasm
• Standard histiocytes
🏆 Touton Giant Cells CLASSIC BUZZWORD: Multinucleated with "wreath-like" ring of nuclei surrounding central eosinophilic cytoplasm + peripheral foamy rim
🏆 Bizarre Foreign Body Giant Cells PROMINENT FEATURE: Large, pleomorphic giant cells with haphazardly arranged nuclei
Lymphoid Component • Lymphoid follicles with germinal centers
• Lymphoplasmacytic nodules
• In CLL patients: clonal leukemic infiltrates
Plasma Cells Common component - often abundant and forming aggregates/nodules
B. Stromal Features
Necrobiosis Zones of degenerated, altered collagen - appears blurred, eosinophilic, and "smudgy"
🏆 Cholesterol Clefts CLASSIC FINDING: Empty, needle-shaped clefts within necrobiotic areas (space from dissolved cholesterol crystals)
III. IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY
Positive Stains CD68+, CD163+
Confirms non-Langerhans cell, macrophage lineage
Negative Stains Factor XIIIa-
Also negative: S100, CD1a, CD207
IV. KEY DIFFERENTIALS
vs. Necrobiosis Lipoidica (NL) NXG has:
• Prominent Touton giant cells (NL typically lacks)
• Cholesterol clefts (absent in NL)
• More abundant plasma cell aggregates
• Nodular pattern vs NL's "tiered/layered" pattern
• Extends deeper into panniculus
vs. Granuloma Annulare (GA) NXG has:
• Necrobiosis + cholesterol clefts (GA has central mucin)
• Prominent foam cells + Touton giant cells (absent in GA)
• No mucin deposition (GA is mucin+)
GA stains with colloidal iron/Alcian blue for mucin
🎯 High-Yield Board Pearls:
Touton Giant Cells + Bizarre Giant Cells + Cholesterol Clefts = NXG | CD68+/CD163+ | No mucin (vs GA) | Deeper location (vs NL)
🚨 MCQ Trap Alert: Look for palisading granuloma → Check for Touton cells + cholesterol clefts + deeper location to distinguish from NL/GA
