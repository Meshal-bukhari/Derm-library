Melanocytic Nevi & Melanoma Risk – Exam Review

High Intrinsic Risk (Direct Transformation Possible)
Nevus Type Key Points Risk Data
Large/Giant Congenital Melanocytic Nevi (CMN) Risk ↑ with size; melanoma may arise deep (dermis, fat, CNS). Associated with Neurocutaneous Melanosis (posterior axial + ≥20 satellites). 5-yr risk ~4.5% (p.1999). Large CMN: 2.5% | Giant CMN: 3.1%.
Atypical Cellular Blue Nevus Features: >1–2 cm, asymmetry, ulceration, mitoses. Can evolve into Melanoma arising from Blue Nevus (MBN) → aggressive. MBN: Large (>2 cm) scalp nodules in adults >45. High metastasis risk.
BAP1-Inactivated Nevus Multiple lesions → BAP1 tumor predisposition syndrome. Associated with melanoma, uveal melanoma, mesothelioma, RCC. Presence = high genetic risk marker. (p.1992)
Risk Markers (High-Risk Patient Phenotype)
Nevus Type Key Points Risk Data
Atypical (Dysplastic) Nevi Marker of “skin at risk,” not transformation itself. Risk ↑ with number (>50–100 ordinary nevi, or many atypical nevi). Annual transformation ≤1 in 33k–200k. Relative risk of melanoma: ~7–9x. (p.1993)
Halo Nevus Benign regression, but red flag in adults. Multiple new halo nevi in older adult → possible hidden melanoma (skin, eye, mucosa). Solitary halo lesion in older adult may represent melanoma with halo phenomenon. (p.2002)
Nevus of Ota Mostly risk for ocular melanoma (uveal). Cutaneous melanoma rare. Uveal melanoma risk ~1 in 400 in White individuals. (p.1981)
Low / Rare / Circumstantial Risk
Nevus Type Key Points Notes
Small/Medium CMN Very low risk, <1%. Many studies = 0 cases. (p.1999)
Nevus Spilus Melanoma can rarely arise, risk related to size. (p.2001)
Pigmented Spindle Cell Nevus (Reed) Rare malignant transformation reported. (p.1990)
Mucosal Melanotic Macules / Melanosis Precursor lesion in 30–60% of oral melanomas. (p.1977)
No Increased Risk
Recurrent Melanocytic Nevus No evidence of melanoma risk. (p.2004)
