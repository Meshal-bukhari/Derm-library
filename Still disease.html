Classic Still's Tetrad
High, Spiking Fevers
  • Pattern: Quotidian (daily), >39°C (102.2°F)
  • Timing: Late afternoon/evening, normalizes later
  • Requirement: ≥2 weeks, daily for ≥3 days (sJIA)
  • Prodrome (AOSD): Sore throat often precedes
Still’s Rash
  • Appearance: Evanescent, salmon-pink, non-pruritic macules/papules (figurate)
  • Timing: Appears with fever spikes, disappears as fever subsides
  • Location: Trunk, axillae, waist; also extremities, palms, soles
  • Key Sign: Koebner phenomenon common
Arthritis
  • Pattern: Symmetric, polyarticular
  • Joints (sJIA): Wrists, knees, ankles, hips
  • Joints (AOSD): Knees, wrists, ankles
  • AOSD Distinctive: Carpal ankylosis (painless but restricted ROM)
  • Severity: Can be destructive in a subset
Systemic Involvement
  • Generalized lymphadenopathy
  • Hepatosplenomegaly
  • Serositis (pericarditis, pleuritis, peritonitis)
Other Cutaneous & Life-Threatening Features
Less Common Skin Findings
  • Persistent violaceous/reddish-brown scaly plaques (worse prognosis)
  • Rare dermatomyositis mimics (eyelid edema, flagellate erythema)
Macrophage Activation Syndrome (MAS)
  • Seen in ~10% of sJIA cases
  • Features: Persistent fever, cytopenias, ↑LFTs, coagulopathy, ↑↑ferritin
Interstitial Lung Disease (PAP/ELP)
  • Rare but severe
  • May begin with digital clubbing & erythema
Laboratory Findings
Inflammatory Markers
  • ↑ ESR, CRP
  • ↑ WBC (neutrophilia)
  • ↑ Platelets (thrombocytosis)
Ferritin
  • Hallmark: Ferritin often >4000 mg/ml
  • Low glycosylated ferritin fraction: <20% in adults
Autoantibodies
  • ANA & RF: Negative in >95%
Other Labs
  • Anemia
  • Abnormal liver function tests
