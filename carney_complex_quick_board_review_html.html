Carney Complex — Quick Board Review

One-page, high-yield summary for rapid pre-exam review. Memorize the mnemonic "CARNEY'S SPOTS & BUMPS".

Feature Key Points (Board high-yield) Clinical clues / Screening / Exam tips
Cardiac Myxomas • Major cause of morbidity/mortality — can arise in any chamber (common: atria).
• Embolic strokes, obstruction (mitral inflow), sudden death possible.
• Histology: myxoid stroma, stellate cells (benign but dangerous by location). 		• Annual/biannual echocardiographic screening from adolescence.
• Red flag: positional syncope, new murmur, systemic emboli in young patient.
Exam tip: cardiac myxoma = most important life-threatening feature.
Adrenal — PPNAD • Primary Pigmented Nodular Adrenocortical Disease → ACTH‑independent Cushing syndrome.
• Findings: weight gain, facial rounding, hypertension, glucose intolerance.
• Often microscopic pigmented nodules; may be missed on routine imaging. 		• Screen: serial cortisol testing; low ACTH, abnormal dexamethasone suppression.
• Exam tip: young Cushing with lentigines → think Carney complex.
Reproductive tumors • Males: Large-cell calcifying Sertoli cell tumor (LCCSCT) — may be bilateral/multifocal.
• Females: Ovarian cysts; rare psammomatous melanotic schwannomas in adnexa. 		• Testicular mass or endocrine changes in young male → ultrasound and consider LCCSCT.
• Exam tip: LCCSCT + lentigines = classic pairing.
Nevi / Blue nevi • Multiple blue nevi (esp. epithelioid blue nevi) — characteristic cutaneous finding.
• May co-exist with lentigines; helps clue diagnosis when present with other features. 		• Dermatologic exam: multiple blue/epithelioid nevi; biopsy if atypical.
• Exam tip: blue nevi + lentigines should raise suspicion for Carney complex.
Lentigines (Spots) • Hallmark finding and often first clue: small brown-black macules (lentigines).
• Typical distribution: centrofacial (cheeks, nose, vermilion border), conjunctiva, mucosa (genital), flexures (axilla, groin). 		• Look carefully at lips, eyelids, conjunctiva, and genital mucosa during exam.
• Often present in childhood/early adolescence — early clue before tumors manifest.
Cutaneous & other Myxomas • Cutaneous myxomas: small, skin-colored dome-shaped papules/nodules (eyelids, canthi, external auditory canal, nipples).
• Breast myxomas/ductal adenomas possible. 		• Biopsy may show myxoid stroma.
• Exam tip: eyelid papules + lentigines = high-yield combination.
Psammomatous Melanotic Schwannoma • Distinctive nerve-sheath tumor: psammoma bodies + melanotic cells.
• Can arise in skin, GI tract, paraspinal region; may be locally aggressive and metastasize. 		• Any paraspinal mass, neuropathic symptoms, or pigmented schwannoma histology → consider Carney complex.
• Exam tip: demonstration of psammoma bodies on pathology is a clue.
Genetics & inheritance • Usually autosomal dominant with variable expressivity.
• Most commonly due to inactivating mutations in PRKAR1A (protein kinase A regulatory subunit).
• Genetic testing available; family screening recommended if mutation found. 		• Exam tip: family history of cardiac myxoma, lentigines, or endocrine tumors suggests screening.
Management principles • Multidisciplinary: cardiology (screening/operative removal of myxomas), endocrinology (PPNAD/Cushing workup), oncology/dermatology (tumor surveillance), genetics.
• Early surgical removal of cardiac myxomas is lifesaving.
• Surveillance: echocardiography, endocrine testing, dermatologic and testicular/ovarian surveillance. 		• Exam tip: immediate action for cardiac myxoma; PPNAD needs biochemical endocrine workup even if imaging negative.
Board-style quick bullets • Classic triad: lentigines + myxomas + endocrine tumors.
• Most important mortality: cardiac myxoma → screen with echo.
• PRKAR1A mutation → AD inheritance; variable expressivity.
• PPNAD → ACTH-independent Cushing in young patients. 		• Rapid recall: "CARNEY'S SPOTS & BUMPS" mnemonic.
• When seeing young Cushing or early stroke from embolus, inspect skin for lentigines.
High-yield screening
Echo (yearly), cortisol/DEXA testing for Cushing features, testicular US if male, dermatology exam annually.
Red flags
New embolic phenomenon, positional syncope, rapid-onset Cushing features, enlarging paraspinal mass, atypical skin tumors.
Exam tricks / confusion
Do not confuse lentigines with cafe-au-lait; blue nevi are distinctive. Carney complex is AD (not X-linked). Myxomas are benign histologically but life-threatening by location.
