MORPHEA TREATMENT ALGORITHM

Board Exam Quick Reference

FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES (HIGH-YIELD)
Active Disease Marker Erythematous or "lilac" inflammatory border of expanding plaque
• Inactive = sclerotic, atrophic, "burnt-out" plaques
• Therapy most effective for ACTIVE lesions
Treatment Goals 1. Halt disease progression
2. Prevent irreversible damage (contractures, atrophy)
3. Improve inflammation and sclerosis
Assessment Tool LoSCAT (Localized Scleroderma Cutaneous Assessment Tool)
Multidisciplinary Referrals • PT/OT for functional impairment risk
• Ophthalmology + Neurology for head/neck involvement
PLAQUE-TYPE (CIRCUMSCRIBED) MORPHEA - Limited BSA
Treatment Line Therapy Specifics
FIRST-LINE
(Topical)		 Topical Corticosteroids
Intralesional Steroids
Tacrolimus 0.1%
Calcipotriene 		• High-potency (Class I), daily-BID
• Triamcinolone 5-10 mg/mL q4-6wks into active rim
• Ointment BID
• Often under occlusion + topical steroid
SECOND-LINE Phototherapy
Topical Crisaborole 2% 		• Lesion-limited: UVA1 > BB-UVA > PUVA
• NB-UVB for superficial disease
• For steroid-refractory cases
THIRD-LINE
(Spreading)		 Generalized Phototherapy
Weekly Methotrexate 		• For widespread but superficial disease
• First step into systemic therapy
LINEAR / GENERALIZED / DEEP / PANSCLEROTIC MORPHEA
HIGH MORBIDITY RISK → AGGRESSIVE SYSTEMIC TREATMENT FROM OUTSET
Treatment Line Therapy Dosing & Specifics
FIRST-LINE
(GOLD STANDARD)		 MTX + Systemic Steroids MTX:
• Adults: 15-25 mg/week (PO or SC)
• Children: 0.3-0.5 mg/kg/week

Steroids (PULSED preferred):
• IV methylprednisolone 1000 mg/day (adults)
• 30 mg/kg/day (peds) × 3 days/month
• Daily: Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day
MONOTHERAPY
(Superficial Only)		 Phototherapy UVA1, BB-UVA, or PUVA
• Only if superficial + no joint contractures
SECOND-LINE
(Steroid-sparing)		 Mycophenolate Mofetil
JAK Inhibitors
Tocilizumab 		• MMF: Well-tolerated, beneficial even after other failures
• Tofacitinib, Baricitinib for recalcitrant disease
• IL-6R inhibitor for pansclerotic morphea (children)
OTHER OPTIONS
(Limited data)		 Abatacept
Systemic Retinoids
Hydroxychloroquine 		• CTLA4 fusion protein
• Acitretin, isotretinoin
• Limited evidence base
ANCILLARY MANAGEMENT (INACTIVE DISEASE)
Physical Therapy CRUCIAL for all patients at risk of joint contractures or mobility loss
Reconstructive Surgery For INACTIVE linear or en coup de sabre morphea
• Functional or cosmetic defect correction
Volume Restoration • Autologous fat transplant for Parry-Romberg syndrome
• Hyaluronic acid fillers for facial morphea
KEY BOARD EXAM PEARLS
Timeline for Response Escalate therapy if NO improvement after 8-10 weeks
Activity Assessment Look for erythematous/lilac border = ACTIVE disease
High-Risk Subtypes Linear, Generalized, Deep, Pansclerotic = Immediate systemic therapy
Phototherapy Hierarchy UVA1 > BB-UVA > PUVA > NB-UVB (superficial only)
