Bowel-Associated Dermatosis–Arthritis Syndrome (BADAS)

I. General Characteristics
Onset 1–6 years after bowel surgery
Systemic Feel Serum sickness-like illness: patient feels systemically unwell
Constitutional Symptoms
  • Flu-like / Serum sickness-like
  • Fever, chills, malaise
  • Often precede skin eruption
II. Cutaneous Manifestations ("Dermatosis")
Lesion Morphology
  • Pleomorphic and recurrent
  • Start as erythematous macules → papules → purpuric vesiculopustules
Number & Distribution
  • Few or many lesions
  • Favors proximal extremities & upper trunk
Course Lesions last 2–4 weeks, recur every 4–6 weeks
Nodular Findings Recurrent, tender, erythematous subcutaneous nodules — may be associated with fever
Histologic Variants
  • Nodular, non-suppurative, lobular neutrophilic panniculitis
    Distribution: Trunk & extremities
    Outcome: Heals with depressed scars
  • Erythema nodosum
    Distribution: Lower extremities
III. Systemic Manifestations ("Arthritis" & More)
Joints
  • Tenosynovitis – highly characteristic
  • Polyarthritis – non-destructive
GI / Hepatic
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Hepatic dysfunction or failure
Renal / Biliary
  • Renal calculi (calcium oxalate)
  • Gallstones
Nutritional Deficiencies
  • Zinc deficiency
  • Vitamin A deficiency
  • Thiamine deficiency (beriberi)
Metabolic Hyperuricemia
