🧬 Acantholytic Disorders

High-Yield Review for Saudi Dermatology Board Exam

Darier's Disease & Hailey-Hailey Disease

🔬 DARIER'S DISEASE (Keratosis Follicularis)

🧬 GENETICS & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Inheritance: AD; complete penetrance, variable expressivity
Peak onset: Puberty (70% prior to 20 yo)
Course: Chronic course, without spontaneous remission
🎯 MUTATION PATHWAY:
ATP2A2 (encodes SERCA2 = calcium ATPase of endoplasmic reticulum) → defective Ca2+ sequestration into ER → impaired synthesis and folding of cell adhesion proteins → keratinocyte acantholysis and apoptosis
📋 CLINICAL FEATURES
Primary Lesions

Malodorous, warty, crusted, and red-brown papules/plaques in seborrheic distribution involving the trunk, scalp (especially its margins), face, and lateral aspects of the neck.

Despite initial description as follicular dyskeratosis, the papules are NOT limited to perifollicular location

Lesions tend to become confluent and may form papillomatous masses.

Associated Features

• Small (2-3 mm) hypomelanotic macules may be admixed with keratotic papules, especially in darkly pigmented population

🎯 ALMOST ALWAYS: Keratotic palmar papules/pits
(NOT Acrokeratosis verruciformis of Hopf!!)
Nail Changes

Longitudinal red and white alternating nail streaks with distal "V-shaped" notching

Oral Involvement

50% have oral cobblestoning

Most common site: Hard palate

Other sites: Gingiva, buccal mucosa, and tongue

🎨 SEGMENTAL DARIER'S
Type 1 (Most Common)

Blaschkoid streaks of Darier's lesions

Cause: Post-zygotic ATP2A2 mutation

Type 2

Generalized Darier's with focal areas of severe involvement

Cause: Heterozygous germline mutation + postzygotic loss of other allele

🔄 LESS COMMON PRESENTATIONS
Morphologic Variants
Guttate hypopigmentation
Hyperpigmented flat-topped papules
Vesiculobullous lesions
Acral hemorrhagic lesions
Keratoderma (spiked)
"Cornifying" (cutaneous horns)
Comedones, facial cysts
Acne conglobata
Periocular nodulocystic lesions
Alopecia, cutis verticis gyrata
🎯 Special Note: Especially in darkly pigmented population
🎯 Acral hemorrhagic lesions: Particular ATP2A2 missense mutation (N767S)
🎯 Vesiculobullous lesions: Since it is acantholytic disease
Distribution Patterns

• Predominantly involving intertriginous areas

• Sun-exposed skin

• The extremities, acral sites

• The scalp (pityriasis amiantacea-like)

• Segmental (type 1 > 2)

Extracutaneous

• Oral mucosal erosions

• Corneal opacities or ulcers

• Salivary gland obstruction

• Laryngeal involvement

*A persistent acantholytic dermatosis and extensive lentiginous "freckling" without an underlying ATP2A2 mutation has also been described.
*The differential diagnosis may include familial dyskeratotic comedones.
⚡ MODIFYING FACTORS
Summer
UV irradiation
Sweating
Heat
Occlusion
Lithium carbonate
🎯 Key Point: UVB irradiation has been shown to suppress ATP2A2 expression in keratinocytes
⚠️ COMPLICATIONS
1
Secondary Infections

• Prone to bacteria, yeast, and dermatophytes → malodor, + viruses

• ~70% Staphylococcus aureus colonization in lesional skin

• ~50% S. aureus in nares, correlates with more severe phenotype

• HSV → Kaposi's varicelliform eruption (most concerning) → systemic antiviral meds

• HPV → warts → risk for SCC

• Cowpox infection

2
Salivary Gland Issues

Obstruction of salivary gland ducts can lead to painful glandular swelling

3
Neuropsychiatric

↑ incidence of epilepsy, intellectual impairment, bipolar disorder, and depression

4
Ocular Complications

Corneal ulcerations or staphylococcal endophthalmitis (very rarely)

5
Malignancy

SCC in lesions may be related to infection with HPV or changes in keratinocyte adhesion and proliferation secondary to SERCA2 mutation

🔬 HISTOPATHOLOGY
Main Features: Papillomatous epidermal hyperplasia with epidermal acantholysis and dyskeratosis (corps ronds and grains)
Corps ronds: Large, round, acantholytic keratinocytes with dark nuclei surrounded by a bright pink rim of condensed keratin; located mostly in spinous layer (+SB)
Corps grains: Flattened cells comprised of bright pink condensed keratin and a very thin dark nuclear remnant (looks like parakeratotic nucleus); located mostly in s.corneum
DIF: Negative
🔄 DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
Acrokeratosis Verruciformis of Hopf

• Flat-topped, wart-like papules on the dorsal aspects of the extremities are a common manifestation of Darier disease

• Debate as to whether acrokeratosis verruciformis is a separate entity or an allelic forme fruste of Darier disease

• Reports of underlying ATP2A2 mutation in some, but not all, patients with acrokeratosis verruciformis suggest genetic heterogeneity

• Patients with Darier disease often develop warty lesions on extremities before keratotic papules appear in seborrheic distribution

• Some individuals presenting with acrokeratosis verruciformis may later be diagnosed as actually having Darier disease

🎯 Particular ATP2A2 mutations: P602L, A698V identified in patients with isolated acrokeratosis verruciformis as well as those with acral warty lesions followed by onset of Darier disease after intense sun exposure
Others

Seborrheic distribution:

• Severe seborrheic dermatitis

• Grover disease

Flexural distribution:

• Hailey-Hailey disease

• Pemphigus vegetans (Hallopeau type)

• Pemphigoid vegetans

• Blastomycosis-like pyoderma

caused by dimorphic fungus Blastomyces dermatitidis,
present as
Verrucous skin lesions (found primarily on sun-exposed areas, including the face and extremities)
Histology showed pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia with broad based budding and yeast

Occasionally, a sporadic disorder referred to as "papular acantholytic dyskeratosis" may be localized to the vulvocrural area
💊 TREATMENT

🏠 GENERAL MEASURES

• Lightweight clothes and sun protection

• Avoid heat, sweating, and exposure to UV radiation

• Daily skin care includes the use of antimicrobial cleansers to prevent malodorous bacterial colonization and keratolytic emollients to reduce scaling and irritation

🧴 TOPICAL THERAPY

🎯 KEY POINT: Topical retinoids are more effective as monotherapy than topical vitamin D analogues, or corticosteroids

• Liberal use of emollients

• Combination therapy with topical retinoids + midpotency topical corticosteroids

• Topical calcineurin inhibitors

• Intermittent use of topical antibiotics and antifungal agents for the malodor

• Others: topical 5-fluorouracil, diclofenac sodium 3% gel, cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) inhibition

💊 SYSTEMIC THERAPY

Isotretinoin, acitretin, and alitretinoin → improvement in approximately 90% of patients
⚠️ IMPORTANT: Relapse occurs after cessation of therapy
⚠️ CONTRAINDICATION: In patients with predominantly bullous or intertriginous lesions, oral retinoids may aggravate the disease and are not recommended

• Reserved for patients with severe disease unresponsive to topical treatment

• Cyclosporine alternative if retinoids not tolerated

🔪 SURGICAL THERAPY

• For focal recalcitrant lesions, particularly in the flexural and gluteal areas

• Options: Excision followed by split-thickness grafting, dermabrasion, or laser removal (CO₂ or erbium:YAG)

🎯 CRITICAL: Destructive treatment must include the follicular infundibulum in order to prevent recurrences
🧪 HAILEY-HAILEY DISEASE (Familial Benign Chronic Pemphigus)

🧬 GENETICS & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Inheritance: AD; complete penetrance, variable expressivity
Frequency: HHD is seen nearly as often as Darier disease
Age of onset: Wider range than Darier's (teens - 20 yo mostly, but may arise later)
🎯 MUTATION PATHWAY:
ATP2C1 (encodes hSPCA1, a Ca2+ ATPase of the Golgi apparatus) → defective Ca2+ sequestration into golgi → impaired processing of proteins involved in cell-cell adhesion → acantholysis
📋 CLINICAL FEATURES
Primary Distribution
🎯 MOST COMMONLY: Affects intertriginous sites

• Lateral neck

• Inframammary

• Axillae

• Groin

• Perianal

Female patients occasionally present with isolated vulvar disease
Lesion Morphology

Progression: Subtle, flaccid vesicle on normal or inflamed skin → ruptures to give macerated, eroded plaques, often with circinate shape

Chronic changes: Development of chronic, moist, malodorous vegetations and painful fissures is common

Healing: Occurs without scarring, leaving postinflammatory hyperpigmentation

Associated Features

Nails: Longitudinal leukonychia

🎯 KEY DIFFERENCE: No mucosal involvement (helps DDx from Darier's) (they can have but quite rare!)
🎨 CLINICAL SUBTYPES OF HAILEY-HAILEY DISEASE
Segmental Type 1

Like Darier segmental type 1

Segmental Type 2

Like Darier segmental type 2

🎯 IMPORTANT: Unlike HHD due to a heterozygous mutation, the affected segments display acantholysis within adnexal structures, which might make it impossible to eradicate foci of blistering using superficial ablative methods
⚡ MODIFYING FACTORS
Friction
Summer
Heat and sweating
Microbial colonization
Secondary infections
🎯 Key Points:
• Especially Staph aureus → more severe and widespread disease
• UV irradiation does NOT appear to influence the course of the disease
⚠️ COMPLICATIONS
1
Secondary Infections

Kaposi's varicelliform eruption is most concerning

Recalcitrant intertriginous lesions have been described in association with HSV infections

2
Malignancy

SCC in chronic lesions

🔬 HISTOPATHOLOGY
Main Features: Psoriasiform hyperplasia (differentiates from pemphigus) with diffuse acantholysis (resembling a "dilapidated brick wall")
Dyskeratosis: Fewer dyskeratotic keratinocytes than Darier's
DIF: Negative, thus excluding pemphigus vulgaris
🔄 DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
Main Differentials

• Intertrigo or candidiasis

• Inverse psoriasis

• Pemphigus vegetans (Hallopeau type) or pemphigoid vegetans

• Darier disease

💊 TREATMENT

🏠 GENERAL MEASURES

• Wearing lightweight clothes

• Avoid friction and sweating

• Adhesive dressings should be avoided

• Colonization and secondary infections are addressed via antimicrobial cleansers and appropriate topical and/or systemic antimicrobial agents, respectively

🧴 TOPICAL AND INTRALESIONAL THERAPY

• Topical corticosteroids often help to reduce inflammation and can be combined with topical antimicrobials and cleansers

• Early application of corticosteroids may ameliorate developing lesions

• Intralesional corticosteroids can be tried when disease is refractory to topical preparations

• Topical calcineurin inhibitors: Both improvement and worsening has been described

• Case reports: Improvement with topical 5-fluorouracil and vitamin D analogues

• Botulinum toxin injections

• Zinc oxide paste in skin folds to protect against friction and moisture

🔪 SURGICAL THERAPY

• Consider for disease unresponsive to general measures and topical treatments

• Evolution: Wide excision followed by grafting has been replaced by more superficial ablative techniques

• Most well-documented method: Dermabrasion

• Equally effective: Laser vaporization of the epidermis (scanned CO₂ or erbium:YAG) and photodynamic therapy

💊 SYSTEMIC THERAPY

Evidence: With the exception of antimicrobial agents for secondary infections, there is no strong evidence to support the use of any additional systemic therapies in HHD

• Long-term continuous or intermittent doxycycline is effective

🎯 KEY DIFFERENCE: Retinoids not nearly as effective as in Darier's

• Others: Immunomodulatory drugs, such as prednisone, cyclosporine, methotrexate, dapsone, or afamelanotide
