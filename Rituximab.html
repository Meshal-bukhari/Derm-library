RITUXIMAB

Anti-CD20 Monoclonal Antibody | Board Review

Core Memory Anchor

RITUXIMAB = "MATURE B-CELL KILLER"

🎯 Targets CD20 on mature B cells only | 💉 IV only | ⏱️ Effects: 3 weeks to start, lasts 6 months

Mechanism & Target

🧬 What Rituximab IS and ISN'T

✅ TARGETS: Mature B cells with CD20

❌ DOES NOT AFFECT:

  • 🔸 B-cell stem cells
  • 🔸 Pro B cells
  • 🔸 Plasma cells

💡 Remember: It's SELECTIVE, not a B-cell genocide!

Dosing Protocols

🏥 Oncology Protocol

375 mg/m² weekly for 4-8 weeks

🦴 Rheumatology Protocol

1g on Day 1 & Day 15

Infusion Rates - CRITICAL!

First Infusion Protocol

▼

START: 50 mg/hour

INCREASE: By 50 mg/hour every 30 minutes

MAXIMUM: 400 mg/hour

⚠️ NEVER give as IV push!

Future Infusions

▼

START: 100 mg/hour

INCREASE: By 100 mg/hour every hour

MAXIMUM: 400 mg/hour

LIFE-THREATENING COMPLICATIONS

⚡ Can occur within 24 hours of first infusion!

  • Anaphylactic shock
  • ARDS
  • Myocardial infarction
  • Ventricular arrhythmias
  • Tumor lysis syndrome → Acute kidney failure (lymphoma patients)
  • Bowel obstruction/perforation

Late Serious Complications

Hepatitis B Reactivation

▼

Can lead to fulminant hepatitis

Prevention: Screen all patients for HBV before treatment

Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)

▼

Timing: Usually after 3-5 courses

Cause: JC virus (polyoma virus) reactivation

Outcome: IRREVERSIBLE

Very Rare but Deadly Mucocutaneous Lesions

Paraneoplastic Pemphigus

In cancer patients

SJS/TEN

Stevens-Johnson/Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis

Dermatology Indications

✅ APPROVED USES

  • Pemphigus vulgaris FIRST LINE!
  • Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (treatment or remission)
  • Microscopic polyangiitis (treatment or remission)

❌ WILL NOT WORK FOR

  • Discoid lupus - Not B-cell dependent!
  • Dermatomyositis - Exception: vasculitis with Anti-MDA, anti-NXP2 (children)
    → Most effective refractory treatment is IVIG

Treatment Timeline

Remember: "2-6-26"

2 • 6 • 26

2-6 months: B-cell regeneration

4-26 months: Pemphigus vulgaris duration (check anti-desmoglein antibodies)

Critical Clinical Pearls

BLACKBOX WARNINGS

  1. Infusion reactions
  2. Severe mucocutaneous reactions
  3. Hepatitis B reactivation
  4. Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy

💉 Vaccine Timing

🔹 Non-live vaccines: Give ≥4 weeks BEFORE rituximab

🔹 Live vaccines: Avoid for 12-20 weeks POST rituximab

⚠️ Drug Interactions

❌ DO NOT combine with cisplatin → Increased nephrotoxicity
