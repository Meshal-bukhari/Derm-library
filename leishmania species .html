Leishmania Species Clinical Summary

``` <
Leishmania Species/Complex Geographic Region Characteristic Clinical Presentation(s) High-Yield Buzzwords/Synonyms
L. tropica Old World Anthroponotic (Urban) CL: A chronic, "dry," late-ulcerating sore. High potential for Leishmaniasis Recidivans (recurrence in a scar). "Oriental Sore," "Baghdad Boil," "Dry/Late Ulcerative," "Recidivans"
L. major Old World Zoonotic (Rural) CL: An acute, "moist," early-ulcerating, inflammatory lesion with a more rapid, self-healing course. "Moist/Early Ulcerative"
L. braziliensis New World
(Viannia Subgenus)		 The #1 cause of Mucocutaneous Leishmaniasis (MCL). Causes severe, destructive lesions of the nose and mouth. Can also cause Disseminated CL. "Espundia", "Tapir Face," Mucosal Destruction
L. mexicana New World Classic New World Cutaneous Leishmaniasis. Typically a single, self-limited ulcer. The characteristic cause of the Chiclero Ulcer on the ear. "Chiclero Ulcer", Autochthonous US (Texas) cases
L. amazonensis New World The #1 New World cause of Diffuse Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (DCL). Anergic form with multiple non-ulcerated nodules, mimicking lepromatous leprosy. Diffuse Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (DCL), Pseudolepromatous
L. aethiopica Old World
(Africa)		 The #1 Old World cause of Diffuse Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (DCL). Can also cause mucocutaneous disease. Diffuse Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (DCL)
L. donovani Old World
(Asia, Africa)		 The classic cause of Visceral Leishmaniasis (VL) in adults. Also, the primary cause of Post-Kala-azar Dermal Leishmaniasis (PKDL). "Kala-azar", "PKDL", Dumdum fever
L. infantum/chagasi Old & New World The primary cause of Visceral Leishmaniasis (VL) in children and in the setting of HIV co-infection. "Infantile Kala-azar"
