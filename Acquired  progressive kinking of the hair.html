Acquired Progressive Kinking of the Hair (APKH)

A High-Yield Clinical Snapshot for the Saudi Board Exam

Synopsis: The Core Concept

Acquired progressive kinking of the hair (APKH) is a rare disorder where hair in one or more scalp regions undergoes a progressive textural change.

  • Hair becomes: Dry, lusterless, wiry, and kinky.
  • Associated changes: Decreased rate of growth and a darkening of color.
  • Boundaries: The transition between normal and abnormal hair is not sharply defined.
  • Demographics: More common in males.
  • Prognostic Link: In some cases, it has preceded the development of male pattern alopecia.

Look For: Clinical Presentation

The key finding is an acquired patch of darker hair that, on closer inspection, reveals a significant textural change. Patients often give a history that the lesion started as a small patch that gradually expanded over time.

Diagnostic Pearl (High-Yield Clue)

The patient's history is crucial. Because the area of altered hair texture has a decreased rate of growth, they will report needing to cut it less frequently than the rest of their scalp hair. This is a highly specific clue.

⚠Differential Diagnosis & Pitfalls

Woolly Hair Nevus
Key Differentiator: ONSET. Woolly hair nevus is congenital. Onset usually occurs at or soon after birth. APKH is, by definition, acquired.
Whisker Hair
Key Differentiator: LOCATION. This is considered a variant of APKH where the textural change is symmetrically localized to the area around the ears. APKH can affect any region of the scalp.
Therapy-Induced Kinking of Hair
Key Differentiator: HISTORY. This is directly linked to a history of receiving therapy, rarely described in patients receiving etretinate.

Confirmation & Future Course

Best Tests

The definitive confirmation is through microscopic evaluation of affected hairs.

Hairs show kinks and twists, and are often finer or thicker than hair elsewhere on the scalp.

Management Pearl (Prognosis)

This is a critical point for patient counseling and exam questions. Male pattern alopecia does eventuate in the majority of cases. APKH is often a harbinger of future androgenetic alopecia.

Therapy

There is no specific treatment for APKH.

Saudi Board Exam Alert

1. The "Congenital vs. Acquired" Trick:

They will describe the classic hair morphology in a very young child or infant. If the onset is from birth, the answer is Woolly Hair Nevus, not APKH. The name "Acquired" is the key.

2. The "Prognosis" Trick:

They will ask for the most likely long-term outcome for a young man with APKH. The correct answer is the development of male pattern alopecia, not spontaneous resolution or a scarring process.

3. The "Unique History" Trick:

They will test the Diagnostic Pearl. A patient reporting that a specific patch of hair grows slower and requires less frequent cutting is pathognomonic for APKH in an exam setting.

4. The "Location" Trick:

If the kinking is described as being symmetrically localized around the ears, the most specific diagnosis is Whisker Hair.
