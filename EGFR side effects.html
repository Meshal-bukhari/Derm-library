EGFR INHIBITORS: Cetuximab | Erlotinib | Gefitinib | Panitumumab

🔥 HALLMARK ADVERSE EVENT

🎯 PAPULOPUSTULAR (ACNEIFORM) ERUPTION
  • Frequency: Most common AE - affects up to 90% of patients
  • KEY DISTINGUISHER: ABSENCE OF COMEDONES
  • Prognostic Value: Severity correlates with tumor response & survival
📅 TIMELINE OF PAPULOPUSTULAR ERUPTION:
  • Week 1: Erythema, edema, dysesthesias
  • Weeks 2-3 (PEAK): Erythematous follicular papules & pustules + crusting
  • Month 2+: Resolves to persistent erythema with telangiectasias
Distribution: Seborrheic areas (scalp, face, postauricular, neck, shoulders, upper trunk in V-pattern)
Complications: Secondary impetiginization with Staphylococcus aureus in up to 38%
Associated Symptoms: Pruritus (distinguishes from acne vulgaris)

🏆 THE "BIG THREE" AEs

Most Common Cutaneous AEs:
  1. Papulopustular Rash - up to 90%
  2. Xerosis (Dry Skin) - up to 35%
  3. Paronychia - 12-56%

🌟 UNIQUE & HIGHLY TESTABLE FINDINGS

🔍 TRICHOMEGALY (Eyelash Growth)
  • Incidence: Up to 100%
  • Description: Elongation and curling of eyelashes
  • ⚠️ Major Complication: Trichiasis → Corneal ulceration
💅 PERIUNGUAL PYOGENIC GRANULOMAS
  • Description: Eruptive, friable vascular papules around nails
  • Significance: Highly characteristic finding
