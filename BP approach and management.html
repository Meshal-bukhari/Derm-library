📋 BULLOUS PEMPHIGOID

Final Board Exam Review Summary

High-Yield Facts for Quick Review

🔬 DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP

1. Skin Biopsies

Routine H&E Biopsy:
  • Site: Edge of fresh, small, tense vesicle/bulla
  • Key Finding: Subepidermal blister
  • Infiltrate: Dermal eosinophils + mononuclear cells
  • Non-bullous phase: Eosinophilic spongiosis + eosinophils at DEJ
🏆 GOLD STANDARD: Direct Immunofluorescence (DIF)
  • Site: Perilesional skin (1-2 cm from bulla)
  • Pathognomonic Finding: Fine, linear, continuous IgG/C3 at BMZ
  • IgG Subclasses: IgG4 > IgG1
  • Pattern: "n-serrated" (vs EBA = "u-serrated")

2. Serological Tests

Test Sensitivity Specificity Key Details
IIF (Salt-split skin) 60-80% High Binding to epidermal side ("roof")
BP180-NC16A ELISA ~80% ≥90% Correlates with disease severity
BP230 ELISA Variable ≥90% Increases overall sensitivity by ~10%

3. Supportive Labs

Peripheral Eosinophilia: Present in ~50% of patients (supportive, not diagnostic)

💊 TREATMENT ALGORITHM

Treatment Principle: Choice depends on disease severity + patient comorbidities
Disease Control Definition: No new lesions or pruritus for ≥2 weeks

🔥 EXTENSIVE Disease (>10 new blisters/day OR large areas)

FIRST-LINE (Preferred): Ultrapotent Topical Steroids
  • Agent: Clobetasol 0.05% cream
  • Dose: 40g/day initially (standard) or 10-30g/day (mild regimen)
  • Advantage: Same efficacy as oral steroids, fewer side effects, ↓mortality
FIRST-LINE (Alternative): Systemic Steroids
  • Agent: Oral prednisone
  • Dose: 0.5-0.75 mg/kg/day
  • ⚠️ WARNING: Doses ≥1 mg/kg/day → significant side effects + ↑mortality

🎯 MILD/LOCALIZED Disease

  • Mainstay: Potent topical corticosteroids
  • Alternative: Tetracyclines ± Nicotinamide
    • Doxycycline + Nicotinamide 500-2000mg/day
    • Better safety profile than systemic steroids

⚡ STEROID-SPARING Agents (Second-line)

Agent Dosage Evidence Level Special Considerations
Azathioprine 0.5-2.5 mg/kg/day Level 1 Check TPMT levels
Mycophenolate Mofetil 1.5-3 g/day Level 1 Generally well-tolerated
Methotrexate 7.5-15 mg/week Level 2 Monitor renal function in elderly
Dapsone Variable Level 3 Requires G6PD screening

🚀 REFRACTORY Disease

  • Biologics: Omalizumab, Dupilumab, Rituximab (Level 3)
  • Others: IVIg (Level 1), Cyclophosphamide (Level 3)

📊 TAPERING & PROGNOSIS

Tapering Protocol:
  • Start tapering: After 2+ weeks of disease control
  • Duration: Progressive taper over 4-6 months
  • Maintenance: Low-dose prednisone <10mg/day or topical clobetasol 10g/week for 3-6 months
⚠️ RELAPSE RATES:
  • ~30% relapse in first year
  • ~50% relapse after therapy cessation (most within 3 months)
📈 MONITORING: Anti-BP180 IgG ELISA levels correlate with disease activity and predict relapse
💀 MORTALITY: 10-40% first-year death rate (mainly from high-dose steroid complications)

🎯 HIGH-YIELD BOARD PEARLS

  • DIF is GOLD STANDARD - remember perilesional sampling
  • "n-serrated" pattern distinguishes BP from EBA
  • IIF on salt-split skin: BP binds to epidermal side
  • BP180-NC16A ELISA best correlates with disease severity
  • Ultrapotent topicals preferred over systemic steroids for extensive disease
  • Never exceed 1mg/kg/day prednisone - increases mortality
  • Tetracyclines + nicotinamide good alternative for mild disease
  • 50% eosinophilia - supportive finding
