Treatment Options for Stage IIB (Tumor Stage) Mycosis Fungoides

Treatment Type Specific Options When/Notes
Skin-directed Narrowband UVB Use when lesions remain patch-only
Skin-directed Topical corticosteroids, topical retinoids, imiquimod For limited patches or thin plaques as second-line therapy
Radiation Local radiotherapy For a single tumor or persistent lesions
Radiation Total skin electron beam (TSEB) Generalized thick plaques; may be followed by other skin-directed therapies or repeated at 10–20 Gy
Combination PUVA or HN2 plus local radiotherapy When only a few tumors are present
Systemic PUVA + IFN-α or PUVA + retinoids (acitretin, oral bexarotene) Consider after relapse following TSEB
Systemic HDAC inhibitors (romidepsin, vorinostat) Second-line options for relapse or refractory disease
Systemic Denileukin diftitox, mogamulizumab Consider in refractory cases before chemotherapy
Systemic Allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation Option prior to systemic chemotherapy
