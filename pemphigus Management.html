🎯 PEMPHIGUS MANAGEMENT

Master Guide for Saudi Board Dermatology Exam

🧠 MEMORY ANCHOR

Pemphigus = "FIRE" on the skin
Flaccid bullae • Intraepithelial split • Rituximab is gold standard • Erosions predominate

⚡ KEY CONCEPT

Pemphigus Vulgaris vs Foliaceus:

  • ✅ Vulgaris: Mucosal + Cutaneous (Dsg3 ± Dsg1)
  • ✅ Foliaceus: Cutaneous ONLY (Dsg1 only)
  • ✅ Remember: "3" in Dsg3 = "E" (Eating affected in vulgaris)

⚠️ MORTALITY ALERT

Pre-steroid era: 70-100% mortality in 1-5 years
Current mortality: 5-10% (mostly from treatment complications)

Autoimmune Mechanism

• Target: Desmoglein proteins (cell adhesion molecules)

• Result: Acantholysis (loss of keratinocyte adhesion)

• Location: Intraepithelial blistering

Board Tip: Nikolsky sign = Lateral pressure causes blister extension

🔍 Pemphigus Vulgaris

• Oral involvement in ~90%

• Often starts in mouth before skin

• Flaccid bullae → erosions

• Positive Nikolsky sign

🔍 Pemphigus Foliaceus

• NO mucosal involvement

• Superficial blisters (subcorneal)

• "Corn flake" scale-crust

• Face/scalp/trunk distribution

📋 DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

Step 1: Clinical Suspicion
Flaccid bullae + Erosions + Nikolsky sign
Step 2: Biopsy for DIF
Perilesional skin → "Fishnet" IgG pattern
Step 3: ELISA Testing
Anti-Dsg1 & Anti-Dsg3 antibodies

🎯 Disease Severity Classification

Mild Pemphigus:

  • • <5% BSA involvement
  • • No/limited oral lesions
  • • No impaired food intake

Moderate-Severe Pemphigus:

  • • ≥5% BSA involvement
  • • Multiple mucosal sites
  • • Weight loss/dysphagia
  • • Significant pain

🧠 ELISA INTERPRETATION

Dsg1+ / Dsg3- = Foliaceus (Skin only)
Dsg1- / Dsg3+ = Mucosal vulgaris
Dsg1+ / Dsg3+ = Mucocutaneous vulgaris

Response Type Definition Clinical Significance
Disease Control No new lesions + healing begins Can start tapering after 2 weeks
Complete Remission ON therapy No lesions while on treatment Continue current regimen
Complete Remission OFF therapy No lesions ≥2 months after stopping Ultimate treatment goal
Relapse ≥3 new lesions/month not healing in 1 week Requires treatment escalation

🚀 FIRST-LINE TREATMENT ALGORITHM

PREFERRED: Rituximab + Prednisone
Superior efficacy & steroid-sparing
ALTERNATIVE: Prednisone + MMF/AZA
When rituximab not feasible
MILD DISEASE ONLY: Rituximab + Topical steroids
Limited data, reserve for selected cases

💊 Initial Prednisone Dosing

• Mild: 0.5-1 mg/kg/day

• Moderate-Severe: 1-1.5 mg/kg/day

⚡ With Rituximab: Use LOWER doses (0.5-1 mg/kg)

Prednisone Tapering Schedule

Prerequisites: Disease control × 2 weeks + 80% healing

  1. 1 mg/kg → 0.75 mg/kg (after 7 days no new lesions)
  2. 0.75 mg/kg → 0.5 mg/kg (after 7 days no new lesions)
  3. 0.5 mg/kg → 30 mg/day (after 14 days + 7 days no new lesions)
  4. Then: 30→25→20→15→10→7.5→5→2.5→0 mg
  5. Each reduction: Wait ≥14 days + 7 days lesion-free

Relapse Definition

≥3 new lesions/month not healing in 1 week
OR extension of established lesions

If on Rituximab + Prednisone:

• Relapse >4-6 months post-rituximab → Repeat rituximab cycle

• Relapse <4-6 months → ↑ prednisone to previous effective dose

If on Prednisone ± MMF/AZA:

• Add rituximab if feasible

• Otherwise: ↑ prednisone + switch immunosuppressant

💊 MEDICATION HIERARCHY

1st: Rituximab + Prednisone
2nd: Prednisone + AZA/MMF
3rd: Dapsone (foliaceus)
Refractory: IVIG, Cyclophosphamide

🎯 RITUXIMAB

Initial: 1000 mg IV × 2 doses (2 weeks apart)
Premedication: Methylprednisolone 100 mg IV
Maintenance: 500 mg at month 12, then q6 months

⚠️ Major Risks:

• Infusion reactions

• Infections (including PML)

• Delayed effect (8-12 weeks)

💊 AZATHIOPRINE

MUST CHECK TPMT BEFORE STARTING
Normal TPMT: Start 1 mg/kg → 2.5 mg/kg/day
Intermediate TPMT: Max 0.5-1.5 mg/kg/day
Absent TPMT: CONTRAINDICATED

Monitoring: CBC q2 weeks × 3 months, then q2-3 months

💊 MYCOPHENOLATE MOFETIL

Adult dose: 1g BID (2g/day total)
Alternative: Mycophenolate sodium 720mg BID

Advantages: Easier dosing, better GI tolerance

Monitoring: CBC q2 weeks × 2-3 months, then monthly × 1 year

DAPSONE

• Best for: Pemphigus foliaceus

• Dose: 50-200 mg/day (max 1.5 mg/kg)

• Check G6PD before starting

• Monitor: Hemolysis, methemoglobinemia

METHOTREXATE

• Dose: 10-25 mg/week

• Limited evidence

• 70% able to stop prednisone in one study

TETRACYCLINE + NICOTINAMIDE

• Tetracycline 500mg QID + Nicotinamide 500mg TID

• Alternative: Doxycycline 100mg BID

• Weak evidence, well-tolerated

🚨 CRITICAL MONITORING POINTS

• Disease activity: Weekly initially, then monthly

• ELISA titers: Correlate with disease (not perfect)

• Drug toxicity: See specific schedules below

• Infection surveillance: Continuous

📊 Laboratory Monitoring Schedule

Medication Baseline Initial Period Maintenance
Prednisone Glucose, BP, BMD Glucose monthly Q3-6 months
Azathioprine TPMT, CBC, LFTs CBC q2 weeks × 3 mo CBC q2-3 months
MMF CBC, Renal, LFTs CBC q2 weeks × 3 mo CBC q3 months
Dapsone G6PD, CBC, LFTs CBC weekly × 1 mo CBC monthly

Infection Prevention & Management

• HSV prophylaxis: Consider after first episode

• Candida: Nystatin S&S for all on steroids

• PCP: Consider if high-dose steroids >1 month

• Warning signs: Teach patient about infection symptoms

Non-healing Lesions DDx:

1. Secondary infection (especially HSV)

2. Inadequate treatment

3. Drug-induced pemphigus

4. Misdiagnosis

🩹 Wound Care

• Drain large blisters (leave roof intact)

• Antibiotic ointment to erosions

• High-potency topical steroids for persistent lesions

• Consider intralesional triamcinolone (20mg/mL)

👄 Oral Care

• Avoid spicy/hot/abrasive foods

• Viscous lidocaine 2% PRN

• Dexamethasone mouthwash TID

• Soft toothbrush + bland toothpaste

🎯 TOP 10 BOARD PEARLS

  1. Rituximab + low-dose prednisone > high-dose prednisone alone
  2. Always check TPMT before azathioprine
  3. Foliaceus = superficial = better prognosis
  4. 90% pemphigus vulgaris has oral involvement
  5. ELISA correlates with activity (not 100%)
  6. Most mortality from treatment complications
  7. HSV can prevent healing - always consider
  8. Dapsone works best for foliaceus
  9. Wait 2 weeks control + 80% healing before tapering
  10. Relapse = ≥3 new lesions/month

🧠 BOARD-STYLE QUESTIONS

Q1: A 45-year-old with newly diagnosed severe pemphigus vulgaris. Best initial treatment?

A: Rituximab 1000mg × 2 doses + Prednisone 1mg/kg/day
Key: Rituximab allows lower steroid doses with better outcomes

Q2: Patient on azathioprine develops pancytopenia. Most likely cause?

A: Low/absent TPMT activity
Key: Always check TPMT before starting azathioprine

Q3: Pemphigus foliaceus patient failing prednisone. Next best add-on?

A: Dapsone (after checking G6PD)
Key: Dapsone particularly effective for foliaceus

Q4: When to start tapering prednisone in pemphigus?

A: After 2 weeks of disease control + 80% healing
Key: Must meet BOTH criteria before tapering

⚡ LAST-MINUTE REVIEW

Rituximab doses: 1000mg day 1 & 15 → 500mg at month 12

Prednisone: Mild 0.5mg/kg, Severe 1-1.5mg/kg

Complete remission off therapy: No lesions ≥2 months after stopping

Relapse definition: ≥3 new lesions/month

TPMT testing: MANDATORY before azathioprine

🚫 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ Starting azathioprine without TPMT testing

❌ Tapering steroids too quickly

❌ Missing HSV superinfection in non-healing lesions

❌ Forgetting Candida prophylaxis in oral pemphigus

❌ Using rituximab alone in moderate-severe disease
