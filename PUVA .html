PUVA Phototherapy Flowchart

Compact Protocol with Key Risks

START PUVA EVALUATION
Absolute Contraindication:
Concomitant Cyclosporine Therapy
Relative Contraindications:
Hepatic/Renal Impairment, Age < 12, Pregnancy
Administer 8-MOP (0.6-0.8 mg/kg)
Wait 1-3 hours before UVA exposure
Starting Dose Method?
European Approach
Determine MPD
Read at 72-96 hours!
Starting Dose = 50-70% of MPD
US Approach
Use Skin Phototype Chart (J/cm²)
  • Type I: 0.25-0.5
  • Type II: 0.5-1.0
  • Type III: 1.5
  • Type IV: 2.0
  • Type V: 2.5
  • Type VI: 3.0
TREATMENT CYCLE
Administer UVA (2-4x / week) & Assess Skin
Skin Reaction?
No Erythema: Increase Dose up to 30%
(Max 2 increases/week) with at least 72 hours between increases to avoid accumulation of delayed phototoxicity.
Minimal or Persistent Erythema:
MAINTAIN current dose (No Increase)
Painful Erythema (Overdose):
STOP, reduce last dose by 50%, restart with 10% increases
Cumulative Risk Check:
Risk of SCC increases with total sessions.
Threshold of concern: >150 treatments.
Patient Clear?
No (Continue Loop)
Yes (Exit Loop)
Optional Maintenance Phase
Note: Text questions effectiveness.
END PROTOCOL

Short-Term Side Effects

  • Gastrointestinal (Common): Nausea (30%), Vomiting (10%)
  • Cutaneous: burn, Pruritus, tingling, "PUVA skin pain"
  • Systemic (from overdose): Fever, malaise

Long-Term Hazards

  • Photocarcinogenesis: High risk of SCC (dose-dependent). Possible Melanoma risk.
  • Photoaging: Wrinkles, xerosis, "PUVA lentiginosis" (dark spots).
  • Ophthalmologic: Theoretical cataract risk (mandates UVA-opaque eye protection).

Post-Care for Oral PUVA (Systemic Photosensitivity)

  • Mandatory Eye Protection: UVA-opaque glasses until the evening of the treatment day.
  • Skin Protection: Implicitly avoid additional, unprotected sun exposure for the remainder of the day.

Post-Care for Bath PUVA (Localized Cutaneous Photosensitivity)

  • photosensitivity lasts no more than 2 hours.
  • Implication for Post-Care: Only need to be cautious about sun exposure for a couple of hours immediately following treatment.
