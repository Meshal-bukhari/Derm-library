GRANULOMATOSIS WITH POLYANGIITIS (GPA)

Clinical Features - Board Review

CLASSIC TRIAD - FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT
System Pathologic Feature Clinical Significance
1. RESPIRATORY TRACT
(Upper & Lower)		 Necrotizing Granulomatous Inflammation Most characteristic feature
Affects up to 90% of patients
2. KIDNEYS Pauci-immune Glomerulonephritis Present in ~20% at presentation
Develops in ~75% eventually
3. SYSTEMIC VASCULITIS Small to Medium-sized Vessels Can involve skin and oral mucosa
Systemic necrotizing vasculitis
CLINICAL FEATURES BY SYSTEM
System Clinical Manifestations Key Features & Frequencies
CONSTITUTIONAL
SYMPTOMS		 • Fever
• Malaise
• Weight loss
• Arthralgias
• Myalgias 		• Nonspecific presenting symptoms
• Common initial manifestation
• Often precede organ-specific symptoms
UPPER RESPIRATORY
TRACT
("The Hallmark")		 Symptoms/Signs:
• Recurrent epistaxis (nosebleeds)
• Bloody nasal discharge
• Nasal crusting
• Sino-nasal congestion

Destructive Changes:
• Mucosal ulcerations
• Nasal septal perforation
• Saddle nose deformity

Other Head/Neck:
• Subglottic stenosis
• Hearing loss (conductive/sensorineural) 		• Presenting complaint in >70% of patients
• Most characteristic feature
• Progressive destructive process

• Pathognomonic findings
• Can cause significant disfigurement
• May require surgical intervention

• Can be life-threatening (airway)
• May be reversible with treatment
LOWER RESPIRATORY
TRACT (LUNGS)		 Symptoms:
• Dyspnea
• Cough
• Hemoptysis
• Pleuritis

Imaging Findings:
• Irregular infiltrates
• Nodules
• Mass lesions
• Cavitation

Severe Complication:
• Alveolar hemorrhage 		• Can be presenting symptom
• May be life-threatening
• Coughing up blood
• Chest pain

• Chest X-ray findings
• Multiple patterns possible
• CT more sensitive
• Cavitation is characteristic

• Life-threatening emergency
• Requires immediate treatment
RENAL
INVOLVEMENT		 Pathology:
• Pauci-immune
• Crescentic
• Necrotizing glomerulonephritis

Laboratory Findings:
• Proteinuria
• Hematuria
• RBC casts
• Progressive renal failure 		• Major cause of morbidity
• Present in ~20% at presentation
• Develops in ~75% eventually

• Active urine sediment
• Can progress rapidly
• May require dialysis
• Can be reversible with treatment
CUTANEOUS &
ORAL		 Skin Lesions:
• Petechiae and purpuric macules/papules
• Deep ulcerations (resemble pyoderma gangrenosum)
• Papulonecrotic lesions ("Churg-Strauss Nodules")
• Subcutaneous nodules
• Digital infarcts

Oral Lesion:
• "Strawberry Gums" 		• Presenting feature in one-third of patients
• May be painful
• Can be deep and destructive
• Common on extensor surfaces, especially elbows
• Histology = PNGD (Palisaded Neutrophilic and Granulomatous Dermatitis)
• May be multiple
• Small clots causing tissue death

• HIGH-YIELD BUZZWORD
• Friable, papular, hemorrhagic gingivae

• Mononeuritis multiplex

Musculoskeletal:

Other:

• Cardiac 		• 30-60% of patients

• 20-50% of patients

LABORATORY FINDINGS
Test Category Specific Findings Clinical Significance
ANCA SEROLOGY
("Defining Lab Test")		 Pattern:
• c-ANCA (cytoplasmic pattern)

Target Antigen:
• Proteinase 3 (PR3) 		• Most frequently positive pattern in GPA
• Overwhelmingly the target antigen
• Essential for diagnosis
• May correlate with disease activity
• Can be negative in limited disease
GENERAL
INFLAMMATORY
MARKERS		 • Elevated ESR
• Elevated C-reactive protein
• Anemia
• Leukocytosis 		• Nonspecific inflammatory markers
• Usually elevated during active disease
• Anemia of chronic disease
• Reflects systemic inflammation
HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM PEARLS
Classic Triad Upper/Lower Respiratory + Kidneys + Systemic Vasculitis
Pathognomonic Oral Finding "Strawberry Gums" = friable, papular, hemorrhagic gingivae
Most Common Presenting Site Upper respiratory tract (>70%)
Characteristic Imaging Finding Pulmonary cavitation on chest imaging
Defining Laboratory Test c-ANCA/PR3 positive
Skin Lesion Location Papulonecrotic lesions on extensor surfaces, especially elbows
Renal Progression Present in ~20% initially → ~75% eventually
Skin Histology PNGD (Palisaded Neutrophilic and Granulomatous Dermatitis)
