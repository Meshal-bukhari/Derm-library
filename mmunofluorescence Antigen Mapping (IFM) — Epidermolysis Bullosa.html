Immunofluorescence Antigen Mapping (IFM) — Epidermolysis Bullosa

Rapid MCQ Review - Fig. 32.14 (pg. 9) - Kindler (pg. 12)
Roof (epidermal side) Floor (dermal side)

Quick Summary Box

Purpose

Map the precise level of skin cleavage in a blister by locating known BMZ proteins.

Principle

Fluorescent monoclonal antibodies bind BMZ proteins (e.g., collagen VII, laminin 332). Fluorescence on the roof or floor reveals the split plane.

Key Diagnostic Use
  • EBS BMZ antigens on floor only.
  • JEB BMZ antigens split between roof & floor.
  • DEB BMZ antigens on roof only.
Additional Utility

Absent/markedly reduced expression pinpoints recessive forms (e.g., absent laminin 332 in severe JEB).

High-Yield Exam Facts

  • Best test requires biopsy of a freshly induced blister, not an old or spontaneous blister.
  • "Roof vs. Floor" is the key pattern recognition for EBS/JEB/DEB.
  • Image cues:
    • Green line on floor only → EBS
    • Green line on roof only → DEB
    • Green lines split roof & floor → JEB
  • Absent staining indicates severe recessive disease:
    • Absent laminin 332 → severe JEB (Herlitz), watch for exuberant granulation tissue.
    • Absent collagen VII → severe RDEB (Hallopeau–Siemens).

Definition & Technique

IFM is performed on a biopsy from the edge of a freshly induced blister to localize BMZ antigens and infer the plane of cleavage.

Inducing a Blister

  • Site: Intact, non-acral skin (e.g., inner arm).
  • Method: Firm pressure with a pencil eraser, applied and rotated to create a mechanical split.

Biopsy & Transport

  • Biopsy: 3–4 mm punch at the blister edge.
  • Medium: Place in Michel’s/Zeus medium to preserve antigens.
Panel: Stain sections with fluorescently labeled monoclonal antibodies to key BMZ proteins (e.g., collagen IV, VII, XVII; laminin 332).
Reference: Fig. 32.14, pg. 9.

Interpretation — Plane of Cleavage

Ask: Where are the BMZ antigens relative to the split?

EB Type Plane of Cleavage BMZ Antigen Location Rationale
EBS Intra-epidermal (basal cell) Floor only Split is above BMZ in keratinocytes; entire BMZ remains attached to dermis.
JEB Lamina lucida Split between roof and floor Within BM itself: proteins above split (e.g., collagen XVII) on roof; below (e.g., collagen VII, collagen IV) on floor.
DEB Sublamina densa Roof only Entire BMZ including lamina densa lifts with epidermis; split is below BMZ in dermis.
Kindler Mixed planes Variable patterns BMZ reduplication with multiple potential cleavage planes.

Interpretation — Protein Expression

Absent/Reduced (Recessive)

  • Absent laminin 332 → severe JEB (Herlitz).
  • Absent collagen VII → severe RDEB (Hallopeau–Siemens).
  • Absent plectin → EBS with muscular dystrophy.
  • Absent α6β4 integrin → JEB with pyloric atresia.

Normal Intensity (Dominant)

  • DDEB: Collagen VII present at BMZ but defective.
  • Dominant EBS: Keratins 5/14 staining usually normal.
Abnormal Distribution

Self-improving DDEB (active phase): Collagen VII shows granular, intracellular staining in basal keratinocytes with reduced/absent BMZ staining, indicating a transport defect (pg. 9).

Diagnostic Algorithm

IFM is used alongside TEM and followed by genetic testing for confirmation.

  1. Primary screening panel (e.g., laminin γ2 chain, collagen IV, VII, XVII) to determine cleavage plane.
  2. Pattern recognition for EBS vs JEB vs DEB based on roof/floor distribution.
  3. Secondary targeted panel to pinpoint specific protein deficiency.
  4. Correlate with clinical phenotype; proceed to genetic analysis for definitive typing.
Tip: Always verify the specimen is from a freshly induced blister to avoid misleading staining.

Image-to-Answer Cues

  • Single green BMZ line on floor → EBS
  • Single green BMZ line on roof → DEB
  • Two green lines (roof and floor) → JEB
  • Report says “absent laminin 332” → severe JEB (Herlitz); think exuberant granulation tissue.

Specimen Essentials

  • Site selection: intact, non-acral skin (inner arm preferred).
  • Induction: pencil eraser pressure + rotation.
  • Biopsy: 3–4 mm punch at blister edge.
  • Transport: Michel’s/Zeus medium.
