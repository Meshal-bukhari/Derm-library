Quick Summary: Bloom Syndrome

  • Classic Triad: Photosensitivity (malar erythema), short stature, immunodeficiency → recurrent infections.
  • Hallmark Cutaneous Finding: “Congenital telangiectatic erythema” – malar erythema + telangiectasias in first weeks of life.
  • Systemic Red Flags: Growth delay, recurrent ear/lung infections, very high malignancy risk (lymphoma, leukemia, carcinomas).
  • Key Differentiator: Patients have normal intelligence.
  • Pathognomonic Lab Test: Chromosomal instability → ↑ sister chromatid exchanges & quadriradial configurations.

1. Definition & Classification

Rare autosomal recessive disorder characterized by chromosomal instability. Also called Congenital telangiectatic erythema.

2. Epidemiology

  • Rare: only 265 cases in Bloom Syndrome Registry.
  • Originally described in Ashkenazi Jews (carrier 1:100), but 75% of cases are non-Jewish.

3. Pathophysiology

BLM gene mutation → defective RECQL3 helicase → genomic instability → ↑ chromosomal breakage → high cancer susceptibility.

4. Clinical Features

Cutaneous:

  • Malar erythema + telangiectasias (butterfly rash) in infancy.
  • Also affects dorsal hands/forearms.
  • Café-au-lait spots + hypopigmented patches.
  • Occasional lip blisters.

Systemic:

  • Growth: Short stature, small head.
  • Immunology: Recurrent ear & lung infections.
  • Craniofacial: Long face, malar hypoplasia, prominent nose.
  • Malignancy: Lymphoma, leukemia, GI carcinomas (50% of patients).
  • Endocrine/Reproductive: Sterility (males), ↓ fertility (females), diabetes (less common).
  • Neurology: Normal intelligence (key differentiator).

5. Investigations

  • Immunology: ↓ IgA, IgM (± IgG).
  • Cytogenetics (diagnostic): ↑ sister chromatid exchanges & quadriradial configurations.
  • Genetic Testing: BLM sequencing confirms mutation.

6. Management & Prognosis

  • Management: Symptomatic care, strict photoprotection, cancer surveillance, treat infections.
  • Prognosis: Poor – avg lifespan ~26 years (malignancy is leading cause of death).
