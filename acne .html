PATHOGENESIS - The Fatal Four

🎯 "FISH" - The 4 Factors of Acne
Follicular hyperkeratinization
Inflammation
Sebum (hormonal influences)
Home of C. acnes (bacterial colonization)

1. Follicular Hyperkeratinization

What starts it all?

Microcomedo formation
• ↑ Keratinocyte proliferation
• ↑ Corneocyte cohesiveness
• IL-1α involved
• Creates the "bottleneck"

2. Hormonal/Sebum

The grease factory

DHT is the villain!
• 5-10x more potent than testosterone
• Sebaceous glands have AR
• Adrenarche (7-8 yrs) = start
• DHEAS rises → acne begins

3. Inflammation

The immune response

Early & Late Players
• CD4+ T cells (early)
• TLR2 activation
• IL-1β, IL-8, TNF release
• Neutrophils → pustules

4. C. acnes

The bacterial player

Not just a pathogen!
• Gram-positive anaerobe
• Makes porphyrins (Wood's lamp+)
• Ribotypes 4 & 5 = acne-associated
• Triggers TLR2 → inflammation

Inflammation occurs BEFORE comedo rupture! CD4+ T cells and IL-1 are increased perifollicularly in early lesions.

CLINICAL FEATURES - Pattern Recognition

HIGH YIELD

Lesion Types - From Mild to Wild

  1. Comedones (non-inflammatory)
    • Closed (whiteheads) - small, skin-colored, no opening
    • Open (blackheads) - dilated opening, oxidized lipids/melanin
  2. Inflammatory lesions
    • Papules - 1-5mm erythematous
    • Pustules - white purulent material
    • Nodules - deep, inflamed, tender
    • Pseudocysts - pus + serosanguineous fluid

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS - Pattern Recognition

Think in 2 Categories:
1. COMEDONAL mimics (closed vs open)
2. INFLAMMATORY mimics (papulopustular)

🔵 Closed Comedone Mimics

  • Milia - white, hard, superficial
  • Syringomas - periorbital preference
  • Trichoepitheliomas - nasolabial folds
  • Eruptive vellus hair cysts - chest/trunk
  • Steatocystoma multiplex - yellowish, inherited

⚫ Open Comedone Mimics

  • Favre-Racouchot - lateral malar, elderly
  • Nevus comedonicus - linear array
  • Trichostasis spinulosa - multiple hairs/follicle
  • Dilated pore of Winer - single large

🔴 Inflammatory Mimics

  • Rosacea - no comedones, flushing, telangiectasias
  • Perioral dermatitis - spares vermillion
  • Folliculitis - monomorphous, no comedones
  • Pseudofolliculitis barbae - shaving areas
  • Demodicosis - adult > children
Gram-negative folliculitis = upper lip + cheeks in patient on prolonged antibiotics for acne!
Solid facial edema (Morbihan disease) - woody induration of mid-face. Treatment: Extended isotretinoin (9-24 months) ± ketotifen

TREATMENT - The Board Algorithm

🎯 Treatment Ladder: "MATCH"
Mild → Topical retinoid + BPO
Add oral antibiotics for moderate
Try hormonal therapy (females)
Change to isotretinoin for severe
High-dose prednisone for fulminans
TOPICAL ARSENAL
Drug Class Examples Key Points Board Pearl
Retinoids Tretinoin, Adapalene, Tazarotene, Trifarotene Normalize keratinization, comedolytic Adapalene = OTC, photostable, can mix with BPO
Antimicrobials BPO, Clindamycin, Erythromycin BPO = no resistance Never use topical antibiotics alone!
Others Azelaic acid, Dapsone, Clascoterone Alternative options Azelaic acid helps PIH; Dapsone + BPO = orange staining

SYSTEMIC THERAPY

🔷 Oral Antibiotics

First-line: Doxycycline, Minocycline

Duration: 3-4 months MAX

Always combine with: Topical retinoid + BPO

Subantimicrobial doxy (40mg) = same efficacy, fewer side effects!

💊 Hormonal (Females)

OCPs: 3 FDA-approved

  • Ortho Tri-Cyclen (norgestimate)
  • Estrostep (norethindrone)
  • Yaz (drospirenone)

Spironolactone: 50-100mg BID

Drospirenone = ↑K+ (like 25mg spiro)

⭐ Isotretinoin

Dose: 0.5-1 mg/kg/day

Cumulative: 120-150 mg/kg

Duration: ~5-6 months

Start LOW (0.5mg/kg) to prevent flares, especially in teenage boys!

TREATMENT OF ACNE IN PREGNANCY

  • Azithromycin: 500 mg three times per week for 8 or 12 weeks
  • Oral Erythromycin: Dose: Erythromycin (base) 500 mg twice daily
  • Topical Erythromycin:
    • 2% gel, solution, pledget
    • Applied once or twice daily
  • Topical Clindamycin:
    • Preparations & Dose: % gel, lotion, pledget, solution, foam
    • Applied once or twice daily for most forms; foam is once daily
  • Topical Azelaic Acid:
    • Preparations & Dose: 20% cream, 15% gel, 15% foam
    • Applied twice daily

ISOTRETINOIN BOARD FACTS

iPLEDGE Requirements:

  • 2 negative pregnancy tests before starting
  • Monthly pregnancy tests
  • 2 forms of contraception
  • Wait 1 month after stopping before pregnancy

Side Effects to Know:

  • Most common: Cheilitis, xerosis
  • Labs: ↑TG/cholesterol (20-50%)
  • Muscles: ↑CK in athletes
  • NO proven link to depression/IBD!

SPECIAL SITUATIONS

🤰 Pregnancy

Safe:

  • Azelaic acid
  • Topical clindamycin/erythromycin
  • Oral azithromycin, cephalexin

Avoid: All retinoids, tetracyclines

🧬 Genetic Syndromes

  • Apert: severe widespread nodulocystic acne, acrocephaly (Craniosynostosis), midface hypoplasia, syndactyly, FGFR2 gene mutation, chronic hyperhidrosis
  • PAPA: Pyogenic arthritis + acne conglobata
  • SAPHO: Synovitis, Acne, Pustulosis, Hyperostosis, Osteitis
    • Most common site: Sternoclavicular area
    • "Bull's head" sign on X-ray
    • Tx: Bisphosphonates, TNF-α inhibitors

🔬 Endocrine Workup

When to test: Hirsutism, irregular menses

Tests:

  • Free/total testosterone
  • DHEAS
  • 17-hydroxyprogesterone

DHEAS >8000 = adrenal tumor!

🏆 FINAL BOARD PEARLS - The Must-Knows

1. Microcomedo = precursor of ALL acne lesions
2. DHT > testosterone (5-10x more potent)
3. C. acnes density ≠ severity
4. Wood's lamp + = porphyrins from C. acnes
5. Adapalene = only OTC retinoid, photostable
6. BPO = NO bacterial resistance ever
7. Oral antibiotics: 3-4 months MAX
8. Isotretinoin: 120-150mg/kg cumulative
9. Acne fulminans: Prednisone FIRST
10. Mid-childhood acne = investigate hormones
11. Gram-neg folliculitis = upper lip location
12. PAPA syndrome = CD2BP1/PSTPIP1 mutation
