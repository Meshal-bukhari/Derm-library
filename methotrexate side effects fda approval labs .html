METHOTREXATE TOXICITY BY SYSTEM
System Adverse Effects Clinical Significance
GASTROINTESTINAL
& HEPATIC
("Daily Grind")		 Most Common:
• Nausea & Anorexia
• Elevated Transaminases

Less Frequent:
• Diarrhea & Vomiting
• Ulcerative Stomatitis

Most Severe:
• Hepatotoxicity (Fibrosis/Cirrhosis) 		• Most common category, frequent dose limitation
• Transaminases often transient, RR = 2.19
• Normal LFTs don't exclude fibrosis

• Stomatitis = STOP MTX immediately

• Risk ↑↑ with: obesity, diabetes, alcohol, NASH
• MTX-induced cirrhosis often "nonaggressive" & reversible
HEMATOLOGIC
("Most Acutely Dangerous")		 Common:
• Macrocytic Indices

LIFE-THREATENING:
• PANCYTOPENIA
• Bone Marrow Suppression 		• Without anemia = usually not significant

BOXED WARNING - Greatest mortality risk
Risk factors:
• Renal insufficiency (MOST IMPORTANT)
• TMP-SMX or NSAIDs
• No folic acid supplementation
• Inadvertent daily dosing
Rescue: Leucovorin (folinic acid)

MONITORING REQUIREMENTS
Phase Tests Required Frequency
BASELINE
(Before first dose)		 Laboratory:
• CBC with platelets
• LFTs (especially transaminases)
• Renal function (BUN, creatinine)

Infectious Disease Screening:
• Hepatitis B & C serologies
• TB testing (QuantiFERON/T-spot)
• HIV testing (if at risk)

Clinical:
• History & Physical 		• One time before initiation

KEY POINT: For patients >50 years:
Creatinine alone unreliable → estimate creatinine clearance

• Risk stratification for concomitant medications
INITIAL PHASE • CBC & LFTs • 5-6 days after initial test dose
• Every 1-2 weeks × first 2-4 weeks
• After each dose escalation
MAINTENANCE
PHASE		 • CBC & LFTs
• Renal function 		• Gradually ↓ to every 3-4 months
• Once or twice yearly
LIVER BIOPSY GUIDELINES
Clinical Scenario Recommendation Rationale
BASELINE BIOPSY
(Pre-treatment)		 Generally NOT necessary for low-risk patients

Strongly consider in high-risk:
• Personal/family history liver disease
• Hepatotoxin exposure (alcohol, IV drugs)
• Diabetes, obesity, hyperlipidemia
• Abnormal baseline LFTs
• Positive hepatitis serologies 		• Low-risk patients can start safely

CRUCIAL: For high-risk patients, "strongly consider avoiding MTX altogether"

• NASH risk factors
• Pre-existing liver compromise
DURING THERAPY
BIOPSIES		 Old Guideline:
• Initial biopsy after 1.5 g cumulative

2009 NPF Consensus:
• First biopsy delayed until 3.5-4.5 g cumulative (low-risk)

Subsequent:
• After each additional 1.5 g 		• More conservative older approach

• Updated guidelines for low-risk patients

Quick Calculation:
12 ÷ (# of 2.5mg tablets/week) = years between 1.5g intervals
MODERN APPROACH
Non-invasive		 Transient Elastography
(Fibroscan™)

Other Options:
• PIIINP (less reliable in PsA)
• NASH FibroSure 		• "Primary screening test" at many institutions
• Monitor liver stiffness
• Start after 1 year therapy
• Repeat yearly
• Significantly ↓ need for biopsies
HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM PEARLS
BOXED WARNINGS 1. Pancytopenia/Bone Marrow Suppression
2. Acute Pneumonitis
3. Lymphoma/LPDs
4. Teratogenicity (Pregnancy Category X)
Most Important Risk Factor Impaired renal function = single most important risk factor for pancytopenia
Immediate Stop Signals • Ulcerative stomatitis = STOP MTX immediately
• Signs of pancytopenia or pneumonitis
Key Drug Interactions TMP-SMX + NSAIDs = ↑↑ pancytopenia risk
Rescue Agent Leucovorin (folinic acid) bypasses DHFR block
