🇸🇦 Saudi Psoriasis Guidelines

Biologic Treatment of Chronic Plaque Psoriasis (2020) - Board Exam Review

📋 Overview & Key Concepts

🎯 High-Yield Board Facts

  • Prevalence: ~2% worldwide, lower in Asian/African, up to 11% in Caucasian/Scandinavian
  • Peak ages: 20-30 and 50-69 years
  • 80% mild-moderate: Topicals, vitamin D analogues, phototherapy
  • 20% severe: Requires systemic therapy (acitretin, MTX, biologics)
💡 Memory Trick: "2-80-20 Rule" - 2% prevalence, 80% mild (topicals), 20% severe (systemics)
Clinical Pearl: Psoriasis is a T-cell mediated systemic inflammatory disease affecting skin, nails, scalp, joints with cardiovascular, metabolic, IBD, and psychological comorbidities.

🎯 Biologic Pathways (Board Favorite!)

IL-23/Th17 Axis

Primary pathway in psoriatic plaque development and chronicity

TNF-α Signaling

Secondary inflammatory pathway targeted by biologics

Biologics are different from conventional systemics because they target SPECIFIC inflammatory pathways, not general immunosuppression.

📚 Guidelines Development

These 2020 Saudi guidelines were developed by:

  • 8 dermatologists + 5 pharmacists
  • Based on 6 international guidelines (European S3, BAD, AAD/NPF, NICE, French)
  • Delphi consensus methodology (≥75% agreement required)
Section Summary: Psoriasis affects 2% globally, peaks at 20-30 and 50-69 years. 80% mild (topicals), 20% severe (systemics). Biologics target specific pathways (IL-23/Th17, TNF-α). Saudi guidelines based on international consensus.

✅ Eligibility & Treatment Goals

🎯 Who Gets Biologics? (Memorize This!)

Prescribers: Dermatologists with extensive systemic therapy experience only

📊 Moderate-to-Severe Disease Criteria

≥10% BSA

Body Surface Area involvement

≥10 PASI

Psoriasis Area & Severity Index

≥10 DLQI

Dermatology Life Quality Index

💡 "Rule of 10s": BSA ≥10%, PASI ≥10, DLQI ≥10

🎯 Special Circumstances (Limited Disease)

Even with limited disease, consider biologics if:
  • Large scalp areas
  • Visible areas (hands, face)
  • Intertriginous areas and genitalia
  • Palms and/or soles
  • Treatment-resistant areas
  • Psoriatic arthritis present

🎯 Treatment Goals (Board Loves This!)

Choose ONE of these treatment goals:
  • Absolute PASI ≤3
  • PASI 90 response
  • PGA score 0-1
  • DLQI <5

📈 Treatment Goal Evaluation Timeline

Weeks 12-16: Evaluate treatment goals
PGA 0-1 or PASI ≤3 or ΔPASI ≥90: Continue treatment
PGA 2 or PASI 3-6 or ΔPASI 75-89: Evaluate nails, face, scalp, palms/soles, genitalia, joints
PGA 3-5 or PASI >6 or ΔPASI <75: Modify treatment regimen
Assessment tools (PASI, PGA, BSA, DLQI) should be used in daily practice to establish and monitor treatment goals.
Section Summary: Biologics for moderate-severe disease (Rule of 10s: BSA≥10%, PASI≥10, DLQI≥10) or limited disease with functional impairment. Goals: PASI≤3, PASI90, PGA 0-1, or DLQI<5. Evaluate at 12-16 weeks.

🔍 Screening & Monitoring

⚠️ Critical Safety Point: TB is common in Saudi Arabia - most cases are LTBI reactivation!

🦠 TB Screening (High-Yield!)

TB Screening Protocol

  • Baseline: TST or IGRA-QuantiFERON + Chest X-ray
  • Annual screening: Medical history + TST or IGRA
  • Post-biologic: Maintain suspicion for 6 months after discontinuation
IGRA vs TST: IGRA is highly specific/sensitive, not affected by prior BCG vaccination. Either can be used for LTBI testing.

🦠 Hepatitis B & C Screening

Required Tests

  • Anti-HBc (IgM/IgG)
  • Anti-HBsAg
  • HBsAg
  • Anti-HCV

If positive: Collaborate with hepatologist before and during therapy

💉 Vaccination Guidelines

LIVE VACCINES CONTRAINDICATED: BCG, measles, mumps, rubella, yellow fever, oral polio, oral typhoid, varicella zoster, nasal influenza
SAFE VACCINES: Inactivated influenza (yearly), pneumococcal, hepatitis A & B
Live vaccines contraindicated in infants up to 6 months if mother received biologics beyond 16 weeks gestation.
Hajj Preparation: Patients planning Hajj should be up to date on all routine vaccines per MOH requirements.

🦠 Infection Management

Mild infections: Continue biologic therapy
Severe infections: Temporary interruption of biologic
After treatment: Reintroduce biologic after sufficient anti-infective therapy
Section Summary: TB screening crucial (TST/IGRA + CXR). Monitor CBC/RFT/LFT every 6 months. Live vaccines contraindicated. Pause biologics for severe infections only.

📊 Safety Comparison

Key Safety Points:
  • Infections: All biologics increase infection risk, but severe infections are rare
  • Malignancy: Monitor for NMSC and other malignancies
  • MACE: Major adverse cardiac events possible
  • IBD: Some drugs may worsen, others may improve

🏥 Apremilast (Non-biologic option)

Apremilast (PDE4 inhibitor)

  • PASI 75: 33.1% at 16 weeks
  • Advantages: Oral, no infection risk, safe in malignancy
  • Indications: Select patients, palmoplantar/scalp/nail psoriasis
  • MOH status: Non-formulary
Section Summary: IL-23 inhibitors most effective (PASI 90: 70-75%), followed by IL-17 (55-65%), then TNF inhibitors (21-57%). Weight-based dosing for infliximab/ustekinumab only.

🔄 Treatment Algorithm

🎯 First-Line: Conventional Systemics

Try first (if appropriate): Methotrexate, Cyclosporine, Acitretin

Duration: 12-16 weeks before switching to biologics

📊 Criteria for Systemic/Biologic Therapy

Any ONE of the following:
  • DLQI ≥10, PASI ≥10, or BSA ≥10%
  • Severe localized psoriasis with functional impairment (nails, face, scalp, palms, soles, genitalia)
  • Presence of psoriatic arthritis

🥇 Second-Line: Biologic Selection

🟢 PREFERRED: IL-23 Antagonists (PSO only)
  • Guselkumab (Tremfya)
  • Risankizumab (Skyrizi)
💡 For PSO only: IL-23 inhibitors are first choice
🔵 PREFERRED: IL-17 Antagonists (PSO + PSA)
  • Secukinumab (Cosentyx)
  • Ixekizumab (Taltz)
💡 For PSO + PSA: IL-17 inhibitors are first choice

🥉 Third-Line Options

After 16-24 weeks of suboptimal control:
  • 16 weeks for skin disease
  • 24 weeks for joint disease
Switch to:
  • IL-17 antagonists
  • TNF antagonists
  • IL-12/23 antagonist (for peripheral arthritis only)
After 28 weeks of suboptimal control with IL-23 inhibitors, consider dose optimization at physician discretion.

🔄 Transitioning Between Therapies

⚠️ Key Principle: No evidence-based studies exist for transition intervals - use clinical judgment!

📋 Conventional to Biologic Transition

From To Washout Period Special Notes
Cyclosporine TNF inhibitors/Ustekinumab No washout needed Can overlap 2-8 weeks, taper CSA ASAP
Methotrexate TNF inhibitors/Ustekinumab No washout needed Can overlap or use concurrently

🔄 Biologic to Biologic Transition

General Rule: Try dose optimization before switching biologics
From Timing Dose Optimization Options
Adalimumab At next scheduled dose (2 weeks) 40mg EOW → 40mg weekly
Etanercept At next scheduled dose (1 week) 50mg weekly → 50mg BIW
Infliximab 2-4 weeks after last dose Q8w → Q6w; 5mg/kg → up to 10mg/kg
Ustekinumab At next scheduled dose 45mg → 90mg; Q12w → Q8w
Secukinumab At next scheduled dose Q4w → Q2w
💡 Transition Memory: "Wait longer for longer half-lives" - Infliximab needs 2-4 weeks, others can switch immediately

📉 Dose Reduction & Discontinuation

⚠️ Generally NOT Recommended: Discontinuation due to recurrence risk and failure to recapture response
Consider discontinuation if:
  • Patient preference
  • Clear/almost clear for minimum 1 year
  • History of disease-free intervals
  • Absence of PsA
  • Low QoL impact
  • No worsening after previous dose reductions
Up to 20% of patients fail to regain PASI 75 response after re-initiation of the same biologic monotherapy.
Section Summary: First-line: conventional systemics 12-16 weeks. Second-line: IL-23 (PSO only) or IL-17 (PSO+PSA). Try dose optimization before switching. Discontinuation generally not recommended.

👥 Special Patient Populations

🎯 Board-Critical Special Populations

Choosing the right therapy based on patient characteristics and comorbidities is frequently tested!

🤰 Pregnancy & Lactation

Pregnancy Effect on Psoriasis: 55% improve, 23% worsen during pregnancy
⚠️ General Rule: Avoid all medications unless benefits outweigh risks. Discontinue biologics before pregnancy or at 16-26 weeks.
Drug Class Pregnancy Lactation Male Conception Key Points
TNF-α Inhibitors ⚠️ Higher risk in 3rd trimester ✅ Safe ✅ Safe Transplacental transfer concern
Certolizumab ✅ Most Preferred ✅ Safe ✅ Safe Minimal placental transfer
Etanercept ⚠️ Alternative to certolizumab ✅ Safe ✅ Safe If certolizumab unavailable
IL-12/23 Inhibitors ❓ Uncertain ❓ Uncertain ✅ Acceptable Limited data
IL-17 Inhibitors ❓ Uncertain ❓ Unknown ✅ Acceptable No human pregnancy studies
IL-23 Inhibitors ❓ Unknown ❓ Unknown ✅ Acceptable Antibodies secreted in milk
💡 Pregnancy Memory: "Certolizumab = Clearly best choice" (minimal placental transfer)
Infants born to mothers who received biologics beyond 16 weeks gestation should NOT receive live vaccines until 6 months of age.

⚖️ Metabolic Syndrome & Obesity

Key Insight: Metabolic syndrome prevalence increases with increased BSA affected by psoriasis
Weight-Based Dosing Required:
  • Infliximab: 5mg/kg (can increase to 10mg/kg)
  • Ustekinumab: <100kg = 45mg, ≥100kg = 90mg

🎗️ Malignancy

⚠️ Avoid all biologics in:
  • Concurrent malignancy
  • History of malignancy
  • Discuss individually if cancer in previous 5 years
Infliximab showed more malignancies in COPD patients with heavy smoking history - exercise caution in high-risk patients.

🏥 Surgery

Elective Major Surgery: Discontinue 4-5 half-lives before surgery
Practical Approach:
  • Discontinue for mouth/GI surgery
  • Continue for clean surgeries

🦠 Hepatitis

⚠️ HBV: Do NOT initiate biologics in active hepatitis B. Risk of severe reactivation.
HCV: Lower risk than HBV. Successful treatment reports with adalimumab and etanercept.

🫁 Tuberculosis

TB Risk by Drug Class

  • Highest Risk: TNF inhibitors (especially monoclonal antibodies > etanercept)
  • Moderate Risk: Ustekinumab
  • Lowest Risk: IL-17 and IL-23 inhibitors (no reported cases)

LTBI Treatment Protocol

LTBI Treatment: INH 9 months OR Rifampin 4 months
Timing: Complete 1 month of LTBI treatment before starting biologic

🧠 Demyelinating Diseases/MS

Drug Class MS Recommendation Notes
TNF-α Inhibitors ❌ Contraindicated Do not use; review alternatives if 1st degree relative has MS
IL-12/23 Inhibitors ✅ May be used Does not improve or worsen MS
IL-17 Inhibitors ✅ Can be used Some benefit in MS symptoms
IL-23 Inhibitors ✅ Likely safe Limited data, no MS worsening reports

❤️ Cardiovascular Disease & CHF

CVD: TNF-α inhibitors are PREFERRED for patients with cardiovascular risk factors
⚠️ CHF (NYHA Class III-IV): Avoid TNF-α inhibitors. IL-12/23, IL-17, IL-23 appear safe.

🠠 Inflammatory Bowel Disease

Drug Class IBD Recommendation Specific Notes
Adalimumab, Infliximab, Certolizumab ✅ Beneficial FDA-approved for IBD treatment
Etanercept ⚠️ Less effective Not as effective for Crohn's
IL-12/23 (Ustekinumab) ✅ Approved Crohn's disease only, not UC
IL-17 Inhibitors ⚠️ Caution Consult GI specialist; avoid if 1st degree relative has IBD
IL-23 Inhibitors ✅ Promising Preliminary positive results

🦋 Lupus Erythematosus

⚠️ TNF-α Inhibitors: Risk of de novo lupus or lupus flare (Anti-TNF Induced Lupus - ATIL)
✅ IL-12/23 Inhibitors: SAFEST option - may improve SLE symptoms (oral ulcers, anemia, thrombocytopenia, arthritis)
💡 Lupus Memory: "IL-12/23 = Lupus Love" (improves lupus symptoms)
Section Summary: Certolizumab best for pregnancy. Only 3 biologics for pediatrics (4-4-6 rule). Avoid all biologics in malignancy. TNF inhibitors contraindicated in MS but preferred for CVD. IL-12/23 safest for lupus.

🎯 Final Board Review Summary

Most Tested Concepts:
  • Rule of 10s for eligibility (BSA≥10%, PASI≥10, DLQI≥10)
  • Treatment goals: PASI≤3, PASI90, PGA 0-1, DLQI<5
  • TB screening mandatory (TST/IGRA + CXR)
  • IL-23 inhibitors most effective, best for psoriasis only
  • IL-17 inhibitors best for psoriasis + psoriatic arthritis
  • Certolizumab best choice in pregnancy
  • TNF inhibitors contraindicated in MS, preferred for CVD
  • IL-12/23 safest for lupus patients
