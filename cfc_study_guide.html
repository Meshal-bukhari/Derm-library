  • Prominent forehead

  • Depressed nasal bridge

Differential Diagnosis - RASopathies

CFC shares overlapping features with other RASopathies - know the distinguishing features!
Syndrome Key Distinguishing Features Gene
CFC Syndrome Ulerythema ophryogenes, sparse curly hair, keratosis pilaris BRAF, MEK1, MEK2, KRAS
Noonan Syndrome More frequent easy bleeding and bruising, fewer cutaneous features PTPN11
Costello Syndrome Papillomata (face/perianal), multifocal atrial tachycardia, ulnar deviation HRAS
Noonan with Multiple Lentigines Multiple skin lentigines, frequent sensorineural deafness, cardiac conduction abnormalities PTPN11

Diagnostic Testing

Genetic Testing Protocol
Multigene RAS/MAPK Panel
80% Detection Rate

If panel unavailable: Sequential testing of (1) BRAF, (2) MEK1 and MEK2, (3) KRAS

If gene testing negative → consider high-resolution chromosome microarray
Additional Testing at Diagnosis
  • ECG and electrophysiologic studies
  • Comprehensive cardiac evaluation
  • Neurologic assessment
  • Ophthalmologic examination

🎯 Board Review Questions

Question 1: A 6-month-old infant presents with sparse, curly hair, absent eyebrows, keratosis pilaris, and distinctive facial features including macrocephaly and hypertelorism. Which gene mutation is MOST likely responsible?
  • A) PTPN11
  • B) BRAF
  • C) HRAS
  • D) NF1
Correct Answer: B) BRAF
The combination of sparse, curly, friable hair with ulerythema ophryogenes (keratosis pilaris of eyebrows), distinctive facial features, and cutaneous findings is classic for CFC syndrome. BRAF mutations are the most common cause of CFC (along with MEK1, MEK2). PTPN11 causes Noonan syndrome, HRAS causes Costello syndrome.
Question 2: What is the key distinguishing cutaneous feature that helps differentiate CFC from other RASopathies?
  • A) Café-au-lait macules
  • B) Ulerythema ophryogenes
  • C) Multiple lentigines
  • D) Papillomata
Correct Answer: B) Ulerythema ophryogenes
Ulerythema ophryogenes (keratosis pilaris affecting the eyebrows) is highly characteristic of CFC syndrome and helps distinguish it from other RASopathies. Multiple lentigines are seen in Noonan syndrome with multiple lentigines, and papillomata are characteristic of Costello syndrome.
Question 3: A patient with suspected CFC syndrome needs genetic testing. What is the recommended first-line approach?
  • A) BRAF gene sequencing only
  • B) Multigene RAS/MAPK pathway panel
  • C) Chromosome microarray
  • D) PTPN11 gene sequencing
Correct Answer: B) Multigene RAS/MAPK pathway panel
The multigene RAS/MAPK pathway panel is recommended as first-line testing with approximately 80% mutation detection rate. If panel testing is unavailable, sequential testing of BRAF, then MEK1/MEK2, then KRAS should be performed.
