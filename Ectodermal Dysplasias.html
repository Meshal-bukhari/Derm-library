Ectodermal Dysplasias — Quick Board Review

High-yield summary comparing Hypohidrotic ED (HED) vs. Hidrotic ED (Clouston Syndrome).

Feature Hypohidrotic ED (HED) Hidrotic ED (Clouston)
Sweating Markedly decreased (hypohidrosis) — critical feature Normal
Teeth Hypodontia, conical/peg-shaped teeth Normal
Nails Normal Abnormal — thickened, dystrophic, distal separation; infants: milky white/small
Hair Sparse to absent, fine, lightly pigmented Wiry, brittle, patchy alopecia
Skin Eczema, periorbital hyperpigmentation, sebaceous hyperplasia Palmoplantar keratoderma (stippled/papular/cobblestone)
Facies Characteristic: saddle nose, frontal bossing, full lips Generally normal
Infant Presentation Fever of unknown origin, collodion-like membrane Milky white, small nails
Inheritance X-linked (EDA) Autosomal Dominant (GJB6)

Mnemonics

HED — "NO SWEAT"
N — Normal nails
O — Outstanding facies (frontal bossing, saddle nose, full lips)
S — Sparse hair, skin eczema/hyperpigmentation
W — Water (sweat) absent → hyperthermia in infants
E — Eruption at birth (collodion-like membrane)
A — Abnormal teeth (hypodontia, peg-shaped)
T — Transmission X-linked (EDA), female carriers show mosaic sweating
Clouston — "HANDS ON"
H — Hair abnormal: wiry, brittle, alopecia
A — Autosomal Dominant (GJB6)
N — Nails abnormal: thick, dystrophic, distal separation
D — Dorsal papules & palmoplantar keratoderma (cobblestone)
S — Spared: teeth & sweating are normal

Exam Tips

HED Red Flags
Fever of unknown origin in infant, hypohidrosis, abnormal teeth. Life-threatening hyperthermia if missed.
Clouston Red Flags
Progressive nail dystrophy + palmoplantar keratoderma + wiry hair. Sweating & teeth normal.
Common Confusions
Do not confuse HED with Clouston: nails normal in HED vs abnormal in Clouston. HED = X-linked, Clouston = AD. Skin clues differ: eczema vs keratoderma.
