Local Anesthetics - High Yield Quick Review

Quick Summary Box

  • Prilocaine & Benzocaine: Risk of methemoglobinemia. Prilocaine (EMLA®) contraindicated in G6PD deficiency.
  • Bupivacaine: Higher cardiotoxicity risk than lidocaine. Use caution in heart disease.
  • Cocaine Hydrochloride: Only anesthetic with vasoconstriction. Used intranasally for anesthesia + hemostasis.
  • Lidocaine: Anesthetic of choice in pregnancy (Category B).
  • Epinephrine (additive): Contraindicated in hyperthyroidism, pheochromocytoma, and digital blocks in PAD patients.

Anesthetics with Specific Risks

  • Prilocaine: Methemoglobinemia (dose >500 mg), avoid in G6PD deficiency & infants.
  • Benzocaine: Methemoglobinemia + strong contact sensitizer → allergic contact dermatitis.
  • Bupivacaine: High risk of arrhythmias/cardiotoxicity. Avoid/limit in severe cardiac disease.
  • Epinephrine:
    • Absolute CI: Hyperthyroidism, pheochromocytoma.
    • Relative CI: β-blockers, MAOIs, TCAs, phenothiazines, CV disease, glaucoma, PAD digital blocks.

Anesthetics with Specific Indications

  • Cocaine: Only vasoconstrictor, best for nasal mucosa anesthesia + hemostasis. Caution in heart disease.
  • Lidocaine: Safe in pregnancy, widely used.
  • Articaine: Effective diffusion, useful in painless facial nerve blocks with small volume.
  • Ophthalmic (Proparacaine, Tetracaine): Conjunctival anesthesia. Proparacaine less stinging.
  • Diphenhydramine: Alternative for patients with true allergy to both esters & amides.

Exam Tricks - How They Trap You

  • Cyanotic patient after spray → Think methemoglobinemia (benzocaine/prilocaine), NOT anaphylaxis.
  • Best anesthetic for nasal tip biopsy → Cocaine (not lidocaine jelly).
  • Boy with hemolytic anemia history → Avoid EMLA® (prilocaine → methemoglobinemia in G6PD).
  • Arrhythmia after block → Bupivacaine (cardiotoxic).
  • Hepatitis C/cirrhosis → Avoid amides (liver metabolism) → choose ester.
  • Prolonged paralysis after surgery → Pseudocholinesterase deficiency → avoid esters.
  • Sunscreen allergy (PABA) → Avoid esters (cross-reactivity).

Contraindications

Amide Anesthetics (Lidocaine, Bupivacaine, Prilocaine)

  • Absolute: True IgE-mediated allergy (rare).
  • Relative: Severe liver disease → risk of toxicity (hepatic metabolism).

Ester Anesthetics (Procaine, Tetracaine, Benzocaine)

  • Absolute: Allergy to esters (PABA metabolite) → cross-reactivity.
  • Absolute: Pseudocholinesterase deficiency → risk of systemic toxicity.

Memory Tools

  • Prilocaine / Benzocaine → Purple/Blue blood (Methemoglobinemia).
  • Bupivacaine → Bad for the Beat (Cardiotoxic).
  • Cocaine → Constricts in the Cavity of the nose.
  • Lidocaine → "Ladies in Labor" (Safe in pregnancy).
  • Amide → Abdomen/Liver metabolism → Avoid in advanced liver disease.
  • Ester → Erased by Esterase → Avoid if enzyme deficient. Allergy via PABA.
