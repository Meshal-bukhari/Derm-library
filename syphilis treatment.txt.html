🤰 PREGNANCY & PENICILLIN ALLERGY

🚨 CRITICAL RULE: Penicillin is MANDATORY in pregnancy to prevent congenital syphilis
📊 PENICILLIN ALLERGY ALGORITHM:

Pregnant Patient:
  • MUST use penicillin (only proven fetal therapy)
  • Perform penicillin desensitization
  • Then treat with appropriate penicillin regimen

Non-Pregnant Patient:
  • Neurosyphilis: Strongly consider desensitization (ceftriaxone alternative)
  • Early/Late syphilis: Use doxycycline (14 days vs 28 days)
⚠️ INEFFECTIVE IN PREGNANCY: Erythromycin & azithromycin don't cross placental barrier adequately

👶 CONGENITAL SYPHILIS TREATMENT

🔍 CRITICAL FIRST STEP: CSF analysis should be performed PRIOR to therapy in any suspected case

Scenario 1: Proven/Probable Disease

Criteria for Proven Disease:
  • Physical signs of congenital syphilis
  • RPR/VDRL 4-fold higher than maternal titer
  • Positive CSF-VDRL or abnormal CSF
  • Mother untreated/inadequately treated
Treatment: Aqueous Crystalline Penicillin G
  • Dose: 50,000 U/kg/dose IV
  • Schedule: Q12h × 7 days, then Q8h × 3 days
  • Total Duration: 10 days

Scenario 2: Low-Risk Asymptomatic Neonate

Criteria:
  • Normal physical exam
  • Non-treponemal titer <4-fold maternal titer
  • Mother adequately treated with documented response
Treatment: Benzathine PCN G 50,000 U/kg IM × 1 (prophylactic)

⚡ JARISCH-HERXHEIMER REACTION

Key Facts:
  • Timing: Hours after first antibiotic dose
  • Symptoms: Fever, headache, myalgias
  • Mechanism: Cytokine release from killed spirochetes
  • NOT a drug allergy - continue treatment
  • Management: Supportive care (antipyretics)

🎯 HIGH-YIELD BOARD TRAPS

🪤 TRAP #1: Pregnant Allergy Dilemma
Scenario: Pregnant patient with syphilis + penicillin allergy history
Wrong Answer: Doxycycline (teratogenic) or ceftriaxone
Correct Answer: Desensitization + penicillin
🪤 TRAP #2: "One vs Three Doses"
Scenario: Positive RPR/TPPA on screening, no symptoms, no treatment history
Wrong Answer: Single dose (treating as early)
Correct Answer: Three weekly doses (unknown duration = late latent)
🪤 TRAP #3: Ocular Syphilis Recognition
Scenario: Secondary syphilis patient with blurred vision
Wrong Answer: Single IM benzathine penicillin
Correct Answer: IV penicillin × 10-14 days (ocular = neurosyphilis)
🪤 TRAP #4: Congenital Penicillin Choice
Scenario: Neonate with syphilitic rash and hepatosplenomegaly
Wrong Answer: Single-dose benzathine penicillin (convenient but wrong)
Correct Answer: 10-day IV aqueous penicillin (symptomatic = proven disease)
🪤 TRAP #5: Maternal Treatment History
Scenario: Mother treated with erythromycin, asymptomatic baby
Wrong Answer: Single prophylactic shot
Correct Answer: Full workup + 10-day IV treatment (non-penicillin = inadequate fetal treatment)
🪤 TRAP #6: J-H vs Allergy
Scenario: Fever/chills 6 hours after first penicillin shot
Wrong Answer: Stop penicillin, switch to doxycycline
Correct Answer: Supportive care, continue treatment
