🩺 Drug-Induced Lipoatrophy

Board Review Quick Reference | Dermatology Final Exam

I. INJECTABLE MEDICATIONS
Drug/Agent Mechanism & Type Clinical Details Board Pearls
Insulin Localized, iatrogenic response at injection sites • Non-human insulin > human insulin/analogues
• Increased risk with repeated same-site injection
• Prevention: Regular site rotation		 Can rarely occur at distant sites from injection
Corticosteroids Intralesional/IV administration causing localized fat loss • Very common cause of localized atrophy
• Typically temporary but cosmetically significant
• Multifocal lipoatrophy with IV use described		 Intralesional steroids are very common cause
II. IMMUNOMODULATORS & BIOLOGICS
Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors
(Nivolumab, Pembrolizumab)		 Anti-PD-1 antibodies causing generalized lipoatrophy • GENERALIZED (not localized)
• Develops ≥10 months after initiation
• Associated: DM, insulin resistance, hypertriglyceridemia, central obesity		 Think generalized + metabolic syndrome
Glatiramer Acetate MS treatment, localized at injection sites • Multiple sclerosis treatment
• Localized lipoatrophy at injection sites
• Associated panniculitis		 Panniculitis mimics lupus profundus
Methotrexate Localized lipoatrophy • Localized atrophy pattern
• Specific morphology described		 Can cause semicircular lipoatrophy
Anti-sense Oligonucleotides
(Drisapersen)		 Experimental drug, prolonged subcutaneous use • Severe localized lipodystrophy
• Following prolonged SC administration
• Experimental/investigational agent		 Severe + prolonged SC use pattern
III. ANTIRETROVIRAL THERAPY (ART) - HIV
HIV Lipodystrophy Syndrome Mixed lipoatrophy + lipohypertrophy • Most common non-localized lipodystrophy worldwide
• Lipoatrophy: face, extremities, buttocks
• Lipohypertrophy: central, "buffalo hump"		 Mixed pattern: peripheral loss + central gain
NRTIs
(Stavudine, Didanosine)		 Key contributors to peripheral fat wasting • Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors
• Highest risk: Stavudine, Didanosine
• Older generation agents		 NRTIs = peripheral lipoatrophy
Protease Inhibitors
(Indinavir, Ritonavir)		 Early PIs associated with lipodystrophy • Early generation protease inhibitors
• Indinavir, full-dose Ritonavir
• Part of mixed lipodystrophy syndrome		 Early PIs more problematic than newer agents
INSTIs
(Raltegravir)		 Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitor • Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitors
• Raltegravir specifically mentioned
• Part of lipodystrophy syndrome		 INSTIs can contribute to syndrome
IV. OTHER KEY MEDICATIONS
Benzathine Penicillin Antibiotic causing localized atrophy • Localized lipoatrophy
• Specific morphologic pattern described		 Causes semicircular lipoatrophy
Vaccines DPT, HPV vaccines • Diphtheria-pertussis-tetanus (DPT)
• Quadrivalent HPV vaccine
• Localized injection site reactions		 Common childhood/adolescent vaccines
Other Injectables Various mechanisms • Amikacin (antibiotic)
• Growth Hormone
• Iron Dextran
• Exogenous materials (cosmetic/self-inflicted)		 Consider injection history in any localized lipoatrophy
💡 Quick Memory Tips: Non-human insulin > human | Anti-PD-1 = generalized + ≥10 months | HIV ART = mixed pattern | Site rotation prevents insulin lipoatrophy
