CN VII — Quick Box

Facial Nerve (VII) — Origin, Branches, Danger Zones

Compact, exam-focused summary for surgical and dermatologic procedures.

Motor to facial expression
Stylomastoid exit → 5 terminal branches
Quick Summary Box Study snapshot
Origin & PathExits stylomastoid foramen → posterior auricular branch → enters parotid gland → bifurcates into temporofacial and cervicofacial divisions → five terminal branches emerge between SMAS and deep fascia.
FunctionMotor innervation to muscles of facial expression. Injury → paralysis/paresis of specific regions (eye closure, smile, lower lip, platysma).
Key Danger Zones
  • Temporal branch: crossing zygomatic arch (middle third) — very superficial within superficial temporalis fascia.
  • Marginal mandibular branch: crosses body of mandible anterior to masseter — lies 1–2 cm above inferior mandibular border; very superficial.
Prognosis after injuryBranch‑dependent: temporal (15% cross‑innervation) & marginal mandibular (10%) → poor recovery. Zygomatic & buccal ≈80% anastomoses → better recovery.
Core surgical principleMotor branches run deep to SMAS. Dissect either above SMAS (superficial subcut fat) or in deep subgaleal plane on forehead to avoid temporal branch.

CervicalPlatysma — tenses neck skin. Injury → minimal functional/cosmetic deficit.
Pathway notes (condensed)
  • Stylomastoid foramen ≈ level of earlobe — immediate posterior auricular branch to occipitalis.
  • Inside parotid, relatively protected; superficial during terminal branch exit between SMAS and deep fascia.
  • On forehead, temporal branch lies in superficial temporalis fascia; use deep subgaleal plane for safety.
High‑yield exam facts Memorize
First‑lineAvoid injury: stay above SMAS or in deep subgaleal plane on forehead.
Danger depthTemporal branch: superficial in temple; Marginal mandibular: very superficial over mandible.
Cross‑innervation%Temporal 15% • Marginal mandibular 10% • Zygomatic/Buccal ~80%
Clinical tipTemporal & marginal mandibular injuries → often permanent; expect poorer recovery.
Surgical danger zones (visualize)
  • Temple: middle third of zygomatic arch — 2.5 cm "danger" zone; anterior ramus >2 cm behind orbital rim.
  • Jawline: along body of mandible anterior to facial artery; locate marginal mandibular branch 1–2 cm above inferior border.
Practical pearls
  • Mark the zygomatic arch and keep dissections superficial there.
  • For lower‑face incisions, evert and stay close to mandibular border or plan incision below to avoid marginal branch.
  • When in doubt, consider nerve monitoring or blunt dissection in the plane deep to SMAS.
