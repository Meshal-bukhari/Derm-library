ANTIPHOSPHOLIPID SYNDROME & LIVEDO RETICULARIS

Board Exam Quick Reference

ANTIPHOSPHOLIPID SYNDROME (APS) - CORE CONCEPTS
Component Definition/Features Clinical Significance
DEFINITION Systemic autoimmune disorder
• Venous/arterial thrombosis AND/OR
• Pregnancy morbidity
• In presence of persistent aPL antibodies		 • Acquired thrombophilia
• Requires both clinical + lab criteria
• Antibodies must be persistent (>12 weeks)
CLASSIFICATION Primary APS: Occurring in isolation
Secondary APS: Associated with autoimmune disease
Catastrophic APS (CAPS): Rare, life-threatening variant 		• Most cases are primary
• Most common secondary association = SLE
• CAPS = widespread small vessel thrombosis + multi-organ failure
MOST COMMON
SKIN SIGN		 Livedo Reticularis
• Persistent, net-like, violaceous mottling
• Often widespread, fixed, broken ("racemosa" pattern)		 • Most frequent cutaneous manifestation
• Different from physiologic cutis marmorata
• Does not resolve with warming
APS CLINICAL CRITERIA (Must Have ≥1)
Criterion Type Specific Requirements Key Details
VASCULAR
THROMBOSIS		 Venous Thrombosis:
• DVT of lower extremities (most common)
• Can lead to pulmonary embolism

Arterial Thrombosis:
• Stroke or TIA (most common arterial event)

Small Vessel Thrombosis:
• Any tissue or organ 		• Key diagnostic clue: Unexplained thrombosis in young person
• Can be arterial, venous, or small vessel
• Location helps determine management
PREGNANCY
MORBIDITY		 • ≥1 unexplained fetal death at/beyond 10th week
• ≥1 premature birth before 34th week due to eclampsia/pre-eclampsia/placental insufficiency
• ≥3 consecutive spontaneous abortions before 10th week 		• Morphologically normal fetus required
• Specific gestational age cutoffs
• Must exclude other causes
• Recurrent pattern is key

APS LABORATORY & TREATMENT ESSENTIALS
Component Details Clinical Notes
DIAGNOSTIC
ANTIBODIES
(Must be persistent
>12 weeks)		 1. Lupus Anticoagulant (LA)
• Functional assay
• Prolongs clotting time in vitro

2. Anticardiolipin (aCL)
• IgG or IgM isotypes

3. Anti-β2-glycoprotein I (anti-β2GPI)
• IgG or IgM isotypes 		• PARADOX: Prolongs aPTT but causes clotting in vivo
• Must test all three types

• "Triple Positive" Profile = highest thrombosis risk
• Must repeat positive tests >12 weeks apart
• Persistence required for diagnosis
TREATMENT
BY SCENARIO		 Thrombosis (DVT/PE):
• Long-term/lifelong Warfarin
• Target INR 2.0-3.0

Obstetric APS:
• Low-dose aspirin + Prophylactic heparin
• Start early in pregnancy

Asymptomatic Positive aPL:
• Low-dose aspirin (controversial)

Catastrophic APS:
• "Triple therapy": Anticoagulation + High-dose steroids + Plasma exchange/IVIG 		• First-line for established thrombosis
• Standard anticoagulation target

• Warfarin CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
• Usually low-molecular-weight heparin

• Management controversial
• Consider if "triple positive"

• Medical emergency
• Requires aggressive treatment
MORTALITY #1 Cause of Death:
• Thrombotic events
• Massive stroke, MI, PE

#2 Cause of Death:
• Sepsis from CAPS
• Multi-organ failure
• Complications from anticoagulation 		• Catastrophic arterial events
• Life-threatening thromboses

• Complications of treatment
• Major hemorrhage risk
• CAPS mortality very high
LIVEDO RETICULARIS DIFFERENTIAL - "3 BUCKETS" APPROACH
Category Mechanism Key Causes Distinguishing Features
1. PHYSIOLOGIC
(BENIGN)		 Normal response
No underlying disease		 • Cutis Marmorata (most common overall)
• CMTC (Cutis Marmorata Telangiectatica Congenita) 		• Faint, symmetric pattern
• Completely reversible with warming
• No ulceration/nodules
• CMTC = fixed, persistent, may have tissue atrophy
2. VASO-OCCLUSIVE
("Plumbing Problem")
⭐ MOST CRITICAL		 Physical vessel occlusion
Thrombotic/Embolic		 Key Causes:
• Antiphospholipid Syndrome
• Cryoglobulinemia (often Hep C)
• Cholesterol Emboli
• Hypercoagulable states
• Calciphylaxis
• Sneddon Syndrome
• DIC/Sepsis
• Hematologic conditions 		• Irregular, broken, "racemosa" pattern
• Fixed (doesn't disappear with warming)
• Often associated with ulceration

Classic Vignettes:
• APS: Young person + thrombosis/pregnancy loss
• Cholesterol emboli: Post-procedure + "blue toe syndrome" + eosinophilia
• Calciphylaxis: ESRD patient + excruciatingly painful necrotic plaques
• Sneddon: Livedo + strokes
3. VASCULITIC
("Pipe Wall Problem")		 Vessel wall inflammation
Inflammatory narrowing		 • Polyarteritis Nodosa
• ANCA-associated vasculitides (GPA, MPA, EGPA)
• Rheumatoid Vasculitis
• SLE
• Relapsing Polychondritis 		• Associated with other vasculitic features
• PAN: Subcutaneous nodules + "starburst" ulcerations
• RV: Severe RA + digital infarcts
• Often systemic disease present
4. MISCELLANEOUS Various mechanisms Drugs:
• Amantadine (classic)
• Minocycline
• Gemcitabine

Infections:
• Syphilis, TB

Malignancy:
• Intravascular Lymphoma 		• Drug-induced usually reversible
• Amantadine = classic board question
• Infections may have other systemic signs
• Intravascular lymphoma = rare but important differential
HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM "TRICKS" & PEARLS
Lupus Anticoagulant Paradox Prolonged aPTT that doesn't correct with normal plasma = PROTHROMBOTIC antibody
⚠️ Sounds like bleeding disorder but causes CLOTTING
Pregnancy Treatment Trap Warfarin = TERATOGEN, NEVER use in pregnancy
✓ Correct answer: Heparin + Aspirin
Livedo Significance Look for other clues: thrombosis history, miscarriages, SLE
Fixed pattern that doesn't warm = pathologic
Asymptomatic aPL Patient DO NOT start warfarin for asymptomatic carriers
✓ Consider low-dose aspirin only
Triple Positive Profile LA + aCL + anti-β2GPI = HIGHEST thrombosis risk
Next Step for Suspected APS Order ALL THREE antibody tests
Must repeat positive tests >12 weeks later
Cholesterol Emboli Classic Triad Post-vascular procedure + Blue toes + Eosinophilia
CAPS Management "Triple Therapy": Anticoagulation + Steroids + Plasma exchange/IVIG
