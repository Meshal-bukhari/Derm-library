🧬 Introduction & Pathogenesis ▼

Key Statistics

Majority of adults and up to 40% of children

Most Important Mutation

Codon 816 at Exon 17

D816V - Substitution of aspartic acid (D) with valine (V)

Activation mutation in codon 816 of C-KIT in up to 40% of cases

Mast Cell Locations & Characteristics

Circulating Mast Cells

  • KIT+ (CD117)
  • CD34+
  • IgG+ (FCγRII)
  • NOT IgE receptor (FCεRI)

Tissue Mast Cells

  • KIT+ (CD117)
  • IgE+ (FCεRI)
  • CD34-
  • IgG-
Memory Aid: Circulating cells have CD34 (like stem cells), Tissue cells have IgE (like allergy)

Other KIT Mutations

Less commonly: deletion K509, delta A533D

Adult patients with advanced systemic mastocytosis - somatic mutations in:

  • KIT and other genes: TET2, ASXL1, JAK2, SRSF2, RUNX1, CBL

NO PULMONARY SYMPTOMS in Mastocytosis

Systemic Disease Distribution

Order of involvement: BM > Bone > Liver > GI > Spleen > LN > CNS

A. SKIN LESIONS

👶 Childhood Skin Lesions

Mastocytoma (35%)
  • May present at birth or during infancy
  • Lesion number: 1-3 (solitary or few)
  • On distal limb extremity
Urticaria Pigmentosa
  • Morphology:Primary Lesions: Small (<1 cm), monomorphic (uniform in appearance) macules and papules.
  • Color: Typically reddish-brown. In patients with lighter skin (skin type I), they may appear pink to red.
  • Associated Features: Close inspection can reveal variable hyperpigmentation and fine telangiectasias.
  • Spared or Less Frequent Sites (Low Density):
    • Central face
    • Distal extremities
    • Palms
    • Soles
Diffuse Cutaneous Mastocytosis
  • Age of Onset: Presents very early in life, specifically "within the first few months of life.
  • Defining Feature: Diffusely thickened, infiltrated skin with a "leathery texture
  • Red to yellow papules or plaques
  • Some develop vesicles or blistering
  • Due to Serine protease from mast cells
  • Remits in 3-5 years
  • NO SYSTEMIC INVOLVEMENT

👨 Adult Skin Lesions

Adult Skin Lesions:
  • 1. Maculopapular Cutaneous Mastocytosis (MPCM) / Urticaria Pigmentosa (UP)
    • Morphology: Small (<1 cm), monomorphic (uniform) macules and papules
    • Color: Reddish-brown. May appear pink to red in fairer skin types (Type I)
    • Distribution: Most numerous on the trunk and proximal extremities. The face, distal extremities, palms, and soles are "less frequently" involved
    • Disease Association: Most often associated with Indolent Systemic Mastocytosis (ISM)
  • 2. Less Common and Rare Cutaneous Presentations
    • A-Nodular Mastocytosis: Yellow-tan to red-brown or violaceous nodules or papulonodules
    • Significance: A hallmark of indolent disease
    • B-Telangiectasia Macularis Eruptiva Perstans (TMEP): A rare form of cutaneous mastocytosis
    • Clinical Lesions: Characterized by macules and patches composed of telangiectasias without significant hyperpigmentation
    • Clinical Sign: The Darier sign tends to be subtle in TMEP lesions
    • C-Mastocytoma: Extremely rare in adults. A key distinguishing feature from the pediatric population

Darier Sign Distribution

mastocytoma > UP > least in adult mastocytosis and negative in TMEP

Mastocytoma shows strongest Darier sign

B. SYSTEMIC DISEASE

🦴 BONE

Appears as: radioopacities or radiolucencies or both

Order of frequency:
  1. Osteoporosis = Demineralization (most common)
  2. Osteosclerosis
  3. osteolytic

Patient complaints: bone pain

Due to: Histamine, Heparin/SCF, IL-6 (IL-6 causes both)

Most commonly affected bones:
• Skull, spine, and pelvis (these are the MC involved)
• proximal long bones

🍃 GASTROINTESTINAL

Symptoms are episodic:

  1. Malabsorption → due to infiltration
  2. Increased motility and/or acid hypersecretion → histamine + prostaglandins
⚠️ Associated with: Gastrointestinal stromal tumors (GISTs) → c-Kit mutation
Radiographic changes in GI:
  • Urticaria-like appearance
  • Thickened gastric, duodenal and jejunal folds
  • Mucosal nodules
  • Peptic ulcers
Clinical presentations:
  • PUD (Peptic Ulcer Disease)->GI hemorrhage.
  • Diarrhea (due to heparin)

🧠 OTHER ORGANS

Bone Marrow:
  • Involved in ALL adults with systemic mastocytosis
Integrated Indications for Bone Marrow Biopsy
  • Indications for BMB in Adults:
    • A bone marrow biopsy is indicated in an adult with cutaneous mastocytosis if any of the following are present:
    • Presence of "B" or "C" Findings (The Strongest Indication):
      • Clinical B Findings: Palpable hepatomegaly with normal LFTs, palpable splenomegaly without hypersplenism , or lymphadenopathy (>2cm); or a serum tryptase >200 ng/mL.
      • Any C Finding:
        • Cytopenias (Neutrophils <1k, Hgb <10, or Platelets <100k).
        • Hepatomegaly with abnormal LFTs or ascites.
        • Osteolytic lesions with pathologic fractures.
        • Malabsorption with weight loss and hypoalbuminemia.
    • Positive KIT D816V Mutation: A positive result on a peripheral blood PCR test, even in the absence of overt B/C findings.
    • High-Risk Score (if KIT is negative): In a patient with a negative KIT D816V blood test, an intermediate-to-high risk score (≥2 points) on the ECNM/Fuchs criteria.
  • Indications for BMB in Children:
    • A bone marrow biopsy is rarely indicated and should only be considered if both of the following are met:
    • Signs of Significant Systemic Disease: Evidence of a B or C finding, most commonly:
      • Hepatosplenomegaly
      • Unexplained cytopenias
      • Basal serum tryptase 200 ng/mL would be an absolute indication)
    • AND
    • Positive KIT D816V Mutation: A positive result on a peripheral blood PCR test.
Spleen:
  • Splenomegaly in 50% of adults
  • Due to histamine and prostaglandins
Lymph Nodes:
  • Lymph node enlargement is uncommon
  • If present → most likely advanced systemic disease
CNS:

Neuropsychiatric and CNS Findings in Mastocytosis:

  • "Mixed Organic Brain Syndrome" (most specific CNS presentation):
    • Cognitive: Poor attention span, limited short-term memory, inability to work effectively, cognitive disorganization
    • Mood/Affect: Irritability, difficulty interacting with others
    • Constitutional: Fatigue, poor motivation
    • Hypothesized cause: May be secondary to mast cell mediators
    • EEG findings: Variable; can be normal or show changes consistent with a toxic/metabolic process
  • General CNS symptoms (mediator-related):
    • Headaches
    • Dizziness
    • Syncope
📋 WHO Classification

WHO Classification (5 Types)

Classification BY WHO: Comprehensive system for all mastocytosis types

1️⃣ CUTANEOUS MASTOCYTOSIS

  • Largest group
  • Includes nearly all affected children and some adults
  • 50-70% resolve by adolescence
  • Childhood onset course → 50% resolution by adolescence
  • 15% persist into adulthood
Diagnosis of Cutaneous Mastocytosis:
1 major criterion (typical skin lesions clinically) + 1 or 2 minor criteria

2️⃣ INDOLENT & SMOLDERING

ISM (Indolent Systemic Mastocytosis)
  • Usually have skin lesions and infiltration
  • Proven multifocal mast cell infiltrates in an extracutaneous organ, usually the bone marrow) but is characterized by a low burden of disease and no evidence of organ damage.
SSM (Smoldering Systemic Mastocytosis)
  • Associated with Hepatomegaly
  • Tryptase >200
  • "B" findings: hepatosplenomegaly, lymphadenopathy, serum tryptase levels >200 ng/ml
  • SSM has B symptoms (Fever)

Also includes: Isolated BM mastocytosis

3️⃣ SM-AHNMD

SM-Associated clonal Hematological Non-mast cell lineage Disease

  • NO skin lesions
  • Associated with:
    • CMML (Chronic Myelomonocytic Leukemia)
    • AML (Acute Myeloid Leukemia)
    • Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
    • HES (Hypereosinophilic Syndrome)

4️⃣ AGGRESSIVE SYSTEMIC

  • Features: Lymphadenopathy and eosinophilia
  • Mast cells in liver and bones
  • Increased Tryptase
  • HIGH MORTALITY

5️⃣ MAST CELL LEUKEMIA

  • NO skin disease
  • POOR PROGNOSIS
Memory Aid for Classification:
Cutaneous (Good) → Indolent/Smoldering (Mild) → SM-AHNMD (No skin) → Aggressive (High mortality) → Leukemia (Worst)
🔬 Diagnosis & Histology

WHO Criteria for Systemic Mastocytosis

Requires: 1 major + 1 minor OR 3 minor criteria

📋 DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

🎯 MAJOR CRITERION

Multifocal, dense infiltrates of mast cells (aggregates of ≥15 mast cells) in bone marrow or extracutaneous tissues

🔹 MINOR CRITERIA

1. >25% of mast cells in BM or extracutaneous tissues are spindle-shaped or atypical
2. Expression of CD25 and/or CD2 by extracutaneous mast cells
3. C-KIT codon 816 mutation in blood, BM or extracutaneous
4. Serum tryptase >20ng/ml

Cutaneous Mastocytosis Diagnosis

Requires: 1 major criterion (typical skin lesions clinically) + 1 or 2 minor criteria

Minor Criteria:
  1. Monomorphic mast cell infiltrate with large aggregates of tryptase positive mast cells (>15 cells/cluster) OR scattered mast cells exceeding 20 cells per microscopic high power field (40×)
  2. Detection of an activating KIT mutation in lesional skin

🧪 LABORATORY STUDIES

🔬 TRYPTASE

Two forms: Alpha and Beta
  • Alpha increased in SM (most useful)
  • Beta increased in anaphylactic shock
Total alpha and beta correlate with disease extent:
  • 50% of patients with levels 20-75 have SM
  • Level >75 had systemic involvement
  • Specific cutoffs: Level >100 → indication for bone marrow biopsy

💧 URINARY MARKERS

Urinary histamine and its major metabolite (MeImAA= 1,4-methylimidazole acetic acid)
  • Increased in symptomatic disease
  • Normal in skin disease
⚠️ May increase with histamine-rich foods:
  • Spinach
  • Eggplants
  • Blue cheese
  • Red wine
Methyl histamine: Another metabolite of H if no MeImAA test available

📊 OTHER MARKERS

PGD2: Increased in systemic mastocytosis
Plasma IL-6:
  • Elevated in mastocytosis
  • Correlates with severity of bone marrow pathology
  • Correlates with organomegaly
  • Correlates with extent of skin involvement

🎯 ADVANCED/AGGRESSIVE MASTOCYTOSIS

Targeted Therapies

⚠️ CODON 816 KIT MUTATION

Associated with aggressive mastocytosis

Does NOT respond to imatinib

Effective treatments:
  • Dasatinib
  • Nilotinib
  • Midostaurin (FDA approved)
  • Avapritinib (FDA approved)

Multitargeted tyrosine kinase inhibitors

✅ OTHER KIT MUTATIONS

Imatinib-responsive mutations:
  • del419
  • K509I
  • F522C
  • V560G

Imatinib mesylate is useful for these mutations

🔬 OTHER TREATMENTS

  • Interferon α-2b (limited success)
  • Local radiotherapy for bone pain
  • Stem cell transplantation

⚠️ Important Treatment Notes

  • Tyrosine kinase inhibitors are NOT effective for long-term treatment of advanced SM
  • Avapritinib and Midostaurin is FDA approved for aggressive systemic mastocytosis
  • Imatinib mesylate approved for treatment of DFSP, systemic mastocytosis, and chronic myelogenous leukemia (CML)

Imatinib Mesylate Details

Approved for treatment of:
  • DFSP (Dermatofibrosarcoma Protuberans)
  • Systemic mastocytosis
  • Chronic myelogenous leukemia (CML)
Mechanism: Potent and specific inhibitor of several protein kinases, including BCR-ABL, c-Kit, and PDGFR
Under investigation for: melanoma, desmoid tumors, and KS (Kaposi Sarcoma)
Side Effects:
  • Most common: Non-specific rash
  • Notable: Periorbital edema
  • Serious: MF-like reaction
