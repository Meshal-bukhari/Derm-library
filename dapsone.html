🎯 Mechanism of Action

🔥 Anti-Inflammatory Actions

  • Inhibit neutrophil MPO - Myeloperoxidase & Hydrogen peroxidase
  • Inhibit neutrophil chemotaxis - Lowers IL-8
  • Reduce respiratory burst - Higher doses >300mg
  • Affect MPO of eosinophils/monocytes

🦠 Other Mechanisms

  • Inhibit Dihydropteroate synthase → Used for LEPROSY
  • N-acetylation → Causes idiosyncratic reactions
  • N-Hydroxylation → Hydroxylamine metabolite (causes hematological side effects except agranulocytosis & leukopenia)

⚠️ Side Effects & Toxicity

🩸 Hematologic

  • Hemolytic anemia (G6PD patients - affects old RBCs)
  • Methemoglobinemia (ALL patients, dose-dependent)
  • Agranulocytosis (Most serious! 3-12 weeks, avg 7 weeks)
  • Hb drop 10% or 2g/dL is acceptable
  • Low HbA1c (false negative in DM)

🧠 Neurologic

  • Peripheral motor neuropathy (predominantly distal motor + some sensory)
  • Cumulative dose: 25g-500g
  • Reversible but takes weeks to 2 years
  • Permanent retinal damage with optic atrophy
  • Photosensitivity
  • Acute psychosis (mainly leprosy patients)

🎭 Skin

  • Hypersensitivity syndrome (3-12 weeks)
  • DRESS with liver failure
  • Maculopapular, EM, SJS/TEN
  • Predict with: HLA-B*1301 → HLA-DRB1

🔄 Other

  • Nausea, gastritis
  • Reversible cholestasis and hepatitis
  • Pregnancy Category C
  • Excreted in breast milk
🚨 Agranulocytosis - Most Serious Reaction

Timing: 3-12 weeks (average 7 weeks)
Symptoms: Fever, sore throat, occasionally sepsis
Management: Discontinue immediately + Give G-CSF → Recovers quickly

💊 Drug Interactions

⚠️ Avoid Combining With
  • Probenecid - Increased dapsone levels
  • Methotrexate (MTX) - More hematological side effects
  • Trimethoprim (TMP) - More hematological side effects
✓ Good News

No cross-reaction with TMP/SMX! ✨

📉 Reduced Absorption

Antacids lower dapsone absorption

🩸 Methemoglobinemia Management

⚠️ Dapsone-Induced Methemoglobinemia

Dapsone is one of the most common causes of drug-induced methemoglobinemia. The hydroxylamine metabolite is responsible for this effect.

📊 Methemoglobin Levels & Clinical Presentation

<3% Normal range - no symptoms No treatment needed
3-15% Blue-gray skin discoloration, minimal symptoms Observation

🔄 Treatment Algorithm

Initial Assessment

  • Discontinue dapsone immediately
  • Check methemoglobin level (co-oximetry)
  • Assess for symptoms: cyanosis, headache, dyspnea
  • Note: Pulse oximetry reads ~85% regardless of actual O2 saturation

Decision Point: Methemoglobin Level

<20% and asymptomatic: Observation + supportive care

20-30% with symptoms OR >30%: Immediate treatment

First-Line Treatment

💊 Methylene Blue (MB)
  • Dose: 1-2 mg/kg IV over 5 minutes
  • Response: Rapid improvement within 10-60 minutes
  • May repeat: Once in 1 hour if needed
  • Maximum: 7 mg/kg total (risk of hemolysis)

Contraindications to Methylene Blue

⚠️ Do NOT use MB if:
  • G6PD deficiency → Use Vitamin C instead
  • Taking SSRIs/SNRIs → Risk of serotonin syndrome

Alternative Treatment

🍊 Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)
  • When: G6PD deficiency or MB contraindicated
  • Dose: 1000mg PO TID or 1.5-2g IV
  • Response: Slower (24-72 hours)
  • Severe cases: Up to 10g IV

Refractory Cases

  • Exchange transfusion
  • Hyperbaric oxygen
  • Hemodialysis (removes dapsone)

🧠 Clinical Pearls for Methemoglobinemia

  • 💡 Chocolate brown blood that doesn't turn red with O2 = pathognomonic
  • 💡 Pulse ox stuck at 85% regardless of actual oxygenation
  • 💡 Cimetidine can prevent methemoglobinemia in chronic dapsone use
  • 💡 Local anesthetics worsen methemoglobinemia - use Vitamin C
  • 💡 G6PD deficiency + MB = hemolysis + worse methemoglobinemia
✓ Prevention Strategies
  • Start low, go slow: Begin with 25-50mg daily
  • Consider prophylaxis: Cimetidine 400mg TID for high-risk patients
  • Monitor symptoms: Educate patients about headache, fatigue, cyanosis
  • Check G6PD: Before starting dapsone in at-risk populations
