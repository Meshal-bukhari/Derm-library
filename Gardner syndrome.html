Gardner Syndrome (Variant of FAP)

A phenotypic variant of Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP), defined by inevitable intestinal polyposis plus multiple extraintestinal manifestations. Mnemonic: "GARDNER’S Cysts And Bumps".

Letter Feature Details
G Gastrointestinal Polyposis Hundreds–thousands of premalignant adenomas (colon ± stomach/small bowel).
Onset: Rare before 10, present in 50% by age 20.
Symptoms: GI bleeding, anemia, abdominal pain, altered bowel habits.
A Adenocarcinoma (inevitable) Without colectomy → colorectal cancer inevitable, usually before 40, sometimes in adolescence.
R Retinal Hamartomas (RPEH) Found in 75–80%. Present at birth, precedes polyposis.
Key: Irregular, bilateral, haphazard lesions (vs. sporadic CHRPE).
D Desmoid Tumors 10–30% of patients. Benign but aggressive fibromatosis.
Sites: Abdominal wall, intra-abdominal (esp. post-colectomy).
Complications: Obstruct bowel/ureters → morbidity/mortality.
Fibromas on back can be precursors.
N Nodules (Epidermoid Cysts) Seen in ~50%, often before puberty.
Sites: Multiple, face/scalp/extremities.
May include pilomatricomas.
E Extra Teeth & Dental Anomalies ~30% prevalence.
Supernumerary teeth, odontomas, unerupted or congenitally missing teeth.
R Ridges on Bone (Osteomas) 65–80%, sometimes by age 8.
Painless bony growths (frontal bone, mandible, maxilla).
S Systemic Malignancies ↑ Risk of thyroid carcinoma (esp. papillary, in women), hepatoblastoma (<3 yrs), pancreatic & biliary cancers.
Turcot Syndrome Type 2: FAP + brain tumors (esp. medulloblastoma).

Exam Pearls

  • Inevitable CRC without colectomy = high-yield exam trap.
  • CHRPE vs. RPEH: Borders & distribution are the differentiator.
  • Desmoid tumors = benign histology but lethal potential → morbidity.
  • Epidermoid cysts before puberty can precede polyposis → clue in pediatrics.
  • Turcot Type 2 = FAP + medulloblastoma (not gliomas — that’s Lynch/Turcot type 1).
