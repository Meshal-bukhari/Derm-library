Tuberous Sclerosis (TSC) – Exam Review Table

Quick Summary Box
  • Classic Triad: Epilepsy, low intelligence, angiofibromas
  • Pathophysiology: TSC1/TSC2 mutations → hyperactive mTOR
  • Cutaneous Hallmarks: Hypomelanotic macules, facial angiofibromas, shagreen patch, ungual fibromas
  • Major Organ Involvement: Brain (tubers, SEGAs), kidneys (AMLs), heart (rhabdomyomas), lungs (LAM)
  • Diagnosis: Major & minor clinical criteria
System Key Features
Oculocutaneous
  • Hypomelanotic macules: ≥3 macules ≥5 mm = Major
  • Facial angiofibromas: ≥3 papules = Major
  • Fibrous cephalic plaque: Major
  • Shagreen patch: Major
  • Ungual fibromas (Koenen tumors): ≥2 = Major
  • Other: Café-au-lait macules, molluscum pendulum, poliosis
  • Retinal hamartomas: Multiple = Major
  • Retinal achromic patch: Minor
Neurologic
  • Seizures: esp. infantile spasms (~60%)
  • Intellectual impairment & TAND: autism, ADHD, behavioral issues
  • CNS Imaging:
    • Cortical tubers & radial migration lines = Major
    • Subependymal nodules (calcify) = Major
    • Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA) = Major
Systemic
  • Cardiac: Rhabdomyomas (infants), WPW arrhythmia
  • Renal: Angiomyolipomas (≥2 = Major), renal cysts (Minor)
  • Pulmonary: LAM in women = Major
  • Dental/oral: ≥3 enamel pits (Minor), ≥2 intraoral fibromas (Minor)
High-Yield Exam Facts
  • Timeline:
    • Infancy: Hypomelanotic macules, rhabdomyoma
    • Childhood: Facial angiofibromas, shagreen patch
    • Later: Ungual fibromas
  • Most likely infant finding: Hypomelanotic macules &/or cardiac rhabdomyoma
  • Genotype-phenotype: TSC2 mutations = more severe
  • Pathognomonic combo: Multiple hypomelanotic macules + angiofibromas + shagreen patch
