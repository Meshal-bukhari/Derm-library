Sjögren’s Syndrome – Quick Review Table

1. Glandular "Sicca" Complex (Hallmark Feature)

Domain Details
Ocular (Keratoconjunctivitis Sicca)
  • Symptoms: Dryness, foreign body sensation, photophobia
  • Test: Schirmer <5 mm in 5 min
  • Complications: Keratitis, corneal ulceration, recurrent infections
Oral (Xerostomia)
  • Symptoms: Dry/burning mouth, difficulty swallowing dry food
  • Signs: Absent salivary pool, cloudy/stringy saliva, gland swelling
  • Complications: Dental caries, candidiasis, worsened GERD
Other Glands Vaginal dryness, burning, dyspareunia

2. Cutaneous Manifestations

Type Details
Common Xerosis (dry, itchy skin)
Most Important Vasculitis (↑ morbidity & lymphoma risk):
  • Palpable purpura
  • Urticarial vasculitis (>24h)
  • Pigmented purpura (capillaritis)
Other Key Findings
  • Annular erythema (SCLE-like)
  • Raynaud phenomenon (~30%)
  • Less common: Erythema nodosum, nodular amyloidosis, Sweet syndrome

3. Systemic & Extraglandular Involvement

System Details
General Fatigue, arthralgias, myalgias
Musculoskeletal Non-erosive, polyarticular arthritis (knees, ankles)
Pulmonary Interstitial pneumonitis (often subclinical)
Renal Interstitial nephritis, tubular dysfunction
Neurologic
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • CNS: Brain fog, memory loss, MS-like symptoms
Most Serious Complication B-cell lymphoma (MALT type):
  • 20x ↑ risk
  • Salivary/lacrimal origin
  • Red flags: Persistent parotid swelling, lymphadenopathy, cryoglobulinemia, vasculitis, low complement
