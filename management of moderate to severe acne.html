🎯 Definition of Moderate to Severe Acne

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: No universally accepted grading system exists!
📋 Board Exam Pearl: Know the 4 key features that define moderate to severe acne - they love testing this classification!

🔍 Key Features (Remember: "VLMS")

V - Visually prominent acne (many comedones, papules, pustules)
⬇️
L - Large papules/nodules (>5mm diameter)
⬇️
M - Multiple body areas involved
⬇️
S - Scarring present
🧠 Memory Aid: "Very Large, Multiple Scars" = VLMS for moderate-severe acne criteria
⚠️ CRITICAL: Consider psychological burden! Even "mild-appearing" acne with significant psychological distress should be treated as moderate-severe.

🎯 General Approach & Systemic Therapy Selection

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: Systemic therapy is used to optimize and simplify treatment!

📊 When to Use Systemic Therapy

🚨 Strong Indications for Early Systemic Therapy:

  • Nodular lesions
  • Extensive skin involvement
  • Existing scarring
  • Psychological disturbance

💊 Primary Systemic Therapies

Therapy Best For Key Advantage Major Limitation
Oral Isotretinoin All lesion types Only drug that can permanently alter course Teratogenicity
Oral Antibiotics Papulopustular/Nodular Well tolerated Resistance development
Oral Contraceptives All lesion types (females) Contraceptive benefit Thrombosis risk
Spironolactone All lesion types (females) Well tolerated Hyperkalemia risk
📋 Board Exam Pearl: Oral isotretinoin is typically given as MONOTHERAPY, while all other systemic therapies are combined with topical therapy!

🎯 Patient Selection Factors

Remember "SPRAVP":
  • Severity of lesions
  • Pregnancy status
  • Risk factors for adverse effects
  • Age and sex
  • Values and preferences
  • Patient clinical manifestations

🔥 Oral Isotretinoin - The Gold Standard

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: Only acne medication that combats ALL 4 factors in acne pathogenesis!

🎯 Mechanism of Action

Remember "SIFIC":
  • Sebum production inhibition
  • Inflammation reduction
  • Follicular hyperkeratinization inhibition
  • Inhibits C. acnes proliferation
  • Comedogenesis inhibition
📊 Standard Dosing:
• Start: 0.5 mg/kg/day
• Target: 1 mg/kg/day
• Total cumulative: 120-150 mg/kg over ~20 weeks
⚠️ ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS:
• Pregnancy (Category X)
• Inability to comply with pregnancy prevention
• Hypersensitivity to isotretinoin

🚨 REMS Program (iPLEDGE)

Board Fact: In the US, isotretinoin requires participation in a Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) program due to teratogenicity!

📋 Common Adverse Effects

System Common Effects Monitoring
Mucocutaneous Dry skin, lips, eyes, nosebleeds Clinical assessment
Ocular Dry eyes, night vision changes Ophthalmologic evaluation if symptoms
Hepatic Elevated transaminases Baseline and monthly LFTs
Lipid Hyperlipidemia (especially triglycerides) Baseline and monthly lipid panel
Musculoskeletal Myalgias, arthralgias Clinical assessment
📋 Board Exam Pearl: Patients with severe acne are at risk for isotretinoin-induced acne flares! May need glucocorticoid pre-treatment.

💊 Oral Antibiotics

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: Tetracyclines are first-line! Always combine with benzoyl peroxide to prevent resistance!
🚨 CRITICAL: Limit treatment to 3-4 months maximum! Always use with topical benzoyl peroxide!

🥇 First-Line: Tetracyclines

Doxycycline

Standard Dosing:
• 50-100 mg twice daily OR 100 mg once daily
• Delayed release: 100 mg q12h × 1 day, then 100 mg daily
Subantimicrobial:
• 40 mg daily (delayed release) OR 20 mg twice daily
⚠️ Contraindications: Pregnancy, children <8 years

Side Effects: Photosensitivity, GI distress, esophagitis, pseudotumor cerebri

Minocycline

Standard Dosing:
• 50-100 mg daily or twice daily
• Extended release: 1 mg/kg/day (max 135 mg/day)
⚠️ Contraindications: Pregnancy, children <8 years

Unique Side Effects: Vestibular effects (headache, dizziness, vertigo), skin discoloration, lupus-like syndrome

Not typically first-line due to greater toxicity concerns!

Sarecycline

Weight-based Dosing (once daily):
• 33-54 kg: 60 mg
• 55-84 kg: 100 mg
• 85-136 kg: 150 mg
⚠️ Contraindications: Pregnancy, children <8 years

Advantage: Narrower spectrum = less C. acnes resistance and gut microbiome disruption

🔄 Alternative Antibiotics

📋 Board Exam Pearl: Alternative antibiotics are reserved for tetracycline failures or contraindications. We rarely use them - prefer to switch to non-antibiotic therapies!
Antibiotic Dose Notes
Azithromycin 500 mg 1-3x/week Pulse therapy; concern for resistance
Erythromycin 500 mg twice daily High C. acnes resistance; GI side effects
TMP-SMX 160/800 mg 1-2x daily Risk of SJS/TEN
Cephalexin 500 mg twice daily Limited data

⚠️ Antibiotic Resistance

🧠 Key Statistics to Remember:
• C. acnes resistance increased from 20% (1978) to 62% (1996)
• Most common with erythromycin
• Minocycline resistance is rare but emerging

🌸 Oral Hormonal Therapies (Females Only)

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: Goal is to inhibit acne through reducing androgen action on pilosebaceous units!
📋 Board Exam Pearl: Both females with hyperandrogenism AND normal androgen levels can benefit from hormonal treatment!

🎯 Appropriate Candidates

  • Postmenarchal females with moderate-severe acne
  • Females with acne-related hyperandrogenism
  • Females with normal serum androgens
  • Consider for milder acne if contraception also desired

💊 Oral Contraceptives

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Remember "ESIS":
  • Estrogen-mediated sebum suppression
  • SHBG increase (reduces free androgens)
  • Inhibition of gonadal androgen production
  • Sebaceous gland opposition to androgens
📊 FDA-Approved for Acne:
• Ethinyl estradiol 20/30/35 mcg + norethindrone 1 mg
• Ethinyl estradiol 35 mcg + norgestimate 180/215/250 mcg
• Ethinyl estradiol 20 mcg + drospirenone 3 mg
⚠️ IMPORTANT: All low-dose combination OCs are estrogen-dominant and have overall antiandrogenic effect, regardless of progestin!

🌟 Antiandrogenic Progestins

Progestin Formulation Key Studies
Drospirenone EE 20 or 30 mcg + DRSP 3 mg OR 3.41 for clear/almost clear skin vs placebo
Dienogest EE 30 mcg + dienogest 2 mg 55% vs 39% lesion reduction vs placebo
Cyproterone acetate* EE 35 mcg + CPA 2 mg Superior to placebo for lesion reduction

*Not available in United States

📊 OCs vs Antibiotics Efficacy

📋 Board Exam Pearl: Meta-analysis shows similar efficacy at 6 months (OCs 55% vs antibiotics 53% lesion reduction), but antibiotics work faster (48% vs 37% at 3 months)!
⚠️ MAJOR CONTRAINDICATIONS:
• History of thromboembolism
• Thrombophilic disorders
• Pregnancy
• Certain cardiovascular conditions

💊 Spironolactone

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Remember "BISHR":
  • Blocks androgen receptors
  • Inhibits androgen biosynthesis
  • SHBG increase
  • Halts androstenedione → testosterone conversion
  • Reduces 5α-reductase activity
📊 Standard Dosing:
• Start: 25-50 mg daily
• Target: 50-100 mg daily (single or divided dose)
• Higher doses (up to 200 mg) used but limited benefit
📋 Board Exam Pearl: Results in 30-50% reduction in sebum excretion! Response typically seen in 3-5 months.

📈 Key Spironolactone Studies

Study Population Results
SAFA Trial 410 adult females 19% vs 6% treatment success at 12 weeks
FASCE Trial 133 adult females Spironolactone superior to doxycycline at 6 months
Retrospective (395 pts) Ages ≥21 years 66% had ≥90% improvement

⚠️ Hyperkalemia Risk

🚨 CRITICAL BOARD FACT: Risk of hyperkalemia is LOW in young, healthy women! No routine monitoring needed <45 years if healthy.

📋 Monitoring Recommendations:

  • No monitoring: Healthy women <45 years
  • Periodic monitoring: Women >45 years
  • Regular monitoring: Cardiac disease, renal disease, multiple comorbidities
  • Baseline + follow-up: Patients on hyperkalemia-risk medications
⚠️ PREGNANCY CONCERNS:
• Risk of male fetus feminization
• Advise pregnancy avoidance
• Discontinue immediately if pregnancy occurs
• Often combined with OCs for contraception

🔥 Severe Acne Variants

⚡ HIGH-YIELD: Acne fulminans and acne conglobata require special management approaches!

🚨 Acne Fulminans

📋 Board Exam Pearl: Rare, severe form with ABRUPT development of large inflammatory nodules + friable plaques with erosions, ulcers, and hemorrhagic crusts ± systemic symptoms!

🎯 Clinical Features:

  • Large inflammatory nodules
  • Friable plaques with erosions
  • Ulcers and hemorrhagic crusts
  • ±Systemic symptoms (fever, malaise, bone pain, arthralgia)
  • ±Erythema nodosum, leukocytosis

💊 Treatment Protocol

🧠 Remember Treatment Steps:
  1. Discontinue isotretinoin if drug-induced
  2. Start oral corticosteroids (prednisone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day)
  3. Wait before isotretinoin:
    • 4 weeks if systemic symptoms
    • 2 weeks if no systemic symptoms
  4. Start low-dose isotretinoin (0.1 mg/kg/day)
  5. Continue steroids for ≥4 weeks
  6. Gradually increase isotretinoin and taper steroids over 4-8 weeks
⚠️ TARGET: Cumulative isotretinoin dose 120-150 mg/kg (may need higher for refractory cases)

⚡ Alternative Treatments for Acne Fulminans

Treatment Use Evidence Level
Oral steroids + tetracyclines Alternative to isotretinoin Not as effective; not first-line
High-potency topical steroids Adjunctive Case reports
Cyclosporine Refractory cases Case reports
Dapsone Alternative Case reports
Anti-TNF biologics Investigational Case reports

🔗 Acne Conglobata

📋 Board Exam Pearl: Severe nodular acne with drainage, sinus tracts, and severe scarring!

💊 Treatment of Choice: Oral Isotretinoin

⚠️ RISK: Patients may experience severe flares at isotretinoin start!
📊 Modified Protocol:
• Low initial doses (≤0.5 mg/kg/day)
• ±Systemic glucocorticoids before/during therapy
• Gradual dose escalation as tolerated

🧬 Emerging Therapies

🧠 TNF-Alpha Inhibitors in Case Reports:
  • Etanercept - Case reports of improvement
  • Adalimumab - Documented benefit
  • Infliximab + isotretinoin - Combination therapy

*Further study needed before routine recommendation

📋 Summary & Board Recommendations

⚡ ULTRA HIGH-YIELD: Master these key recommendations for board success!

🎯 Grade 2C Recommendations (Remember These!)

Early systemic therapy for nodular lesions, extensive involvement, or scarring
⬇️
Oral isotretinoin for severe, extensive, nodular acne
⬇️
Tetracyclines over other antibiotics
⬇️
OCs over spironolactone if contraception desired
⬇️
No routine K+ monitoring in healthy women <45 years on spironolactone

🚨 Critical Board Points

🧠 Must-Know Facts:
  • Isotretinoin - Only drug that permanently alters acne course
  • Antibiotics - Always limit to 3-4 months + benzoyl peroxide
  • Pregnancy - Avoid isotretinoin, tetracyclines, spironolactone, OCs
  • Resistance - C. acnes resistance: 20% (1978) → 62% (1996)
  • Monitoring - No routine K+ monitoring for spironolactone in healthy young women
