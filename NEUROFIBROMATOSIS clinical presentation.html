🔬 NEUROFIBROMATOSIS TYPE 1 (NF1)

🏆 MOST COMMON FORM - von Recklinghausen Disease
Incidence: 1 in 3,000 births | Chromosome 17 | Gene: NF1

📋 Diagnostic Criteria (Need ≥2)

  1. ≥6 CALMs: >5mm prepubertal, >15mm postpubertal
  2. ≥2 neurofibromas OR 1 plexiform neurofibroma
  3. Axillary/inguinal freckling (Crowe sign)
  4. ≥2 Lisch nodules (iris hamartomas)
  5. Optic pathway glioma
  6. Distinctive skeletal abnormality (sphenoid dysplasia, tibial pseudarthrosis)
  7. First-degree relative with NF1

🌟 Cutaneous Manifestations

📅 TIMELINE OF APPEARANCE:
  • First year: CALMs appear (>90%)
  • Age 4-6: Axillary/inguinal freckling (~80%)
  • Around puberty: Cutaneous neurofibromas (60-90%)
  • Congenital: Plexiform neurofibromas (25-50%)
🔍 KEY CUTANEOUS FEATURES:
  • CALMs: "Coast of California" borders (smooth, regular)
  • Neurofibromas: "Buttonhole sign" (invaginate with pressure)
  • Plexiform NFs: "Bag of worms" consistency
  • Nevus anemicus: Pale macule (30-50%)
  • JXG: Yellow-red papules (15-35% in first 3 years)

👁️ Ocular Features

  • Lisch nodules: ~90% by age 20 - yellow-brown iris hamartomas
  • Choroidal abnormalities: >80% in adults (near-infrared imaging)

🦴 Skeletal Features

  • Common: Macrocephaly, scoliosis, hypertelorism, osteopenia
  • Pathognomonic: Sphenoid wing dysplasia (<5%), tibial pseudarthrosis (2%)

🧠 Tumors & Malignancies

🚨 MALIGNANT TRANSFORMATION RISK:
MPNST: 8-15% lifetime risk from plexiform neurofibromas
Red flags: Rapid growth, new pain, neurologic deficit
  • Optic pathway glioma: 15-20% of children (before age 10)
  • JMML risk: Markedly increased if JXG present
  • Other: Pheochromocytoma (~1%), GISTs, rhabdomyosarcoma

🧠 Neurologic Features

  • Learning difficulties: 30-50% (most common neurologic issue)
  • T2 hyperintensities: 50-75% of children ("UBOs")
  • ADHD: Frequent
  • Seizures: ~5%

❤️ Cardiovascular Complications

Hypertension (~30%):
  • Most Common Cause: Essential hypertension
  • Secondary Causes: Renal artery stenosis (especially in children) and pheochromocytoma
  • Other Abnormalities: Pulmonic stenosis (~1%) and cerebrovascular anomalies like stenoses and aneurysms (2-5%)

🎧 NEUROFIBROMATOSIS TYPE 2 (NF2)

🎯 DEFINING FEATURE: BILATERAL VESTIBULAR SCHWANNOMAS
Chromosome 22 | Gene: NF2 | "NF2 = 2 Ears on Chromosome 22"

🔑 Key Features

Hallmark: Bilateral vestibular schwannomas (hearing loss)
Other tumors: Meningiomas, spinal tumors
Cutaneous: Schwannomas (66%), few CALMs (50%)

❌ Key NEGATIVE Features (Distinguish from NF1)

  • NO Lisch nodules
  • NO significant learning disabilities
  • NO axillary/inguinal freckling
  • Fewer than 6 CALMs

🧩 MOSAIC (SEGMENTAL) NF1

Pathophysiology: Postzygotic NF1 mutation during development
Presentation: NF1 features confined to specific body segment
Pattern: Dermatomal or block-like distribution
Risk: If gonads involved → child can have full NF1

🎭 "NF1-LIKE" SYNDROMES

Legius Syndrome (SPRED1 mutation)

🎯 MAIN NF1 MIMIC:
Has: Multiple CALMs + axillary/inguinal freckling
LACKS: Neurofibromas, Lisch nodules, optic gliomas, skeletal defects

Watson Syndrome

  • Features: CALMs, short stature, pulmonic stenosis, intellectual disability
  • May have: Neurofibromas and Lisch nodules

🪤 HIGH-YIELD BOARD TRAPS

TRAP #1: The Mimic Trap (NF1 vs Legius)
Scenario: Child with multiple CALMs and freckling, but NO neurofibromas or Lisch nodules
Answer: Legius Syndrome (not NF1)
TRAP #2: Timeline Trap
Scenario: 2-year-old with CALMs - will they have cutaneous neurofibromas?
Answer: No - neurofibromas appear around puberty
TRAP #3: Key Negative Trap (NF1 vs NF2)
Scenario: Patient with few CALMs + subcutaneous nodule + hearing loss + normal slit-lamp
Answer: NF2 (no Lisch nodules = key negative)
TRAP #4: Red Flag Trap
Scenario: Painful, rapidly growing plexiform neurofibroma
Answer: Investigate for MPNST (PET-CT, biopsy)
TRAP #5: JXG Risk Recognition
Scenario: NF1 patient with juvenile xanthogranulomas
Answer: Increased JMML risk - monitor closely

🧠 MEMORY TOOLS

🔤 NF1 MNEMONIC: "CAFE SPOT"
  • CALMs
  • Axillary freckling
  • Fibromas (neurofibromas)
  • Eye (Lisch nodules)
  • Skeletal defects
  • Pedigree (family history)
  • Optic tumor
  • Tumor (any qualifying tumor)
🎧 NF2 MNEMONIC:
"NF2 = 2 Ears on Chromosome 22"
🕐 AGE-BASED MEMORY AID:
  • Year 1: CALMs appear
  • Age 4-6: Freckling appears
  • Age 10: Optic gliomas (before this age)
  • Puberty: Cutaneous neurofibromas
  • Age 20: 90% have Lisch nodules

⚡ RAPID FIRE FACTS

🚀 MUST-KNOW FACTS:
  • NF1 most common: 1 in 3,000 births
  • CALM criteria: ≥6, size >5mm pre-pubertal, >15mm post-pubertal
  • Buttonhole sign: Pathognomonic for neurofibromas
  • MPNST risk: 8-15% from plexiform neurofibromas
  • Lisch nodules: 90% by age 20 in NF1
  • JXG + NF1: High JMML risk
  • NF2 hallmark: Bilateral vestibular schwannomas
  • Legius syndrome: CALMs + freckling WITHOUT tumors
🎯 EXAM PEARLS:
  • If NO neurofibromas or Lisch nodules → Think Legius
  • If bilateral hearing loss → Think NF2
  • If rapid growth of neurofibroma → Think MPNST
  • If segmental pattern → Think mosaic NF1
