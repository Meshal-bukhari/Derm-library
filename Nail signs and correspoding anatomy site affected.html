NAIL MATRIX DYSFUNCTION (The "Factory" Defects)
Matrix Location Clinical Signs Mechanism Key Associations & Differentiators
PROXIMAL MATRIX
("Dorsal/Superficial
Nail Plate Producer")		 • Beau's Lines
• Pitting
• Onychorrhexis
• Trachyonychia 		• Temporary interruption of mitotic activity
• Foci of parakeratosis → shed cells leave pits
• Damage to discrete cell columns
• Mild diffuse inflammatory damage 		• Trauma, systemic illness, chemo
• Psoriasis: Large, deep, irregular pits
• Alopecia areata: Small, geometric grid pits
• Lichen planus, vascular impairment
• "Sandpapered" appearance
DISTAL MATRIX
(LUNULA)
("Ventral/Deep
Nail Plate Producer")		 • True Leukonychia
  - Punctate/Striate
  - Diffuse (Totalis) 		• Parakeratosis in distal matrix
• Parakeratotic cells in ventral plate cause white appearance
• KEY: Within nail plate, grows out with nail 		• Microtrauma (manicures, shoes) → punctate
• Genetic (PLCD1, GJA1 mutations) → totalis
• Does NOT fade with pressure
ENTIRE MATRIX
("Full-Thickness
Insult")		 • Onychomadesis
• Koilonychia
• Onychauxis 		• Complete arrest of matrix activity
• Thinning + altered growth dynamics
• Matrix hyperproliferation 		• Severe systemic illness, hand-foot-mouth
• Iron deficiency, infants (physiologic)
• Nail thickening
MATRIX MELANOCYTES
("Pigment Source")		 • Longitudinal Melanonychia • Active matrix melanocytes
• KEY: Melanocytes ONLY in matrix 		• Benign activation, drugs, ethnic variation
• RED FLAGS: Hutchinson's sign, single band, adult onset

DISEASE-SPECIFIC NAIL PATTERNS
Condition Characteristic Pattern Pathophysiology Key Differentiators
PSORIASIS • Large, deep, irregular pits
• "Oil Drop" sign
• Onycholysis
• Subungual hyperkeratosis 		• Proximal matrix parakeratosis
• Nail bed inflammation + parakeratosis
• Disrupted plate-bed seal
• Excessive nail bed proliferation 		• Oil drop sign HIGHLY SPECIFIC
• Often precedes onycholysis
• vs Alopecia areata: geometric pits
• Yellow-orange discoloration
ALOPECIA AREATA • Small, geometric grid pits
• Trachyonychia (common) 		• Organized parakeratotic foci
• Diffuse matrix inflammation 		• Regular, geometric pattern
• vs Psoriasis: irregular pits
• "Sandpapered" appearance common
GENETIC SYNDROMES • Triangular lunulae (Nail-Patella)
• Pachyonychia (PC)
• Diffuse true leukonychia 		• LMX1B mutation → matrix development
• Dense subungual hyperkeratosis
• PLCD1, GJA1 mutations 		• Associated with limb defects
• Plantar keratoderma
• Entire nail white from birth
SYSTEMIC DISEASE • Beau's lines (all nails)
• Koilonychia (iron deficiency)
• Apparent leukonychia patterns 		• Systemic matrix disruption
• Matrix thinning + altered growth
• Nail bed vascular/edema changes 		• Timing reflects systemic event
• "Spoon" shape with iron deficiency
• Specific patterns by organ system
HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM PEARLS
Matrix Localization Rules Proximal Matrix → Surface defects (pitting, Beau's, ridging)
Distal Matrix → Deep plate defects (true leukonychia)
Entire Matrix → Severe changes (shedding, spoon nails)
True vs Apparent Leukonychia • True: IN nail plate, grows out, doesn't fade with pressure
• Apparent: Nail bed color, fades with pressure, doesn't grow out
Psoriasis Nail Signs "Oil Drop" sign = PATHOGNOMONIC
Large, deep, irregular pits (vs alopecia areata geometric pits)
Pitting Pattern Recognition • Psoriasis: Large, deep, irregular
• Alopecia areata: Small, geometric, grid-like
• Eczema: Irregular pattern
Splinter Hemorrhage Reality Trauma is #1 cause - NOT endocarditis
Multiple splinters → consider systemic disease
Beau's Lines Mechanism Depth = severity, Width = duration
Distance from cuticle = timing of insult
Nail Bed Pathology Sign Granular layer presence = pathologic
(Normal nail bed lacks granular layer)
Trachyonychia ("20-Nail Dystrophy") "Sandpapered" appearance
Most common in alopecia areata
Apparent Leukonychia Types • Muehrcke's: Paired bands → Hypoalbuminemia
• Terry's: Nearly all white → Liver disease
• Half-and-half: Proximal white → Renal disease
Onychomadesis vs Beau's Lines Onychomadesis = "Severe Beau's line"
Complete matrix arrest → nail shedding
