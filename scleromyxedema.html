SCLEROMYXEDEMA - Board Review Summary
🎯 CLINICAL PRESENTATION
CUTANEOUS FEATURES Primary Lesion - The Papule:
  • Size: 2-3 mm
  • Quality: Firm, waxy, closely aligned
  • KEY PATTERN: Linear array arrangement 📏
  • Distribution: Widespread, symmetrical
  • Common sites: Head/neck (glabella), upper trunk, hands, forearms
Sclerodermoid Changes:
  • Shiny, indurated, stiffened skin between papules
  • Leonine Facies: Deep glabellar furrowing → lion-like face
  • "Shar-Pei Sign": Deep furrowing of trunk/extremities
  • "Doughnut Sign": Central depression + elevated rim over PIP joints
  • Functional: ↓ mouth mobility, sclerodactyly
Key NEGATIVES (vs. Systemic Sclerosis):
  • ❌ No mat/cuticular telangiectasias
  • ❌ No calcinosis
SYSTEMIC FEATURES 🎯 CORE ASSOCIATION - Monoclonal Gammopathy:
  • Found in nearly ALL patients
  • Most common: IgG with lambda light chains
  • Plasma cell dyscrasia (progression to myeloma <10%)
Internal Organ Involvement:
  • 🧠 NEUROLOGIC (Most Feared):
    • Peripheral neuropathy (sensory)
    • Dermato-neuro syndrome: Life-threatening encephalopathy
    • Features: Flu-like prodrome → fever → seizures → coma
  • 💪 MUSCULAR: Myositis → proximal weakness
  • 🦴 RHEUMATOLOGIC: Arthropathies, carpal tunnel
  • 🫁 GI: Dysphagia
  • Other: Pulmonary, renal, cardiovascular
🔬 HISTOPATHOLOGY
THE DIAGNOSTIC TRIAD
🧠 MNEMONIC: "Mucin, Many Fibroblasts, More Collagen"
  1. Mucin Deposition: Diffuse in upper-mid reticular dermis
  2. Fibroblast Proliferation: Marked ↑ irregularly arranged fibroblasts
  3. Increased Collagen: Significant fibrosis
🔑 KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: The marked ↑ fibroblasts distinguishes from Scleredema (normal fibroblast count)
💊 MANAGEMENT - Treatment Ladder
TREATMENT HIERARCHY
🥇 FIRST-LINE: IVIg
  • Widespread acceptance as first-line
  • For skin + systemic disease (including dermato-neuro syndrome)
  • Maintenance infusions typically required
🥈 SECOND-LINE: Myeloma-Directed
  • Thalidomide or Lenalidomide
  • Combination: Bortezomib + Lenalidomide + Dexamethasone
  • Note: Combination may be 1st-line for severe disease (CNS/cardiac)
🥉 THIRD-LINE: HSCT
  • Autologous Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation
  • Reserved for disabling/life-threatening disease
⚠️ DANGEROUS: Melphalan
  • Historical therapy
  • High mortality (30%) from secondary malignancies + sepsis
  • Board trap: Will appear as distractor
🎯 HIGH-YIELD EXAM TRAPS
CLINICAL MIMICRY
Scleromyxedema vs. Scleredema Type II
Vignette: Indurated skin + monoclonal gammopathy
🔑 Key: Look for "firm, waxy papules" or "linear array" = Scleromyxedema
vs. "Diffuse, smooth induration" without papules = Scleredema
HISTOLOGY TRAP
Scleromyxedema vs. Scleredema Histology
Vignette: Biopsy with dermal mucin + fibrosis
🔑 Key: Marked ↑ fibroblasts = Scleromyxedema
vs. Normal fibroblast count = Scleredema
EMERGENCY COMPLICATION
Life-Threatening Emergency
Vignette: Known scleromyxedema + fever + new seizure
🔑 Answer: Dermato-neuro syndrome
Most feared neurologic complication
TREATMENT TRAP
First-Line Therapy Question
Vignette: Newly diagnosed moderate skin disease
🔑 Answer: IVIg (first-line)
Distractors: Melphalan (dangerous), HSCT (too advanced for initial)
