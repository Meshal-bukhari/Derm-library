Argininosuccinic Aciduria

Also known as: Argininosuccinate Lyase (ASL) Deficiency

Board Exam At-a-Glance: A baby with seizures, lethargy, and hyperammonemia has a urea cycle defect. If they also have trichorrhexis nodosa (brittle hair) and elevated citrulline, think Argininosuccinic Aciduria.

🧬The Core Defect

A life-threatening urea-cycle metabolism disorder. The body cannot properly process nitrogen waste, leading to toxic buildup.

➡️
Gene & Inheritance:
Mutated ASL gene inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern.
🧪
Core Problem:
Deficiency of the enzyme Argininosuccinate Lyase leads to hyperammonemia (high ammonia levels).

🩺Clinical Presentation

Severe Neonatal-Onset Form

Presents within days of birth. This is a medical emergency.

Warning Signs: Lethargy, somnolence, poor feeding, vomiting, hypothermia, and tachypnea. Can rapidly progress to seizures, coma, and death if untreated.

Milder, Late-Onset Form

Often triggered by metabolic stressors like infection or stress. Presents with recurring episodes.

🧠
Neuro-Behavioral: Irritability, behavioral dysfunction, developmental delay, intellectual disability.
🤢
Systemic: Vomiting, failure to thrive.
🩺
Hepatic: Hepatomegaly.
✂️
Dermatologic Hallmark:
Trichorrhexis Nodosa (dry, brittle hair that breaks easily). This is a key clue on a dermatology exam!

🔬Diagnosis & Workup

The diagnostic path often starts with newborn screening and is confirmed with specific tests.

📈
Best Initial Test (Screening):
Elevated blood citrulline level detected on newborn screen. Arginosuccinic acid will also be present.
✅
Confirmatory Test:
Skin biopsy confirming deficiency of ASL enzyme activity in cultured fibroblasts.
Clinical Trick: While multiple urea cycle disorders cause hyperammonemia, the combination of elevated citrulline AND the presence of arginosuccinic acid points specifically to ASL deficiency.

💊Management Strategy

Management focuses on treating acute hyperammonemia and preventing future episodes.

1
Treat Hyperammonemia:
Provide supplemental Arginine therapy. This helps promote nitrogen excretion through an alternative pathway.
2
Dietary Control:
Reduction of oral protein to limit the nitrogen load on the urea cycle.
!
For Resistant Cases:
In severe or refractory hyperammonemia, hemodialysis may be required to rapidly clear ammonia.

🧠Test Yourself: Active Recall

What is the key dermatologic finding in Argininosuccinic Aciduria?

Trichorrhexis nodosa (dry, brittle hair).

What is the mode of inheritance and the affected gene?

Autosomal recessive inheritance of a mutated ASL gene.

What two lab values are key for diagnosis on a newborn screen?

1. Elevated blood citrulline
2. Presence of arginosuccinic acid.

What is the cornerstone of long-term management?

A combination of arginine therapy and a low-protein diet.
