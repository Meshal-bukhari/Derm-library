HLA ASSOCIATIONS IN DERMATOLOGY

Board Exam Quick Reference

PSORIASIS - HLA ASSOCIATIONS
Clinical Presentation HLA Association Clinical Significance
Early-onset & Severe Psoriasis HLA-B17 • Associated with more aggressive disease course
• Younger age of onset
• Greater disease severity
Early-onset & Guttate Psoriasis HLA-Cw6 • Most common psoriasis HLA association
• Strongly linked to guttate pattern
• Often triggered by streptococcal infections
Psoriatic Arthritis HLA-B27 • Joint involvement pattern
• Axial skeleton involvement
• Associated with enthesitis
LICHEN PLANUS - HLA ASSOCIATIONS
LP Variant HLA Association Clinical Notes
Oral Lichen Planus HLA-B8 • Most common association
• Mucosal involvement predominant
• Higher malignant transformation risk
Cutaneous Lichen Planus HLA-Bw35 • Most common for skin involvement
• Classic papular LP
• Wickham's striae
HCV-induced Lichen Planus HLA-DR6 • Hepatitis C-associated LP
• Often more widespread
• May resolve with HCV treatment
DRUG REACTIONS - HLA ASSOCIATIONS
Drug & Reaction HLA Association Clinical Significance
Worse Prognosis in SJS HLA-DQB1 • Prognostic marker
• More severe disease course
• Higher mortality risk
Abacavir-induced DRESS HLA-B*5701 • Strong association
• Screening recommended before use
• HIV medication
Allopurinol-induced DRESS/SJS HLA-B*5801 • High-risk association
• More common in Asian populations
• Screening in high-risk groups
Carbamazepine-induced SJS/EM HLA-B*1502 • Asian population predominant
• Anticonvulsant hypersensitivity
• Cross-reactivity with other AEDs
Bactrim-induced FDE HLA-B55 • Fixed drug eruption pattern
• Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole
• Recurrent at same sites
Dapsone hypersensitivity syndrome HLA-B*13:01 • Strong association in Chinese
• Severe cutaneous adverse reaction (SCAR)
• Screening recommended in high-risk populations

INFECTIONS & AUTOIMMUNE CONDITIONS
Condition HLA Association Clinical Significance
Tuberculoid Leprosy HLA-DR2 and DR3 • Better immune response
• Limited disease
• Better prognosis
Lepromatous Leprosy HLA-DQ1 • Poor immune response
• Disseminated disease
• Worse prognosis
Renal Disease in HSP HLA-B35 • Henoch-Schönlein purpura
• Nephritis development
• Prognostic significance
Behçet Disease HLA-B51 • Strong association
• More common in certain populations
• Systemic vasculitis
Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) HLA-DR2 and DR3 • Multiple organ involvement
• Photosensitive rash
• Systemic autoimmunity
SCLE, Löfgren Syndrome, LABD HLA-B8/DR3 • Subacute cutaneous LE
• Sarcoidosis variant
• Linear IgA bullous dermatosis
Hydralazine-induced SLE HLA-DR4 • Drug-induced lupus
• Antihypertensive medication
• Often reversible
Worse Prognosis in Alopecia Areata HLA-DQ7 and DR4 • Prognostic markers
• More extensive hair loss
• Poorer treatment response
HIGH-YIELD BOARD EXAM PATTERNS
Most Common Associations • Psoriasis: HLA-Cw6 (early-onset/guttate)
• Oral LP: HLA-B8
• Cutaneous LP: HLA-Bw35
• Behçet: HLA-B51
Drug Screening Recommendations • Abacavir: Screen HLA-B*5701
• Allopurinol: Screen HLA-B*5801 (Asian populations)
• Carbamazepine: Screen HLA-B*1502 (Asians)
Prognostic HLA Associations • Worse SJS prognosis: HLA-DQB1
• Worse alopecia areata: HLA-DQ7/DR4
• HSP renal disease: HLA-B35
Blistering Disease Patterns • DH: HLA-DQ2 > DQ8 (gluten sensitivity)
• PV: Multiple associations (DQB1*0502, DR4, DR6)
• BP & LS: Both HLA-DQ7
Leprosy Pattern • Tuberculoid (good prognosis): HLA-DR3/DR4
• Lepromatous (poor prognosis): HLA-DQ1
Lupus Variants • SLE: HLA-DR2/DR3
• SCLE: HLA-B8/DR3
• Drug-induced (hydralazine): HLA-DR4
Key Mnemonics • B8 = "Bite" (Oral) → Oral LP = HLA-B8
• B27 = "Bone" → Psoriatic Arthritis
• DQ2 = "Dough" → DH (gluten)
