1️⃣ Etiology & Pathogenesis

ABCA12 mutation - Autosomal recessive inheritance
Functions in lipid transport to keratinocytes via lamellar granules
Loss of function → compromised skin lipid layer

2️⃣ Clinical Presentation

Classic presentation at birth: "armor-plated" appearance with thick scales

Skin Findings

  • Thick, hyperkeratotic scale encasing body
  • Large adherent plates
  • Deep fissures throughout body
  • Restriction of movement

Facial Features

  • Pronounced eclabium
  • Severe ectropion
  • Misshapen/absent ears
  • Disfigured nose
  • Absent eyebrows/eyelashes

Extremities

  • Disfigured fingers and toes
  • Joint contractures
  • Limited movement

3️⃣ Diagnostic Approach

🔬

Prenatal Diagnosis

Fetal skin biopsy - Absent lamellar bodies on electron microscopy

Premature hyperkeratosis around hair follicles on H&E stain

🔍

Postnatal Histology

Extraordinarily thickened and compact orthokeratotic stratum corneum

Variable parakeratosis

Ichthyosis Scoring System (ISS) - used to measure global ichthyosis severity

4️⃣ Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Differentiating Features Clinical Pearls
Collodion Baby • Less severe thickening
• "Plastic wrap" appearance
• Membrane sheds within 2 weeks 		Common presentation of: • Lamellar ichthyosis • Congenital ichthyosiform erythroderma • May evolve into self-healing form
🎯 Key Point: Harlequin ichthyosis has a unique presentation that rarely mimics other conditions

5️⃣ Management & Prognosis

Immediate Care

  • Transfer to pediatric ICU
  • Humidified incubator
  • Temperature regulation
  • Electrolyte monitoring
  • Sepsis prevention

Dermatologic Care

  • Oral retinoids (isotretinoin/acitretin)
  • Emollients
  • Keratolytics
  • Wound care

Multidisciplinary Approach

  • Ophthalmology (ectropion)
  • Surgery (deformities)
  • Family counseling
  • Genetic counseling
⚠️ Poor Prognosis: Most infants die within weeks due to:
  • Respiratory failure
  • Sepsis
  • Hypothermia
  • Hypernatremic dehydration
  • Complications of prematurity
Advanced NICU care + oral retinoids have allowed survival beyond neonatal period
Survivors develop severe exfoliative erythroderma requiring lifelong care

Hover for answer: B - ABCA12 mutation causes defective lipid transport to keratinocytes

🎯 Board Exam High-Yield Points

  • ABCA12 mutation - autosomal recessive inheritance
  • Classic "armor-plated" appearance at birth with deep fissures
  • Ectropion, eclabium, and deformed ears are characteristic
  • Poor prognosis with most dying within weeks
  • Oral retinoids have improved survival in select cases
  • Differential: Collodion baby (less severe, sheds in 2 weeks)
